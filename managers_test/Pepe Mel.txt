'''José''' "'''Pepe'''" '''Mel Pérez''' (born 28 February 1963) is a Spanish former professional [[Association football|footballer]] who played as a [[Forward (association football)#Striker|striker]], currently a [[Manager (association football)|manager]].

He amassed [[Segunda División]] totals of 215 matches and 78 goals over eight seasons, for [[Real Madrid Castilla]], [[CD Castellón|Castellón]] and [[Real Betis|Betis]]. He also played in [[La Liga]] with the last of those clubs.

Mel took up coaching in 1999, going on to be in charge of several teams, mainly [[Rayo Vallecano]] and Betis, leading the latter to the second-tier championship in [[2010–11 Segunda División|2011]] and [[2014–15 Segunda División|2015]]. He also worked in England with [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] and Greece with [[OFI Crete F.C.|OFI]], being dismissed five times throughout his career.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.givemesport.com/football-soccer-managers-who-have-been-sacked-most-times/#:~:text=Claudio%20Ranieri%20has%20been%20sacked,have%20a%20history%20of%20sackings.|title=8 football managers who have been sacked the most times|publisher=Give Me Sport|first=Alex|last=Mitton|date=27 January 2024|access-date=22 October 2024}}</ref>

==Playing career==
===Castellón and Betis===
Born in [[Madrid]], Mel started his career with [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]], joining the club's youth system in 1974 at the age of 11 and playing alongside the likes of [[Emilio Butragueño]] and [[Míchel (footballer, born 1963)|Míchel]], although he never represented the first team, only competing at senior level with [[Real Madrid Castilla|the reserves]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1985/10/13/pagina-23/1139052/pdf.html|title=1–1: Balón de oxígeno para el Mallorca|trans-title=1–1: Oxygen balloon for Mallorca|newspaper=[[Mundo Deportivo]]|first=Jesús|last=Alcaide|language=es|date=13 October 1985|access-date=13 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1987/01/12/pagina-24/1157859/pdf.html|title=1–1: Balaídos dejó de ser inexpugnable|trans-title=1–1: Balaídos is no longer virgin territory|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|language=es|date=12 January 1987|access-date=13 January 2014}}</ref> In late 1987, after a very brief spell with [[CA Osasuna|Osasuna]], he moved to [[Segunda División]] with [[CD Castellón|Castellón]] where he played two years, helping the [[Valencian Community]] side to return to [[La Liga]] in his [[1988–89 Segunda División|second season]] after an absence of seven years.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1989/06/23/pagina-22/1442515/pdf.html|title=Renovó Mel y Vinyals se va al Oviedo|trans-title=Mel renewed and Vinyals goes to Oviedo|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|language=es|date=23 June 1989|access-date=13 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1989/06/26/pagina-23/1432589/pdf.html|title=El Castellón puso la "guinda"|trans-title=Castellón "iced the cake"|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|first=A.|last=Cabrera|language=es|date=26 June 1989|access-date=13 January 2014}}</ref>

Mel enjoyed his best years at [[Real Betis]], signing with the [[Andalusia]] team in 1989 and helping them to [[1989–90 Segunda División|achieve promotion in his debut campaign]] whilst winning the [[Pichichi Trophy]] with 22 goals, one better than in the previous season at Castellón.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1990/05/12/pagina-26/1219939/pdf.html|title=Mel: "El Español se equivocó"|trans-title=Mel: "Español made a mistake"|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|first=Rogelio|last=Román|language=es|date=12 May 1990|access-date=13 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1990/05/28/pagina-21/1227906/pdf.html|title=Racing: Se consumó la tragedia|trans-title=Racing: Tragedy consummated|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|language=es|date=28 May 1990|access-date=13 January 2014}}</ref> He scored 14 times [[1990–91 La Liga|the following year]], but suffered immediate relegation; his debut in the competition was on 2 September 1990, and he found the net in a 2–2 home draw against [[Sporting de Gijón]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1990/09/03/pagina-14/1220799/pdf.html|title=Betis y Sporting, de poder a poder|trans-title=Betis and Sporting, from strength to strength|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|first=Ricardo|last=Ríos|language=es|date=3 September 1990|access-date=13 January 2014}}</ref>

===Later career===
Mel played his remaining years in the [[Segunda División B]] and also in [[Championnat National|France]], with [[Granada CF|Granada]] – two years – [[Benidorm CF|Benidorm]] and [[Getafe CF|Getafe]], splitting his final season between [[Écija Balompié|Écija]] and [[Angers SCO|Angers]]. He retired at the age of 35, with a Spanish second division tally of 215 games and 78 goals.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.diariosur.es/malagacf/pepemel-perfil-entrenador-malaga-futbol-20220920000645-nt.html|title=Pepe Mel, una trayectoria consolidada: más de 600 partidos dirigidos entre Primera y Segunda|trans-title=Pepe Mel, solid career: in charge for more than 600 matches between ''Primera'' and ''Segunda''|newspaper=[[Diario Sur]]|first=Pedro Luis|last=Alonso|language=es|date=20 September 2022|access-date=31 March 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://colgadosporelfutbol.com/diez-goleadores-miticos-los-90-segunda-division-parte-ii/|title=Diez goleadores míticos de los 90 en Segunda División parte II|trans-title=Ten legendary scorers of the 90s in ''Segunda División'' part II|publisher=Colgados por el Fútbol|first=Rafa|last=Bevia|language=es|access-date=31 March 2023}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
===Early years and Rayo===
Mel started coaching one year after retiring, his first club being amateurs [[CD Coslada|Coslada]] in the [[Community of Madrid]]. In 2001, after one year at [[Real Murcia CF|Real Murcia]] in division two, he replaced [[Valencia CF|Valencia]]-bound [[Rafael Benítez]] at the helm of [[CD Tenerife|Tenerife]], having his first spell in the top flight as a coach<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elmundo.es/elmundodeporte/2002/02/18/liga/1014062785.html|title=El Tenerife despide a Mel y ficha a Javier Clemente|trans-title=Tenerife fire Mel and sign Javier Clemente|newspaper=[[El Mundo (Spain)|El Mundo]]|language=es|date=18 February 2002|access-date=7 September 2010}}</ref> and [[2001–02 La Liga|suffering relegation]].

In the following four seasons, Mel worked in the second level, being sacked early into [[2005–06 Segunda División|2005–06]] by [[Polideportivo Ejido]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elmundo.es/elmundodeporte/2005/11/14/futbol/1132003380.html|title=Pepe Mel, destituido como técnico del Poli Ejido|trans-title=Pepe Mel, dismissed as Poli Ejido's coach|newspaper=El Mundo|language=es|date=14 November 2005|access-date=7 September 2010}}</ref> Shortly after, he dropped down a tier and moved to [[Rayo Vallecano]], helping the Madrid club to [[2007–08 Segunda División B|return to the second division]] in his second year<ref name=RAYO>{{cite news|url=http://archivo.marca.com/edicion/marca/futbol/2a_division/rayo_vallecano/es/desarrollo/1135303.html|title=El Rayo vuelve a la División de Plata del fútbol español|trans-title=Rayo return to Silver Division of Spanish football|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=15 June 2008|access-date=12 May 2014}}</ref> and subsequently leading it to [[2008–09 Segunda División|fifth place]], with chances of promotion until the last month of competition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://as.com/futbol/2009/12/31/mas_futbol/1262244437_850215.html|title="Le pondría al Rayo un notable alto o un sobresaliente"|trans-title="I would give Rayo a B plus or an A"|newspaper=[[Diario AS]]|first=M.|last=Martín|language=es|date=31 December 2009|access-date=31 March 2023}}</ref>

===Betis===
[[File:Pepe mel 2.jpg|thumb|Mel training with Betis in December 2010]]
On 15 February 2010, after a string of poor results, Mel was dismissed by Rayo, being replaced by [[director of football]] [[Felipe Miñambres]] who helped the side to the [[2009–10 Segunda División|11th position]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://es.globedia.com/pepe-mel-destituido-entrenador-rayo-vallecano|title=Pepe Mel, destituido como entrenador del Rayo Vallecano|trans-title=Pepe Mel, dismissed as Rayo Vallecano coach|publisher=Globedia|language=es|date=15 February 2010|access-date=7 September 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200612231945/http://es.globedia.com/pepe-mel-destituido-entrenador-rayo-vallecano|archive-date=12 June 2020}}</ref> In the summer, he signed with former side Betis also in the second tier,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://as.com/futbol/2010/07/11/mas_futbol/1278799226_850215.html|title=Pepe Mel, nuevo entrenador del Betis|trans-title=Pepe Mel, new manager of Betis|newspaper=Diario AS|language=es|date=11 July 2010|access-date=31 March 2023}}</ref> leading the ''Verdiblancos'' back to the top flight in his [[2010–11 Segunda División|first year]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/2011/05/26/futbol/equipos/betis/1306432866.html|title=Pepe Mel renueva con el Betis tres temporadas más|trans-title=Pepe Mel renews with Betis for three more seasons|newspaper=Marca|first=Mercedes|last=Torrecillas|language=es|date=27 May 2011|access-date=12 May 2014}}</ref>

Mel coached Betis to the seventh position in the [[2012–13 La Liga|2012–13 campaign]], with the subsequent [[2013–14 UEFA Europa League|qualification]] for the [[UEFA Europa League]]. He was relieved of his duties on 2 December 2013, however, after falling to a 4–0 [[Seville derby|derby]] loss at [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] and conceding a [[Last-minute goal|last-minute]] 2–2 home equaliser to Rayo Vallecano.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://deportes.elpais.com/deportes/2013/12/02/actualidad/1386009069_685165.html|title=El Betis echa a su héroe|trans-title=Betis sack their hero|newspaper=[[El País]]|first=Rafael|last=Pineda|language=es|date=2 December 2013|access-date=2 December 2013}}</ref>

===West Bromwich Albion===
On 9 January 2014, Mel signed an 18-month contract with [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] of the [[Premier League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/25677329|title=West Brom appoint Pepe Mel as their new head coach|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=9 January 2014|access-date=9 January 2014}}</ref> His first match in charge came 11 days later, a 1–1 home draw against [[Everton F.C.|Everton]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/25708307|title=West Bromwich Albion 1–1 Everton|publisher=BBC Sport|date=20 January 2014|access-date=20 January 2014}}</ref>

Mel obtained his first win in [[Football in England|English football]] after seven games, with a 2–1 victory at [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] on 15 March 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/av/football/26597343|title=Swansea 1–2 West Brom: Pepe Mel dedicates first win to 'kind' Baggies fans|publisher=BBC Sport|date=15 March 2014|access-date=4 September 2021}}</ref> On 12 May, one day after the end of [[2013–14 Premier League|the season]] where he led the Baggies to the 17th place, he left by mutual consent with a record of three wins from 17 games.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27357004|title=Pepe Mel: West Bromwich Albion part company with manager|publisher=BBC Sport|date=12 May 2014|access-date=12 May 2014}}</ref>

===Return to Betis===
Mel returned to former club Betis in December 2014, with a contract lasting until the end of [[2014–15 Segunda División|the campaign]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.marca.com/2014/12/20/futbol/equipos/betis/1419079139.html|title=Mel, nuevo entrenador del Betis|trans-title=Mel, new Betis manager|newspaper=Marca|language=es|date=20 December 2014|access-date=22 December 2014}}</ref> He was the April 2015 [[Segunda División Manager of the Month]] after his team won all their games, scoring 11 goals and conceding two.<ref name=Month>{{cite web|url=http://www.laliga.es/en/news/pepe-mel-wins-aprils-liga-adelante-coach-of-the-month-award|title=Pepe Mel wins April's Liga Adelante coach of the month award|publisher=[[La Liga]]|date=15 May 2015|access-date=15 March 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160315141043/http://www.laliga.es/en/news/pepe-mel-wins-aprils-liga-adelante-coach-of-the-month-award|archive-date=15 March 2016}}</ref> On 24 May, after only one loss in his tenure, the league was won as champions and promotion achieved with a 3–0 win over [[AD Alcorcón|Alcorcón]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11827/9864193/real-betis-win-promotion-to-primera-division|title=Real Betis win promotion to Primera Division after Alcorcon victory|publisher=[[Sky Sports]]|date=25 May 2015|access-date=26 May 2015}}</ref>

Mel was fired on 11 January 2016, with his team in 15th place following a poor run of form and also strained relationship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35276986|title=Pepe Mel: Real Betis sack manager after defeat at Getafe|publisher=BBC Sport|date=11 January 2016|access-date=11 January 2016}}</ref>

===Deportivo===
On 28 February 2017, Mel was appointed at [[Deportivo de La Coruña]] until the end of [[2016–17 La Liga|the season]], replacing the sacked [[Gaizka Garitano]] at a team two points above the relegation zone in the top division and winless in the new year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://espanol.eurosport.com/futbol/oficial-pepe-mel-nuevo-entrenador-del-deportivo_sto6074934/story.shtml|title=Oficial: Pepe Mel, nuevo entrenador del Deportivo|trans-title=Official: Pepe Mel new Deportivo coach|publisher=[[Eurosport]]|language=es|date=27 February 2017|access-date=28 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/39121633|title=Pepe Mel: Deportivo La Coruna appoint new manager until end of season|publisher=BBC Sport|date=28 February 2017|access-date=4 March 2017}}</ref> He was himself dismissed on 24 October, due to poor results.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laliga.es/en/news/rc-deportivo-part-ways-with-pepe-mel|title=RC Deportivo part ways with Pepe Mel|publisher=La Liga|date=24 October 2017|access-date=24 October 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171025022445/http://www.laliga.es/en/news/rc-deportivo-part-ways-with-pepe-mel|archive-date=25 October 2017}}</ref>

===Las Palmas===
Mel was named manager of [[UD Las Palmas|Las Palmas]] on 4 March 2019, replacing [[Paco Herrera]] who had been fired earlier the same day, and signed a contract until the end of [[2018–19 Segunda División|the season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.udlaspalmas.es/en/news/new/pepe-mel-is-ud-las-palmas-new-manager|title=Pepe Mel is UD Las Palmas' new manager|publisher=UD Las Palmas|date=4 March 2019|access-date=14 March 2019}}</ref> On 23 January 2022, he was relieved of his duties.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.udlaspalmas.es/noticias/noticia/pepe-mel-no-continuara-en-el-banquillo-de-la-ud-las-palmas|title=Pepe Mel no continuará en el banquillo de la UD Las Palmas|trans-title=Pepe Mel will not continue in the bench of UD Las Palmas|publisher=UD Las Palmas|language=es|date=23 January 2022|access-date=23 January 2022}}</ref>

===Málaga===
On 21 September 2022, Mel joined [[Málaga CF|Málaga]] also in the second division.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malagacf.com/en/news/pepe-mel-named-as-new-malaga-cf-coach|title=Pepe Mel named as new Málaga CF coach|publisher=Málaga CF|date=21 September 2022|access-date=21 September 2022}}</ref> He was sacked the following 25 January 2023, with the club still in the relegation places.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malagacf.com/noticia/pepe-mel-no-proseguira-a-cargo-del-equipo|title=Pepe Mel no proseguirá a cargo del equipo|trans-title=Pepe Mel will not continue in charge of the team|publisher=Málaga CF|language=es|date=25 January 2023|access-date=25 January 2023}}</ref>

===OFI===
On 14 December 2023, Mel was appointed at [[OFI Crete F.C.|OFI]] in the [[Super League Greece]], signing a contract until the end of [[2023–24 Super League Greece|the campaign]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oficretefc.com/genika/proponitis-tou-ofi-o-pepe-mel|title=Προπονητής του ΟΦΗ ο Pepe Mel|trans-title=Pepe Mel is the new coach of OFI|publisher=OFI Crete F.C.|language=el|date=14 December 2023|access-date=15 December 2023}}</ref> In February 2024, however, following only one win in 12 matches and eight losses, the last being a 4–0 defeat to [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]], he left by mutual agreement.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.in.gr/2024/02/11/sports/football/episimo-telos-apo-ton-ofi-o-mel|title="Τέλος" από τον ΟΦΗ ο Μελ|trans-title="End of the road" for Mel at OFI|publisher=[[In.gr]]|language=el|date=11 February 2024|access-date=11 February 2024}}</ref>

===Almería===
On 13 March 2024, Mel took over [[UD Almería|Almería]] back in his home country's top tier, becoming their third coach of [[2023–24 Segunda División|the season]] after [[Vicente Moreno]] and Garitano.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.estadiodeportivo.com/futbol/ud-almeria/garitano-despedido-pepe-mel-confirmado-almeria-20240313-445664.html|title=Garitano, despedido y Pepe Mel, confirmado en el Almería|trans-title=Garitano, fired and Pepe Mel, confirmed at Almería|newspaper=[[Estadio Deportivo]]|first=Juande|last=Pérez|language=es|date=13 March 2024|access-date=15 March 2024}}</ref> He achieved the only three wins in the league, not being able to move from the 20th and last position (shared with Granada);<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ideal.es/udalmeria/liga-pepe-mel-almeria-salvado-20240508133703-nt.html|title=En la Liga de Pepe Mel el Almería estaría salvado|trans-title=In Pepe Mel's League Almería would be saved|newspaper=[[Ideal (newspaper)|Ideal]]|first=Juanjo|last=Aguilera|language=es|date=8 May 2024|access-date=24 May 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/almeria/20240525/1002253437/almeria.html|title=El Almería se despide con una última victoria de sabor agridulce|trans-title=Almería say goodbye with a last bittersweet win|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|first=Mario|last=Calderón|language=es|date=25 May 2024|access-date=25 May 2024}}</ref> he left on 28 May after his link expired.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.udalmeriasad.com/noticias/el-almeria-agradece-la-labor-de-pepe-mel|title=El Almería agradece la labor de Pepe Mel|trans-title=Almería appreciate the work of Pepe Mel|publisher=UD Almería|language=es|date=28 May 2024|access-date=6 June 2024}}</ref>

===Tenerife return===
On 16 September 2024, Mel returned to Tenerife on a one-year deal, replacing the dismissed [[Óscar Cano]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clubdeportivotenerife.es/noticias/pepe-mel-nuevo-entrenador-del-cd-tenerife|title=Pepe Mel, nuevo técnico del CD Tenerife|trans-title=Pepe Mel, new manager of CD Tenerife|publisher=CD Tenerife|language=es|date=16 September 2024|access-date=16 September 2024}}</ref> He was himself fired on 23 December after just three victories in 16 matches, in spite of having renewed his link the month before.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clubdeportivotenerife.es/noticias/pepe-mel-concluye-su-segunda-etapa-en-el-banquillo-del-cd-tenerife|title=Pepe Mel concluye su segunda etapa en el banquillo del CD Tenerife|trans-title=Pepe Mel ends his second spell on the bench of CD Tenerife|publisher=CD Tenerife|language=es|date=23 December 2024|access-date=23 December 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mundodeportivo.com/futbol/tenerife/20241223/1002376444/surrealista-patada-pepe-mel-renovado-despedido-49-dias.html|title=La surrealista 'patada' del CD Tenerife a Pepe Mel: de renovado a despedido en 49 días|trans-title=CD Tenerife's surrealistic 'kick' to Pepe Mel: from renewed to fired in 49 days|newspaper=Mundo Deportivo|first=Óscar|last=Zárate|language=es|date=23 December 2024|access-date=26 December 2024}}</ref>

==Outside football==
Mel is also a novelist, having published three books, ''Liar'', ''The Road to the Afterlife'' and ''The Test''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://sevilla.abc.es/deportes/alfinaldelapalmera/noticias/real-betis/pepe-mel-presenta-la-prueba-su-tercer-libro-en-sevilla-109482-1465236878.html|title=Pepe Mel presenta "La prueba", su tercer libro, en Sevilla|trans-title=Pepe Mel presents "The test", his third book, in Seville|newspaper=[[ABC (newspaper)|ABC]]|language=es|date=6 June 2016|access-date=7 March 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201043848/http://sevilla.abc.es/deportes/alfinaldelapalmera/noticias/real-betis/pepe-mel-presenta-la-prueba-su-tercer-libro-en-sevilla-109482-1465236878.html|archive-date=1 December 2017}}</ref>
