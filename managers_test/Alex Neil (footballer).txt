'''Alexander Francis Neil''' (born 9 June 1981)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leaguemanagers.com/managers/alex-neil/|title=League Managers Association – Alex Neil|access-date=30 December 2015}}</ref> is a Scottish professional [[Manager (association football)|football manager]] and former [[Association football|player]] who played as a [[midfielder]]. He is currently head coach of {{English football updater|Millwall}} club [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]].

Neil began his professional career at [[Airdrieonians F.C. (1878)|Airdrieonians]] in 2000, playing half a season in the [[Scottish Football League First Division]] before moving to [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]]. He played 142 games across all competitions in his four-year spell at Barnsley, and then signed for [[Mansfield Town F.C.|Mansfield Town]] on a free transfer. After a season at Mansfield, he returned to Scotland's First Division with [[Hamilton Academical F.C.|Hamilton Academical]], featuring in 246 matches across a decade and winning promotion to the [[Scottish Premier League]] in 2008.

He became Hamilton's [[player-manager]] in 2013 and led them to promotion to the [[Scottish Premiership]] in 2014. In January 2015 he was appointed manager of [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]], as the second youngest manager in [[the Football League]] at the time. Neil guided Norwich to promotion to the [[Premier League]], by winning the [[2015 Football League Championship play-off final]]. Norwich suffered relegation the following season, and in March 2017, Neil was sacked. He was appointed Preston North End's manager on 4 July 2017 and led the team to seventh place in the 2017–18 Championship. On 11 February 2022 Neil was appointed manager of [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]]. They were promoted from [[EFL League One|League One]] to the [[EFL Championship|Championship]], winning 2–0 in the [[2022 EFL League One play-off final|play-off final]]. He left later that year to become the manager of [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]].

==Playing career==
===Airdrieonians===
Born in [[Airdrie, North Lanarkshire|Airdrie]], North Lanarkshire,<ref name="Hugman"/> Neil began his career at [[Dunfermline Athletic F.C.|Dunfermline Athletic]], before joining [[Airdrieonians F.C. (1878)|Airdrieonians]] on a [[free transfer (association football)|free transfer]] in the summer of 1999.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://spfl.co.uk/news/accies-skipper-more-than-happy-to-get-his-boots-on-for-friday-night-football-89|title = Accies skipper more than happy to get his boots on for Friday Night Football &#124; SPFL}}</ref> He made his professional debut in the [[Scottish Football League First Division]] on 3 January 2000, as a 64th-minute [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] for [[Steve McCormick (footballer)|Steve McCormick]] in a 2–0 home loss to [[Falkirk F.C.|Falkirk]]. Five days later, on his first start, he scored in the eighth minute of a 3–1 loss at [[St Mirren F.C.|St Mirren]]. He finished his first season with 5 goals from 16 appearances, with the other four scored in three consecutive games in April, including two in a 3–0 home win over [[Greenock Morton F.C.|Greenock Morton]] on the 8th.<ref>{{cite news|title=Goal Hero Niall Praises Protest Fans; Airdrie ..3 Morton.0|url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-61389654.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160220163941/https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-61389654.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=20 February 2016|access-date=30 December 2015|newspaper=Sunday Mail|location=Glasgow|date=9 April 2000}}</ref>

===Barnsley===
After his first professional season, Neil was recommended to [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]] by his next-door neighbour [[Peter Hetherston]], a friend of their manager [[Dave Bassett]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=McGivern|first1=Mark|title=Mum of Norwich City manager Alex Neil on his amazing rise through £130m play-off into Premier League|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/news/scottish-news/mum-norwich-city-manager-alex-5789389|access-date=30 December 2015|newspaper=Daily Record|location=Glasgow|date=30 May 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.barnsleyfc.co.uk/99_00/news/000703a.htm |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20000919120937/http://www.barnsleyfc.co.uk/99_00/news/000703a.htm |archive-date=19 September 2000 |title=Double Signing Confirmed |website=BarnsleyFC.co.uk |publisher=Barnsley Football Club |date=3 July 2000 |access-date=7 May 2020}}</ref> He made his debut for the Tykes in the [[Football League First Division]] on 26 August 2000, replacing [[Lee Jones (footballer, born 1973)|Lee Jones]] for the final ten minutes of a 4–1 home win over [[West Bromwich Albion]]. He made 32 league appearances in his first season at [[Oakwell]], and was sent off on 28 April 2001 in a 1–0 home loss to [[Bolton Wanderers]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Barnsley 0–1 Bolton|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_1/1297808.stm|access-date=30 December 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=28 April 2001}}</ref>

On 15 September 2001, Neil scored his first goal in English football, concluding a 2–0 home win over [[Crewe Alexandra]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Barnsley 2–0 Crewe|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_1/1542000.stm|access-date=30 December 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=15 September 2001}}</ref> He netted again on 8 November in a 2–2 draw at [[Preston North End]],<ref>{{cite news|title=Preston 2–2 Barnsley|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_1/1643655.stm|access-date=30 December 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=8 November 2001}}</ref> in a campaign which ended in relegation. Neil spent the following two seasons in the Second Division, scoring the decisive goals in 3–2 wins at [[Rushden & Diamonds]] and [[Stockport County]] on 10 April and 8 May 2004.<ref>{{cite news|title=Rushden 2–3 Barnsley|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_2/3592777.stm|access-date=30 December 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=10 April 2004}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Stockport 2–3 Barnsley|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_2/3676105.stm|access-date=30 December 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=8 May 2004}}</ref>

===Mansfield Town===
On 20 July 2004, Neil joined [[Mansfield Town F.C.|Mansfield Town]] on a free transfer.<ref>{{cite news|title=Transfers – July 2004|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/3859763.stm|access-date=30 December 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=29 July 2004}}</ref> He made 41 appearances for them in [[Football League Two|League Two]] [[2004–05 Football League Two|that season]], and scored his first goal on 23 November in the first round of the [[FA Cup]], a consolation in a 4–1 loss to [[Colchester United]] at [[Layer Road]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Foulkes|first1=Dean|title=Colchester United 4–1 Mansfield Town|url=http://www.stagsnet.net/match/reports/viewreport.php?matchid=138|access-date=30 December 2015|publisher=Stagsnet|date=23 November 2004}}</ref> His only league goal for the Stags came on 5 March 2005, opening a 1–1 draw against [[Shrewsbury Town]] at [[Field Mill]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Football: Mansfield 1 Shrewsbury 1|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/world-news/football-mansfield-1-shrewsbury-1-1606226|access-date=30 December 2015|newspaper=The Sunday People|date=6 March 2005}}</ref>

[[Scottish Cup]], with Neil scoring a penalty kick in the fourth round replay at [[Alloa Athletic F.C.|Alloa Athletic]], a 3–0 win.<ref>{{cite news|title=Alloa Ath 0–3 Hamilton Neil played 30 league games in [[2007–08 Scottish First Division|2007–08]], as Hamilton won the division and promotion to the [[Scottish Premier League]].<ref name=div>{{cite news|title=Hamilton Accies 2–0 Clyde|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/scot_div_1/7353608.stm|access-date=30 December 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=19 April 2008}}</ref> He scored once that season, opening a 4–0 home win over [[Stirling Albion F.C.|Stirling Albion]] on 15 September 2007 with a 40-yard free kick.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hamilton Acc 4–0 Stirling Albion|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/scot_div_1/6990272.stm|access-date=30 December 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=15 September 2007}}</ref>

On 6 May 2011, Neil signed a new three-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/13308444 |title=Alex Neil agrees new three-year deal with Hamilton |publisher=BBC Sport |date=6 May 2011 |access-date=7 January 2015 }}</ref>

==Managerial career==

===Hamilton Academical===
After manager [[Billy Reid (footballer, born 1963)|Billy Reid]] vacated his managerial position at Hamilton in April 2013, Neil (who had already been involved in coaching the [[Hamilton Academical F.C. Reserves and Academy|club's youth teams]])<ref name=speirs>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/sport/13183205.Spiers_on_Saturday__understanding_the_Hamilton_Accies_success_story/|title=Spiers on Saturday: understanding the Hamilton Accies success story|work=[[The Herald (Scotland)|The Herald]]|author=Graham Speirs|date=4 October 2014|access-date=3 August 2018}}</ref><ref name=strategy>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/29515367|title=Long-term strategy buoys Hamilton as Celtic seek immediate answers|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=6 October 2014|access-date=10 August 2018}}</ref> was appointed [[player-manager]] on an interim basis. He was made manager on a permanent basis on 24 May 2013, at the age of 31.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/22663584|title=Alex Neil is named Hamilton player-manager|work=BBC Sport|date=24 May 2013|access-date=14 January 2016}}</ref> Assisted by [[Frankie McAvoy]], Neil led the club to promotion back to the top flight in his first full season, winning through the play-off system in May 2014.<ref name = "Norwich"/> Hamilton then enjoyed a good start to the [[2014–15 Scottish Premiership]], defeating [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] away for the first time in 76 years.<ref name = "Norwich"/><ref name=strategy/>

Neil had his contract terminated in March 2017, with the Canaries 8th in the Championship table.<ref>{{cite web|title=CLUB STATEMENT: ALEX NEIL LEAVES NORWICH CITY|url=http://www.canaries.co.uk/news/article/2016-17/norwich-city-alex-neil-3618768.aspx|access-date=10 March 2017}}</ref> They had won seven of their last 24 games in [[EFL Championship|The Championship]] and were nine points outside the top six play-off places.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39232229|title = Manager Neil sacked by Norwich|work = BBC Sport}}</ref> Neil admitted to regret over summer recruitment, which led to his sacking in March.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39630510|title=Norwich: Sacked Alex Neil admits to regret over summer recruitment|date=2017-04-18|work=BBC Sport|access-date=2017-06-28|language=en-GB}}</ref>

===Preston North End===
On 4 July 2017, Neil was appointed as manager of [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]], replacing [[Simon Grayson]], who left the club to take over as manager of [[Sunderland A.F.C|Sunderland]].<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40493654 | title=Alex Neil: Preston North End appoint ex-Norwich boss as manager | publisher=BBC Sport | date=4 July 2017 |access-date=5 August 2018}}</ref>

On 21 March 2021, Neil left Preston North End by mutual consent with the club 16th in the Championship table.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Preston part company with boss Neil|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/56477270|access-date=2021-03-21}}</ref>

===Sunderland===
On 11 February 2022, Neil was appointed as the new manager of [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]], on a 12-month rolling contract.<ref>{{Cite news|title= Alex Neil: Sunderland appoint ex-Norwich and Preston boss as new head coach|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60337931|access-date=2022-02-11}}</ref> On 21 May 2022, Neil guided Sunderland to promotion back to the Championship after a four-year absence after they beat [[Wycombe Wanderers F.C.|Wycombe Wanderers]] 2–0 in front of almost 50,000 Sunderland fans at Wembley in the [[2022 EFL League One play-off final]].<ref name=bbc2022may>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/61479674|title=Neil 'proud' as Sunderland start journey back|work=BBC Sport |date=21 May 2022|access-date=22 May 2022}}</ref>

===Stoke City===
On 28 August 2022, Neil was appointed as the new manager of [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]], after the dismissal of [[Michael O'Neill (footballer)|Michael O'Neill]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/62700286/ |title=Alex Neil: Sunderland boss leaves to become Stoke City's new manager|work=BBC Sport |date=28 August 2022 |access-date=28 August 2022}}</ref> His first game in charge ended in a 1–1 draw against [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Stoke City 1–1 Swansea City |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/62644194 |website=BBC Sport |date=31 August 2022 |access-date=27 September 2023}}</ref> Stoke ended the [[2022–23 Stoke City F.C. season|2022–23 season]] in 16th place.<ref>{{cite web |title=Watford 2–0 Stoke City |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/65445708 |website=BBC Sport |date=8 May 2023 |access-date=27 September 2023}}</ref> On 10 December 2023, Neil was sacked following a 1–0 loss to [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]]. Stoke were 20th in the table at the time of his departure.<ref>{{cite web |title=Alex Neil departs Potters|url=https://www.stokecityfc.com/news/2023/december/10/alex-neil-departs-potters/|website=Stoke City F.C.|date=10 December 2023 |access-date=10 December 2023}}</ref>

===Millwall===
On 30 December 2024, Neil was appointed head coach of Championship side [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]] on a long-term contract.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.millwallfc.co.uk/news/2024/december/30/millwall-confirm-alex-neil-as-new-head-coach/|title=Millwall confirm Alex Neil as new Head Coach|website=www.millwallfc.co.uk|date=30 December 2024|accessdate=30 December 2024}}</ref> He lost his first game in charge as Millwall were defeated 1–0 by [[Oxford United F.C.|Oxford United]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Millwall Millwall 0-1 Oxford United |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cy8y1v4r73pt#Report |access-date=1 January 2025 |work=BBC Sport |date=1 January 2025}}</ref> In May 2025, he signed a new contract with the club.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.millwallfc.co.uk/news/2025/june/04/alex-neil-signs-new-millwall-contract/|title=Alex Neil signs new Millwall contract|website=www.millwallfc.co.uk|date=4 June 2025|accessdate=4 June 2025}}</ref>
