The [[Premier League]] is the top tier of [[Professional sports#Association football|professional football]] in [[English football league system|England]].  The league was [[Foundation of the Premier League|formed in 1992]] as a replacement for the original [[Football League First Division]]. To date, there have been 307 [[Manager (association football)|managers]] in charge of the [[List of Premier League clubs|51 clubs]] which have played in the Premier League. 270 of those are permanent appointments, and 118 of them in a caretaker/interim capacity.

[[Arsène Wenger]] holds the record for most games managed in the Premier League with 828, all with [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]], which he managed between [[1996–97 Premier League|1996]] and [[2017–18 Premier League|2018]]. The most successful manager in the Premier League is [[Alex Ferguson]], who won 13 titles – more than twice as many as any other manager – with [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] between [[1992–93 FA Premier League|1993]] and [[2012–13 Premier League|2013]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Ronay |first=Barney |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2017/dec/29/arsene-wenger-arsenal-record-games-in-charge-alex-ferguson |title=Arsène Wenger overhauls Ferguson but legacy may prove a millstone |work=The Guardian |date=29 December 2017 |access-date=13 May 2018}}</ref> [[Sam Allardyce]] has managed the most teams in the Premier League, having taken charge of nine different clubs: [[Bolton Wanderers F.C.|Bolton Wanderers]], [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]], [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]], [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]], [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]], [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]], [[Everton F.C.|Everton]], [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] and [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]].

Many of the managers listed below served as caretaker managers in the period between a managerial departure and appointment. Several of these, however, went on to secure a permanent managerial post.

==Managers==

The list of managers includes everyone who has managed clubs while they were in the Premier League, whether in a permanent or temporary role. Caretaker managers are listed only when they managed the team for at least one match in that period.

The dates of appointment and departure may fall outside the club's [[List of Premier League clubs|period in the Premier League]], for example, [[Ron Atkinson]] was appointed as Aston Villa manager in 1991 (before the Premier League was formed in 1992) and remained in his position through the Premier League's establishment. Similarly, [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]]' first spell in the Premier League lasted for only one season ([[2003–04 FA Premier League|2003–04]]) but manager [[Dave Jones (footballer, born 1956)|Dave Jones]] remained in his position until November 2004.
