'''Nigel Worthington''' (born 4 November 1961) is a Northern Irish former professional [[Association football|footballer]] who was most recently the [[Manager (association football)|manager]] of [[York City F.C.|York City]].

He played a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] and a [[midfielder]], playing his club football for [[Ballymena United F.C.|Ballymena United]], [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]], [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]], [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]], [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] and [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]]. With Sheffield Wednesday he won the [[Football League Cup|League Cup]] in [[1991 Football League Cup Final|1991]]. As an international player, he was [[Cap (sport)|capped]] 66 times for [[Northern Ireland national football team|Northern Ireland]], and appeared for them at the [[1986 FIFA World Cup]].

Worthington began his managerial career as [[Player-coach#Player-managers in association football|player-manager]] of Blackpool in 1997 before he took over as manager of [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] in 2000. He guided Norwich into the [[Premier League]] in the [[2003–04 Norwich City F.C. season|2003–04 season]] as [[Football League First Division|First Division]] title winners. He left just over two years afterwards, having failed to keep them in the top-flight in 2005. He was briefly [[caretaker manager]] of [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] in 2007 prior to starting as manager of Northern Ireland, from which he stepped down in 2011.

==Club career==
Born in [[Ballymena]], [[County Antrim]], Worthington played as a [[left-back]] and a [[midfielder]] and started his career with hometown team [[Ballymena United F.C.|Ballymena United]] in 1979, where he was Ulster Football Writers Young Player of the Year in 1981.<ref name="Nigel Worthington NIFG">{{cite web |title=Nigel Worthington |url=http://nifootball.blogspot.co.uk/2007/04/nigel-worthington.html |publisher=NIFG |date=11 April 2007 |access-date=3 March 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Ex-Sky Blue gets top job |url=http://www.ballymenaunitedfc.com/news/news_2007_06_01_1.htm |publisher=Ballymena United F.C. |date=1 June 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071102231927/http://www.ballymenaunitedfc.com/news/news_2007_06_01_1.htm |archive-date=2 November 2007 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He moved into English football with [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]] on 1 July 1981 for a £100,000 fee.<ref name="Hugman1998-99"/>

Worthington signed for [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]] on 6 February 1984 for a £125,000 fee, and he stayed at the club for over 10 years.<ref name="Hugman1998-99"/> He earned [[Promotion and relegation|promotion]] into the [[Football League First Division|First Division]] with Wednesday in the 1983–84 season as [[Football League Second Division|Second Division]] runners-up.<ref name="Profile of Nigel Worthington">{{cite web |title=Profile of Nigel Worthington |url=http://www.irishfa.com/international/squad-profiles/profile/2239/senior/nigel-worthington-manager |publisher=Irish Football Association |access-date=3 March 2013}}</ref> He was a member of the team that won the [[Football League Cup|League Cup]] as well as a return into the First Division after finishing in third place in the Second Division in 1990–91.<ref name="Profile of Nigel Worthington"/><ref name="Rothmans1991-92"/> Two years later, Worthington and his teammates were losing finalists in the [[1993 FA Cup Final]].<ref name="Profile of Nigel Worthington"/>

Worthington left Wednesday to sign for [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] on 4 July 1994 for a £325,000 fee, spending two seasons at the club and one season at [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]], after signing on a [[Free transfer (association football)|free transfer]] on 18 July 1996.<ref name="Hugman1998-99"/>

==International career==
Worthington was [[Cap (sport)|capped]] 14 times by the [[Northern Ireland national under-18 football team|Northern Ireland national under-18 team]] before making his debut for the [[Northern Ireland national football team|senior national team]] in a 1–1 draw away to [[Wales national football team|Wales]] in the [[1983–84 British Home Championship]] on 22 May 1984.<ref name="Profile of Nigel Worthington"/> This was notable for being Northern Ireland's final match in the last ever [[British Home Championship]], the point gained being enough for Northern Ireland to win, and keep in perpetuity, the trophy.<ref>{{cite web |first1=Macario |last1=Reyes |first2=Neil |last2=Morrison |title=British Home Championship overview |url=https://www.rsssf.org/tablesb/bhc.html |publisher=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation |date=1 October 2009 |access-date=3 March 2013}}</ref> In Northern Ireland's subsequent successful [[1986 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|qualifying]] campaign for the [[1986 FIFA World Cup]], Worthington started one qualifying match and came on as a [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] in three more.<ref name="Nigel Worthington NIFG"/> In the Finals tournament itself, Worthington started two of Northern Ireland's three matches, against [[Algeria national football team|Algeria]] and [[Spain national football team|Spain]].<ref name="Nigel Worthington NIFG"/> He served as Northern Ireland [[Captain (association football)|captain]] from 1995 to 1996.<ref name="Nigel Worthington NIFG"/> Worthington's 66th and final cap was gained in Northern Ireland's 3–0 victory in a home [[Exhibition game#Association football|friendly]] against [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]] on 11 February 1997.<ref name="Profile of Nigel Worthington"/>

==Managerial career==
===Blackpool===
Worthington's managerial career started as the [[Player-coach#Player-managers in association football|player-manager]] at [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]] on 8 July 1997.<ref name="Profile of Nigel Worthington"/><ref name="Managers: Nigel Worthington"/> He soon retired from playing to take on sole managerial duties, but two and a half seasons in charge ended with his resignation on 23 December 1999 following a dip in form that saw the club lying in the Second Division relegation battle.<ref name="Managers: Nigel Worthington"/><ref name=Gillatt>{{cite book |first=Peter |last=Gillatt |title=Blackpool FC On This Day: History, Facts and Figures from Every Day of the Year |publisher=Pitch Publishing |year=2009 |isbn=978-1-905411-50-4}}</ref> "I have taken the club as far as I can," he stated.<ref name=Gillatt/>

===Norwich City===
After acting as assistant to [[Howard Wilkinson]] with the [[England national under-21 football team|England under-21]] team, Worthington moved to [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] on 27 June 2000 as assistant to [[Bryan Hamilton]].<ref name="Nigel Worthington Flown From the Nest">{{cite web |title=Nigel Worthington |url=http://www.ex-canaries.co.uk/players/worthington.htm |publisher=Flown From the Nest |access-date=3 March 2013}}</ref> He was given the role of assistant manager for the [[Northern Ireland national under-21 football team|Northern Ireland national under-21 team]] on 22 August, which he shared with his duties at Norwich.<ref name="Nigel Worthington Flown From the Nest"/> He was appointed [[caretaker manager]] at Norwich on 4 December after Hamilton resigned, and on 2 January 2001 was appointed as permanent manager.<ref>{{cite news |title=Worthington handed Norwich chance |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/n/norwich/1097980.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 January 2001 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref> He later saved the club from relegation into the Second Division.<ref name="Worthy cause for a party">{{cite news |first=Lewis| last=Wiltshire |title=Worthy cause for a party |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/n/norwich/3648827.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=4 May 2004 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref> The [[2001–02 Norwich City F.C. season|2001–02 season]], Worthington's first full season, saw Norwich reach the [[2002 Football League First Division play-off final|2002 First Division play-off final]], in which they lost to [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]] in a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Birmingham reach Premiership |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_1/1979806.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=12 May 2002 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref>

In his third full season in charge, [[2003–04 Norwich City F.C. season|2003–04]], Worthington took Norwich into the [[Premier League]] after winning the [[Football League First Division|First Division]] championship with a club record 94 points.<ref name="Worthy cause for a party"/><ref>{{cite news |first=David |last=Cuffley |title=A record-breaking start – but what of City's finish? |url=http://www.eveningnews24.co.uk/sport/other-sport/a_record_breaking_start_but_what_of_city_s_finish_1_487068 |newspaper=Norwich Evening News |date=20 January 2010 |access-date=18 September 2016 |archive-date=20 December 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191220052656/https://www.eveningnews24.co.uk/sport/other-sport/a-record-breaking-start-but-what-of-city-s-finish-1-487068 |url-status=dead }}</ref> With one match remaining in [[2004–05 Norwich City F.C. season|2004–05]], Norwich were one point above the relegation zone,<ref>{{cite web |title=Norwich City 2004–2005: Table: 11.05.2005 |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/norwich-city/2004-2005/table/2005-05-11 |publisher=Statto Organisation |access-date=18 September 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160918234418/http://www.statto.com/football/teams/norwich-city/2004-2005/table/2005-05-11 |archive-date=18 September 2016 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> but after a 6–0 defeat by [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] were relegated in 19th place.<ref>{{cite news |title=Fulham 6–0 Norwich |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_prem/4525103.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=15 May 2005 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Norwich City 2004–2005: Table: Final table |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/norwich-city/2004-2005/table |publisher=Statto Organisation |access-date=18 September 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160324064339/http://www.statto.com/football/teams/norwich-city/2004-2005/table |archive-date=24 March 2016 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Norwich were tipped to make an instant return into the Premier League in [[2005–06 Norwich City F.C. season|2005–06]] having retained the majority of their squad.<ref>{{Cite news |title=The Championship form guide |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_1/4671691.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=1 August 2005 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref> The team failed to mount the expected promotion challenge, which led to some supporters calling for Worthington to leave the club, with Norwich finishing the season ninth in the [[Football League Championship|Championship]] table.<ref>{{cite news |title=Supporters' meeting calls for change at the top |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/norfolk/content/articles/2006/03/03/sport_canary_fan_meeting_feature.shtml |publisher=BBC |publisher=Statto Organisation |access-date=3 March 2013 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110810044628/http://www.statto.com/football/teams/norwich-city/2005-2006/table ===Leicester City===
Worthington was appointed [[caretaker manager]] of relegation threatened Championship club [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] for the rest of [[2006–07 Leicester City F.C. season|2006–07]] on 11 April 2007.<ref name="Worthington takes Leicester job">{{cite news |title=Worthington takes Leicester job |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/l/leicester_city/6544431.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=11 April 2007 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref> Despite losing his first match to former club Norwich, he was able to steer Leicester to safety and stated his desire to become Leicester's permanent manager.<ref>{{cite news |title=Worthington awaits Foxes decision |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/l/leicester_city/6616817.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 May 2007 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref> He lost out on the position to [[Martin Allen]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Allen named new Leicester manager |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/l/leicester_city/6665547.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=25 May 2007 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref>

===Northern Ireland===
Worthington was appointed as manager of Northern Ireland on 1 June 2007, initially on a short-term contract until the end of the [[UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying]] campaign in November.<ref name="Worthington named N Ireland boss">{{cite news |title=Worthington named N Ireland boss |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/internationals/6708553.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=1 June 2007 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref> At the time of his appointment, Northern Ireland led [[UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying Group F|their qualification group]].<ref name="Worthington named N Ireland boss"/> Although Northern Ireland eventually finished third in their qualification group, the [[Irish Football Association]] gave Worthington a two-year contract in January 2008.<ref>{{cite news |title=IFA to name Worthington as boss |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/internationals/7175412.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=7 January 2008 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref> This was subsequently renewed in January 2010, lasting until the end of the [[UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web |title=Worthington agrees new contract |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/news/newsid=1157046.html |publisher=FIFA |date=14 January 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121110193113/http://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/news/newsid=1157046.html |archive-date=10 November 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref> However, after a poor UEFA Euro 2012 qualification campaign he stepped down on 11 October 2011 after the final qualifier against [[Italy national football team|Italy]].<ref name="Nigel Worthington to step down as N Ireland manager">{{cite news |title=Nigel Worthington to step down as N Ireland manager |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/15243945 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=10 October 2011 |access-date=18 September 2016}}</ref>

club's League Two status, finishing the season in 17th place.<ref>{{cite news |title=Dag and Red 0–1 York |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/22228800 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=27 April 2013 team that reached the [[2014 Football League play-offs#League Two|League Two play-offs in 2013–14]], in which York were beaten 1–0 on aggregate in the semi-final by [[Fleetwood Town F.C.|Fleetwood
