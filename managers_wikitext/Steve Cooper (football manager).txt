'''Steven Daniel Cooper''' (born 10 December 1979) is a Welsh professional [[Manager (association football)|football manager]] and former [[Association football|player]] who is the manager of [[Danish Superliga]] club [[Brøndby IF]].

Cooper began his coaching career at [[Wrexham A.F.C.|Wrexham]]'s academy while playing as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] in the [[Welsh football league system|Welsh football leagues]]. He moved to [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] in 2008 and was appointed manager of their [[Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy|academy]] in 2011. Cooper joined the England youth set-up in 2014, initially coaching the [[England national under-16 football team|U16s]], before winning the [[2017 FIFA U-17 World Cup]] with the [[England national under-17 football team|U17s]]. He became head coach of [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] in 2019 before managing [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] from 2021 to 2023. He then became the manager of [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]].

==Playing career==
In the late 1990s, Cooper joined [[Wrexham A.F.C.|Wrexham]] but did not make an appearance for the club.<ref name="Steve Cooper BBC 26/07/20">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53529822 |title=Steve Cooper: Swansea City hope coach with a big future can inspire play-off glory |website=BBC Sport |date=26 July 2020 |access-date=9 January 2021 |first=Gareth |last=Vincent}}</ref> He was signed by manager [[Brian Flynn (footballer)|Brian Flynn]], who suggested Cooper pursue a coaching career instead of playing professionally.<ref name="Steve Cooper BBC 26/07/20"/> Cooper later played for [[The New Saints F.C.|Total Network Solutions]] (now called The New Saints), [[Rhyl F.C.|Rhyl]], [[Bangor City F.C.|Bangor City]] and [[Porthmadog F.C.|Porthmadog]] in the Welsh football leagues.<ref>{{cite web|title=Steve Cooper|url=http://www.welsh-premier.com/player_detail.php?player_id=10370|publisher=www.welsh-premier.com|access-date=5 May 2016|archive-date=7 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191207210635/http://www.welsh-premier.com/player_detail.php?player_id=10370|url-status=dead}}</ref> He featured for Bangor City in the [[UEFA Europa League|UEFA Cup]] against [[FK Smederevo 1924|Sartid Smederevo]] in 2002.<ref>{{cite web|title=UEFA Europa League 2002/03 - History - Bangor-Smederevo – UEFA.com|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/match/69776--bangor-vs-smederevo/|publisher=UEFA|access-date=5 May 2016}}</ref>

==Managerial career==
===Early coaching===
While a player, Cooper studied for his coaching badges and began coaching at Wrexham's academy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.swanseacity.com/news/profile-steve-cooper |title=In Profile: Steve Cooper |publisher=Swansea City A.F.C. |date=14 June 2019 |access-date=17 August 2019}}</ref> At the age of 27, Cooper obtained his [[UEFA Pro Licence]], becoming one of the youngest coaches to achieve the qualification.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41753974 |title=England Under-17s: Six things you didn't know about World Cup winners |website=BBC Sport |date=27 October 2017 |access-date=17 May 2020}}</ref><ref name="U16From">{{cite web |url=https://www.thefa.com/news/2014/oct/13/steve-cooper-appointed-england-u16s-head-coach |title=Steve Cooper appointed as England U16s head coach |publisher=The Football Association |date=13 October 2014 |access-date=2 October 2021}}</ref> 

After several years coaching at Wrexham's academy, Cooper became the club's head of youth development.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailypost.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/former-wrexham-coach-steve-cooper-2693878|title=Former Wrexham coach Steve Cooper relishing Liverpool FC role |publisher=North Wales Post |date=3 June 2011 |access-date=19 May 2020}}</ref> On 3 September 2008, Cooper was appointed as a youth coach at Liverpool, initially taking charge of the under-12s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dailypost.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/steve-cooper-leaves-wrexham-liverpool-2814632|title=Steve Cooper leaves Wrexham for Liverpool FC Academy|publisher=North Wales Daily Post|date=3 September 2008|access-date=6 May 2016}}</ref> On 18 July 2011, he was named manager of Liverpool's [[Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy|academy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/first-team/99306-i-m-honoured-to-take-new-role|title='I'm honoured to take new role'|publisher=Liverpool F.C. |date=18 July 2011|access-date=6 May 2016}}</ref> Cooper coached the under-18s for the 2012–13 season, leading them to the semi-finals of the [[FA Youth Cup]], where they lost to [[Chelsea F.C. Under-23s and Academy|Chelsea]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/kop-kids-steve-cooper-pays-3571816 |title=Kop Kids: Steve Cooper pays tribute to Liverpool under-18s after FA Youth Cup exit |newspaper=Liverpool Echo |date=25 April 2013 |access-date=17 May 2020}}</ref> While at Liverpool, Cooper oversaw the development of such players as [[Raheem Sterling]], [[Trent Alexander-Arnold]] and [[Ben Woodburn]].<ref name="Steve Cooper BBC">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48541951 |title=Swansea City make Steve Cooper a leading contender for manager role |website=BBC Sport |date=6 June 2019 |access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref> In 2013, Cooper joined the [[The Football Association|FA]] as a youth coach educator and also taught on the [[Football Association of Wales|FA Wales]] [[UEFA A Licence|A Licence]] course.<ref name="Steve Cooper BBC"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/steve-cooper-liverpool-swansea-city-16409897 |title=The impressive Steve Cooper presentation, his footballing identity and the Liverpool moment that changed everything |website=WalesOnline |publisher=Media Wales |date=11 June 2019 |access-date=17 May 2020 |first=Tomos |last=Clarke}}</ref>

===England youth===
On 13 October 2014, Cooper was appointed as manager of the [[England national under-16 football team|England national under-16 team]].<ref name="U16From"/> The following year, he took charge of the [[England national under-17 football team|under-17]]s, coaching players such as [[Jadon Sancho]], [[Phil Foden]] and [[Callum Hudson-Odoi]].<ref name="Steve Cooper BBC"/> Cooper led his side to the final of the [[2017 UEFA European Under-17 Championship|2017 UEFA European U17 Championship]], where they lost to [[Spain national under-17 football team|Spain]] 4–1 on penalties following a 2–2 draw.<ref name="U17 European Championship">{{cite news|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2017/may/19/spain-v-england-u17-report-190517 |title= Young Lions beaten on penalties in UEFA Euro U17 Championship final |publisher=The Football Association |date= 19 May 2017 |access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref> Cooper's U17s then won the [[2017 FIFA U-17 World Cup|2017 FIFA U17 World Cup]] in October 2017, beating [[Brazil national under-17 football team|Brazil]] 3–1 in the semi-final and Spain 5–2 in the final.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41749649 |title=Under-17 World Cup semi-final: Brazil 1–3 England |website=BBC Sport |date=25 October 2017 |access-date=14 August 2019}}</ref><ref name="FA 2">{{cite web|title=England U17s Head Coach Steve Cooper Reflects on Winning the World Cup in India|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2017/oct/28/steve-cooper-world-cup-final-reaction-281017|publisher=The Football Association |access-date=27 March 2017|date=28 October 2017}}</ref>

The following year, Cooper's side reached the semi-finals of the [[2018 UEFA European Under-17 Championship|2018 UEFA European U17 Championship]], where they lost to [[Netherlands national under-17 football team|Netherlands]] on penalties.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2018/may/17/england-v-netherlands-u17-euro-170518 |title=England knocked out of U17 Euro semi-final by Netherlands after penalty shootout |publisher=The Football Association |date=17 May 2018 |access-date=14 August 2019}}</ref> They did not qualify for the quarter-finals of the [[2019 UEFA European Under-17 Championship|2019 UEFA European U17 Championship]], despite beating [[Sweden men's national under-17 football team|Sweden]] 3–1 in their final group game.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2019/may/09/england-u17s-sweden-report-090519 |title= Young Lions win final group game of U17 Euros, but miss out on a quarter final spot |publisher=The Football Association |date=9 May 2019 |access-date=14 August 2019}}</ref> During his time at England, Cooper oversaw a specialised coaching model which included in-[[Possession (sports)|possession]] and out-of-possession coaches.<ref name="FIFA 2017">{{cite web|url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/6846913c20995277/original/fjwpw6fm22y9jrkkarj8-pdf.pdf |title=FIFA Technical Report FIFA U-17 World Cup India 2017 |publisher=FIFA |access-date=14 September 2020}}</ref> He was also responsible for the coaching programme and curriculum for the under-15s.<ref name="FIFA 2017"/> On training young players, Cooper said, "Sometimes I will talk and tell the players my thoughts, but most of the time I facilitate... that's how players learn, as a modern player. The days are gone, for me, where everything is the coach telling the player, that's finished."<ref name="FIFA 2017"/>

===Nottingham Forest===
Cooper was appointed head coach at Championship club Nottingham Forest on 21 September 2021 on a two-year contract. At this point, Nottingham Forest were bottom of the division.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2021/september/Steve-Cooper-appointed-Head-Coach/ | title=Steve Cooper appointed head coach | date=21 September 2021 }}</ref> Cooper improved Forest's style of play by encouraging them to be confident with the ball and attack in greater numbers. He also improved the mentality of the players – fostering a greater sense of togetherness,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Taylor|first=Daniel|title=How Steve Cooper has transformed Nottingham Forest – now they must build on it|url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/3031316/2021/12/25/how-steve-cooper-has-transformed-nottingham-forest-now-they-must-build-on-it/|access-date=2021-12-26|website=The Athletic|date=26 December 2021 }}</ref> and instilling a 'big club mentality'.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Mitchelmore|first=Ian|date=2021-12-08|title=Cooper's startling transformation of Nottingham Forest as he returns to Swans|url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/steve-coopers-startling-transformation-nottingham-22392117|access-date=2021-12-26|website=WalesOnline |publisher=Media Wales}}</ref> On 29 May 2022, Cooper led [[Nottingham Forest]] to promotion to the [[Premier League]], after a 1–0 win over [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] in the [[2022 EFL Championship play-off Final|Championship play-off final]] at [[Wembley Stadium]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Nottingham Forest promoted to Premier League for first time since May 1999|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/soccer/nottingham-forest-promoted-to-premier-league-for-first-time-since-1999/ |access-date=2022-05-29 |website=Sportsnet.ca}}</ref>

On 7 October 2022, he signed a new contract with the club until 2025.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/63172328 |title=Steve Cooper: Nottingham Forest manager signs new deal until 2025 |website=BBC Sport |date=7 October 2022 }}</ref> On 5 April 2023, after a string of poor results and reports that Cooper was at risk of dismissal, club owner [[Evangelos Marinakis]] stated that he still had faith in Cooper, but added results and performances must "improve immediately".<ref>{{Cite news |last=sport |first=Guardian |date=2023-04-05 |title=Forest owner stands by Cooper but says results must improve |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/apr/05/nottingham-forest-stand-by-steve-cooper-results-must-improve-immediately-owner-marinakis |access-date=2023-04-05 |issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Forest secured their Premier League status on 20 May 2023, following a 1–0 home victory against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]].<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/65579920 | title=Forest win to seal safety and end Arsenal title hopes | work=BBC Sport }}</ref>

On 19 December 2023, Cooper was dismissed as manager of Nottingham Forest.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=19 December 2023 |title=Nottingham Forest confirm departure of head coach Steve Cooper |url=https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2023/december/19/Nottingham-Forest-confirm-departure-of-head-coach-Steve-Cooper/ |access-date=19 December 2023 |website= |publisher=Nottingham Forest Football Club}}</ref> A run of just one win in 13 matches had left the club in 17th place in the table. A day later, he was replaced by [[Nuno Espírito Santo]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Stone |first1=Simon |title=Nottingham Forest sack Steve Cooper after poor Premier League run |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/67766124 |access-date=14 February 2024}}</ref>

===Leicester City===
On 20 June 2024, Cooper was appointed as manager of newly promoted [[Premier League]] side [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on a three-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |title=Steve Cooper: Leicester City appoint former Nottingham Forest boss |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/ckvvwxj881yo |website=BBC Sport |access-date=20 June 2024 |date=20 June 2024}}</ref>

Following a winless run of five games, Cooper was sacked on 24 November, the day following a 1–2 home defeat against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] while being managed by [[Enzo Maresca]], the ex-manager of the Foxes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/c8dmndv2n6zo|title=Leicester City sack manager Cooper|work=BBC Sport|date=24 November 2024|access-date=24 November 2024}}</ref> Leicester were 16th in the league table, having only won two of the first twelve matches and being knocked out from the [[2024-25 EFL Cup|League Cup]] by [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Leicester City Parts Company With Steve Cooper |url=https://www.lcfc.com/news/4175174/leicester-city-parts-company-with-steve-cooper |website=LCFC {{pipe}} Official Website |publisher=Leicester City |access-date=25 November 2024 |date=25 November 2024}}</ref> It was later announced that [[Ruud van Nistelrooy]] would be appointed as his successor and become Leicester City's new manager.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-11-27 |title=Ruud van Nistelrooy: Leicester set to appoint Dutchman as head coach to replace Steve Cooper |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/cd0g98m8eyvo |access-date=2024-11-28 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref>

==Style of coaching==
{{Quote box |quote  = We want to play with purpose, dominate [[Possession (sports)|possession]], play forward, and get the ball back as quick as we can. |source =—Cooper on his preferred style of play, 25 October 2017<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.firstpost.com/sports/fifa-u-17-world-cup-2017-england-coach-steve-cooper-proud-and-satisfied-to-make-a-statement-4176145.html |title=FIFA U-17 World Cup 2017: England coach Steve Cooper 'proud and satisfied' to make a statement |publisher=First Post |date=25 October 2017 |access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref>|align = right |quoted = 1 | width = 33%}}

Cooper likes his teams to play with "good organisation and structure".<ref name="Steve Cooper Guardian">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/may/27/steve-cooper-england-u17-coach-optimism |title=England coach Steve Cooper sees hope in painfully familiar defeat to Spain |newspaper=The Guardian |date=27 May 2017 |access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref> He prefers his teams to control the game by being brave on the ball and confident when [[passing (association football)|passing]].<ref name="FA 2"/> Cooper coaches his teams to have tactical discipline; defensively, he prefers his players to regain possession through composure and aggression.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/under17/news/0252-0cda39b3c126-2fc47cfde7bd-1000--england-s-golden-age/ |title=England's golden age |publisher=UEFA |date=9 May 2018 |access-date=8 June 2019}}</ref>

Cooper cites former [[FC Barcelona B|Barcelona B]] coach [[José Segura]] as his main influence; the pair worked together at Liverpool's academy.<ref name="Steve Cooper Guardian"/> Tactically, Cooper likes to deploy a [[Formation (association football)#4–2–3–1|4–2–3–1]] with two [[Midfielder#Holding midfielder|holding midfielders]] and attacking [[Defender (association football)#Wing-back|wing-backs]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/what-learnt-far-steve-cooper-16768361 |title=What we have learnt so far about Steve Cooper at Swansea City – a tactical analysis |website=WalesOnline |publisher=Media Wales |date=16 August 2019 |access-date=1 December 2019}}</ref> He has also used a [[Formation (association football)#5–3–2|5–3–2]] or [[Formation (association football)#3–5–2|3–5–2]] formation to ensure greater defensive stability and more flexibility in attack.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/swansea-city-tactical-switch-worked-18616546 |title=The Swansea City tactical switch which has worked and why fans can look ahead with pride and optimism |website=WalesOnline |publisher=Media Wales |date=18 July 2020 |access-date=15 August 2020 |first=Guto |last=Llewelyn}}</ref>
