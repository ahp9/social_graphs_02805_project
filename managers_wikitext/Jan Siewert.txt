'''Jan Siewert''' (born 23 August 1982) is a German professional [[association football|football]] coach and former player who last managed [[SpVgg Greuther Fürth|Greuther Fürth]].

==Playing career==
Born in [[Mayen]], Siewert played as a [[defensive midfielder]] for [[TuS Mayen]].<ref name = "WF">{{cite web|url=https://www.worldfootball.net/person/pe57609/jan-siewert/|title=Profile|access-date=21 January 2019|publisher=World Football}}</ref> He retired at the age of 22 due to injury.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/51181591|title=What is life like for a manager after being sacked following Premier League failure?|date=23 January 2020|publisher=BBC}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
===Early career===
Siewert trained at the Hennes-Weisweiler-Akademie.<ref name = "Waz"/> After working as the assistant manager for the German under-17 and under-18 national teams,<ref name = "WF"/> Siewert signed a three-year contract to become manager of German fourth-tier side [[Rot-Weiss Essen]] in June 2015.<ref name = "Waz">{{cite web|url=https://www.waz.de/sport/fussball/rwe/jan-siewert-ist-der-neue-trainer-von-rot-weiss-essen-id10782120.html|title=Jan Siewert ist der neue Trainer von Rot-Weiss Essen|date=15 June 2015|access-date=21 January 2019|language=de|author=Ralf Wilhelm|publisher=Waz}}</ref> He was sacked by the club in April 2016 after the club entered the relegation zone.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.derwesten.de/sport/fussball/rwe/rot-weiss-essen-trennt-sich-von-trainer-jan-siewert-id11700454.html|title=Rot-Weiss Essen trennt sich von Trainer Jan Siewert|date=3 April 2016|access-date=21 January 2019|language=de|publisher=Der Westen|archive-date=1 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190501164952/https://www.derwesten.de/sport/fussball/rwe/rot-weiss-essen-trennt-sich-von-trainer-jan-siewert-id11700454.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> Siewert then became assistant manager of [[VfL Bochum]], manager of the VfL Bochum under-19 team, and manager of [[Borussia Dortmund II]].<ref name = "WF"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dfb.de/datencenter/personen/jan-siewert/trainer|title=Profile|access-date=22 January 2019|language=de|publisher=DFB}}</ref>

===Huddersfield Town===
In January 2019, he was linked with the vacant manager's position at [[Premier League]] club [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11740/11610948/jan-siewert-considered-by-huddersfield-to-replace-david-wagner|title=Jan Siewert considered by Huddersfield to replace David Wagner|date=18 January 2019|access-date=21 January 2019|publisher=Sky Sports}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46943786|title=Jan Siewert: Huddersfield expected to name new manager within 24 hours|date=21 January 2019|access-date=21 January 2019|author=Simon Stone|work=BBC Sport}}</ref> and later that month he was appointed to the role, signing a contract until the summer of 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46943388|title=Huddersfield Town appoint Jan Siewert from Borussia Dortmund as new manager|date=21 January 2019|access-date=21 January 2019|work=BBC Sport}}</ref> In doing so, he became the third Borussia Dortmund II manager in a row to leave that role to work in England; after [[David Wagner (soccer)|David Wagner]] (who he replaced at Huddersfield) and [[Daniel Farke]] (who became manager of [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46959225|title=Jan Siewert: How have three Borussia Dortmund coaches ended up in England?|date=22 January 2019|access-date=22 January 2019|author=Simon Stone|work=BBC Sport}}</ref> Siewert stated that he did not wish to be compared to Wagner, his predecessor at Huddersfield.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46956890|title=Jan Siewert: New Huddersfield Town boss does not want David Wagner comparison|date=22 January 2019|access-date=22 January 2019|work=BBC Sport}}</ref> Huddersfield were relegated to [[EFL Championship|The Championship]] in March 2019 with six games remaining, joining [[Ipswich Town F.C.|Ipswich Town]] and [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]] as being the only Premier League teams to have been relegated so early in the season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47761884|title=Huddersfield relegated: Terriers captain Christopher Schindler feeling 'empty'|date=30 March 2019|work=BBC Sport|access-date=27 August 2019}}</ref> Huddersfield gained one point from their first three league games in the 2019–20 season and were knocked out of the [[EFL Cup]] first round by [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]] and on 16 August 2019, after one win during 19 games in charge, Siewert was sacked.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/49319183|title=Jan Siewert: Huddersfield sack manager after 7 months in charge|work=BBC Sport|access-date=27 August 2019}}</ref> He later said that he "left his heart" at Huddersfield.<ref name=autogenerated1 /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.examinerlive.co.uk/sport/football/news/jan-siewert-i-left-heart-17616661|title=Jan Siewert: "I left my heart at Huddersfield Town"|first=Steven|last=Chicken|date=23 January 2020|website=huddersfieldexaminer}}</ref>

===Return to Germany===
On 1 July 2020 he became the Academy Manager at [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mainz05.de/en/news/jan-siewert-joins-as-new-head-of-academy-coaching/|title=Jan Siewert joins as new Head of Academy Coaching|website=mainz05.de|access-date=23 July 2020|archive-date=11 July 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200711171410/https://www.mainz05.de/en/news/jan-siewert-joins-as-new-head-of-academy-coaching/|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.examinerlive.co.uk/sport/football/news/new-job-former-huddersfield-town-18497909|title=New job for former Huddersfield Town head coach Jan Siewert|first=Steven|last=Chicken|date=26 June 2020|website=YorkshireLive}}</ref> On 28 December 2020, Siewert was appointed as interim manager of Mainz's first team in the [[Bundesliga]] after [[Jan-Moritz Lichte]] was dismissed.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.mainz05.de/news/neustart-mit-mainzer-fussball-dna/ |title=Neustart mit Mainzer Fußball-DNA |trans-title=New start with Mainz football DNA |work=[[1. FSV Mainz 05]] |date=28 December 2020 |access-date=3 January 2021 |language=de |archive-date=28 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201228193701/https://www.mainz05.de/news/neustart-mit-mainzer-fussball-dna/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> He managed one game before being replaced by [[Bo Svensson]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mainz05.de/en/news/svensson-new-05-head-coach/|title=Svensson is new Main 05 head coach|date=4 January 2021|website=mainz05.de|publisher=[[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]]|access-date=4 January 2021|archive-date=12 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210112223814/https://www.mainz05.de/en/news/svensson-new-05-head-coach/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On 2 November 2023, he became interim manager once again, replacing Svensson until further notice.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sport.sky.de/fussball/artikel/bo-svensson-tritt-als-trainer-von-mainz-05-zurueck/12999007/35311|title=Jan Siewert Nachfolger von Bo Svensson als Trainer von Mainz 05|website=Sky Sport}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.zeit.de/news/2023-11/02/trainer-siewert-uebernimmt-in-mainz-nach-svensson-ruecktritt|title=Fußball: Trainer Siewert übernimmt in Mainz nach Svensson-Rücktritt|date=2 November 2023|via=Die Zeit}}</ref> On 22 December 2023, Mainz announced that Siewert had been made permanent manager of the club.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.mainz05.de/news/jan-siewert-bleibt-bundesliga-trainer-von-mainz-05|title=Jan Siewert bleibt Bundesliga-Trainer von Mainz 05|date=22 December 2023 |website=mainz05.de |publisher=[[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]] |access-date=2 January 2024}}</ref> He was sacked on 12 February 2024 after just one win in twelve matches.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spiegel.de/sport/fussball/bundesliga-fsv-mainz-05-trennt-sich-von-trainer-jan-siewert-a-6711da34-386c-472b-9cf8-e5022cc82ebb|title=Bundesliga: FSV Mainz 05 trennt sich von Trainer Jan Siewert|date=12 February 2024|via=www.spiegel.de}}</ref>

In November 2024, he was named the new head coach of [[SpVgg Greuther Fürth|Greuther Fürth]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.sgf1903.de/news/profis/fuerstner-wird-sportdirektor|title=Fürstner wird Sportdirektor|publisher=sgf1903.de|date=12 November 2024 |accessdate=12 November 2024}}</ref> In May 2025, he was sacked.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.sgf1903.de/news/profis/spielvereinigung-stellt-siewert-frei|title=Spielvereinigung stellt Siewert frei|language=de|publisher=Greuther Fürth|date=5 May 2025|accessdate=6 May 2025}}</ref>

==Personal life==
As of January 2020 Siewert was married with a three-year-old son.<ref name=autogenerated1 /> At that time the family were still living near Huddersfield.<ref name=autogenerated1 />
