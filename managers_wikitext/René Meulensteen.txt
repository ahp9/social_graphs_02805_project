'''Reinhard Jozef Petrus "René" Meulensteen''' (born 25 March 1964) is a Dutch former [[association football|footballer]] and coach who is assistant coach of the [[Iraq national football team]].<ref>[https://www.news.com.au/sport/football/graham-arnold-thrilled-with-being-given-task-of-guiding-iraq-to-a-fifa-world-cup-for-the-first-time-since-1986/news-story/cc7f22577a15a2855b16690e578e5e3d], ''Caltex Socceroos'', 9 August 2018</ref> He spent the early parts of his career working in the Netherlands before taking up managerial roles with the Qatari youth team, as well as clubs [[Al-Gharafa Sports Club|Al-Ittihad]] and [[Al Sadd SC|Al-Sadd]]. He then spent 12 years, split either side of a year stint at [[Brøndby IF|Brøndby]], with [[Premier League]] side [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in various non-managerial capacities. Following his departure from the club in 2013, Meulensteen was in charge for short spells at [[FC Anzhi Makhachkala|Anzhi Makhachkala]], [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] and [[Maccabi Haifa F.C.|Maccabi Haifa]].

==Managerial career==
===Early career===
While still playing, Meulensteen became a youth coach at [[NEC Nijmegen|NEC]].<ref>{{in lang|da}} [http://www.brondby.com/article.asp?aid=38630&id=5 We need to f... get there], ''Brondby'', 8 September 2006</ref>

In June 2006, he signed a three-year contract with [[Denmark|Danish]] club [[Brøndby IF|Brøndby]] to become manager of the club, but resigned after six months.<ref>{{in lang|da}} [http://www.brondby.com/article.asp?aid=37599&id=145 René Meulensteen ny cheftræner i Brøndby IF], ''Brondby'', 6 June 2006</ref>

On 18 January 2007, Meulensteen rejoined Manchester United as technical skills development coach mainly to work with the first team. After the departure in July 2008 of their assistant manager, [[Carlos Queiroz]] to [[Portugal]] as their [[Portugal national football team|national team]] manager, Meulensteen took over as first team coach, with [[Mike Phelan]] being promoted to assistant manager. Both assumed their new roles on 13 August 2008.<ref>[http://www.manutd.com/en/News-And-Features/Football-News/2008/Sep/Boss-names-new-assistant.aspx Sir Alex names new assistant], ''Manchester United'', 3 September 2008</ref>

During his time as first-team coach, he helped Sir Alex Ferguson secure the [[Premier League]] title in [[2008–09 Premier League|2008–09]], [[2010–11 Premier League|2010–11]] and [[2012–13 Premier League|2012–13]]; the [[FA Community Shield|Community Shield]] in [[2008 FA Community Shield|2008]], [[2010 FA Community Shield|2010]], [[2011 FA Community Shield|2011]], the [[Football League Cup|League Cup]] in [[2009 Football League Cup Final|2008–09]] and [[2010 Football League Cup Final|2009–10]]; the UEFA Champions League in [[2008 UEFA Champions League Final|2007–08]] and [[FIFA Club World Cup]] in [[2008 FIFA Club World Cup|2008]].

Manchester United confirmed his departure from the club on 26 June 2013, after incoming manager [[David Moyes]] decided to bring in his own coaching team.<ref>{{cite news |title=Club confirms Rene exit |url=http://www.manutd.com/en/News-And-Features/Football-News/2013/Jun/manchester-united-confirms-departure-of-rene-meulensteen.aspx |work=ManUtd.com |publisher=Manchester United |date=26 June 2013 |access-date=2 December 2013 }}</ref>

On 1 July 2013, Meulensteen signed a contract with [[FC Anzhi Makhachkala|Anzhi Makhachkala]], joining [[Guus Hiddink]] as an assistant coach.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.fc-anji.ru/news/ru/club_news/ukreplenie_trenerskogo_shtaba/ |title=УКРЕПЛЕНИЕ ТРЕНЕРСКОГО ШТАБА, Официальный сайт ФК «Анжи» |access-date=5 July 2013 |archive-date=4 July 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130704034331/http://fc-anji.ru/news/ru/club_news/ukreplenie_trenerskogo_shtaba |url-status=dead }}</ref> Hiddink left Anzhi after only two games into the 2013–14 Russian Premier League season, allowing Meulensteen to step up to head coach.<ref>[http://en.rsport.ru/football/20130722/675443697.html Hiddink Resigns as Anzhi Coach]</ref> After 16 days in charge, Meulensteen was sacked and replaced by [[Gadzhi Gadzhiyev]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2013/aug/07/rene-meulensteen-anzhi-makhachkala|title=René Meulensteen shown door at Anzhi Makhachkala after 16 days as coach|date=7 August 2013|work=The Guardian|access-date=13 August 2013 }}</ref>

===Fulham===
Meulensteen returned to England in November 2013 to take up a coaching position under [[Martin Jol]] at [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Rene Meulensteen joins Fulham as new head coach |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/24929877 |work=BBC Sport |publisher=British Broadcasting Corporation |date=13 November 2013 |access-date=13 November 2013 }}</ref> Less than three weeks later, Jol was sacked by Fulham after five consecutive league defeats, with Meulensteen taking over as manager.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rene Meulensteen replaces sacked Martin Jol at Fulham |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/25170983 |work=BBC Sport |publisher=British Broadcasting Corporation |date=1 December 2013 |access-date=2 December 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131201181115/http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/25170983 |archive-date=1 December 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> His first match as Fulham manager was a 2–1 loss to [[Tottenham Hotspur]] on 4 December, where [[Ashkan Dejagah]] scored the only goal for Meulensteen's side. Fulham won their first match under Meulensteen days later on 8 December, beating [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] 2–0 with goals from [[Steve Sidwell]] and [[Dimitar Berbatov]]. On 14 February 2014, Fulham hired [[Felix Magath]] to replace Meulensteen as manager, sacking Meulensteen four days later.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rene Meulensteen, Ray Wilkins and Alan Curbishley exit Fulham |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/26251769 |work=BBC Sport |publisher=British Broadcasting Corporation |date=18 February 2014 |access-date=19 February 2014 }}</ref>

In November 2014, he was hired as a consultant by the [[Philadelphia Union]] from [[Major League Soccer]].<ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2014/11/14/philadelphia-union-add-former-manchester-united-assistant-coach-rene-meulens| url-status = dead| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20141117143158/http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/2014/11/14/philadelphia-union-add-former-manchester-united-assistant-coach-rene-meulens| archive-date = 2014-11-17| title = Philadelphia Union add former Manchester United assistant coach Rene Meulensteen as consultant {{!}} MLSsoccer.com}}</ref>

===Maccabi Haifa===
On 9 August 2016, Meulensteen was presented as head coach by [[Maccabi Haifa F.C.|Maccabi Haifa]]<ref>{{cite news |script-title=he:רנה מולנסטיין מונה למאמן מכבי חיפה|url=http://mhaifafc.com/newsPage.asp?newsID=26350|website=mhaifafc.com|publisher=Maccabi Haifa FC|language=he|date=9 August 2016|access-date=9 August 2016}}</ref> from [[Israeli Premier League]]. His appointment lasted just over six months, and then resigned as head coach on 13 February 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.football-oranje.com/meulensteen-leaves-maccabi-haifa/|title=Meulensteen leaves Maccabi Haifa|date=13 February 2017|publisher=Football Oranje|access-date=16 February 2017}}</ref>

===Kerala Blasters===
On 14 July 2017, Meulensteen was appointed as head coach of [[Indian Super League]] side [[Kerala Blasters FC|Kerala Blasters]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Abhijit |last=Bharali |title=ISL 2017: Kerala Blasters name Rene Meulensteen as head coach |url=https://www.sportskeeda.com/football/isl-2017-kerala-blasters-name-rene-meulensteen-as-head-coach |work=sportskeeda |date=14 July 2017 |access-date=14 July 2017 }}</ref> The club parted ways with Meulensteen after poor start of the season. He resigned as head coach on 2 January 2018.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/sport/football/indian-super-league/2018/jan/02/kerala-blasters-coach-rene-meulensteen-steps-down-1742747.html |title=Kerala Blasters coach Rene Meulensteen steps down |date=2 January 2018 |work=[[New Indian Express]] |access-date=10 February 2018}}</ref>

===Australia===
Meulensteen served as an assistant coach with [[Australia men's national soccer team|Australia]] from 2018 to 2024.<ref>{{cite web |title=Subway Socceroos coaching staff – update |url=https://www.socceroos.com.au/news/subway-socceroos-coaching-staff-update |website=Socceroos |publisher=Football Australia |access-date=27 September 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Rene Meulensteen and Luke Wilkshire leave Socceroos’ coaching staff |url=https://sport.optus.com.au/news/socceroos/os80800/socceroos-rene-meulensteen-luke-wilkshire-leave-coaching-staff |website=Optus Sport |access-date=27 September 2024}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Meulensteen's daughter [[Pien Meulensteen|Pien]] is a television presenter and reporter in the UK,<ref>{{Cite news|title=What's it like to interview Erik ten Hag?|url=https://www.manutd.com/en/news/detail/pien-meulensteen-on-being-the-first-person-to-interview-man-utd-boss-erik-ten-hag|website=Manutd.com|publisher=Manchester United|date=24 May 2022|accessdate=23 September 2022}}</ref> whilst his son [[Melle Meulensteen|Melle]] is also a footballer.
