'''Keith Derek Millen''' (born 26 September 1966) is an English [[association football|football]] [[manager (association football)|manager]] and former player who played as a [[centre back]]. He was most recently manager of [[AFC Croydon Athletic]].

Millen was appointed as [[Steve Coppell]]'s successor, after Coppell chose to resign from Bristol City and retire from football. Millen spent most of his professional career at [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]], where he reached the [[Associate Members' Cup]] final in 1985, and won the [[Football League Third Division]] in 1992. After leaving Brentford in 1994, he finished his playing career with [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] and [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]]. Following retirement, Millen remained at City as a member of the coaching staff. He was caretaker manager at [[Ashton Gate stadium|Ashton Gate]] following [[Gary Johnson (footballer, born 1955)|Gary Johnson]]'s departure towards the end of the 2009–10 season. He took charge of nine competitive matches, winning five, drawing three and losing once. He was caretaker manager at Crystal Palace for 4 matches in the 2013–14 season winning one drawing one and losing 2. He was again appointed caretaker manager at [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] on 14 August 2014. On 27 August 2014 [[Neil Warnock]] was appointed full-time [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and at that time Millen's role at the club was not certain. However, on 12 September 2014 it was reported that Warnock was happy for Millen to continue in the role of assistant manager at Crystal Palace.<ref name="RefA">{{cite web|url=http://www.croydonadvertiser.co.uk/Keith-Millen-Ben-Garner-impress-Crystal-Palace/story-22915785-detail/story.html|title=Keith Millen and Ben Garner impress Crystal Palace boss|work=Croydon Advertiser|access-date=19 October 2015|archive-date=23 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923211646/http://www.croydonadvertiser.co.uk/Keith-Millen-Ben-Garner-impress-Crystal-Palace/story-22915785-detail/story.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> Millen was re-appointed as caretaker manager on 27 December 2014 after Warnock was sacked following a poor run of results. On 2 January 2015, [[Alan Pardew]] was appointed to replace Warnock, and Millen reverted to assistant manager. On 9 January 2017, after [[Sam Allardyce]] was appointed as manager, Millen left Crystal Palace.

==Playing career==
===Brentford===
After spells as an apprentice at [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] and [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]], Millen spent time as a junior at [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]], before turning professional in 1983.<ref name=jones>{{cite book|title=The Watford Football Club Illustrated Who's Who|author=Trefor Jones|year=1996|isbn= 0-9527458-0-1|page=164}}</ref> Between then and 1994, he made 305 [[English Football League|League]] appearances for the club, scoring 17 goals. He helped Brentford reach the final of the [[Associate Members' Cup]] in 1985, where they were defeated 3–1 by [[Wigan Athletic]] at [[Wembley Stadium (1923)|Wembley Stadium]].<ref name=rsssf>{{cite web|url=https://www.rsssf.org/tablese/engassmembcuphist.html|title=The Football League Trophy|publisher=[[Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation]]|author=Jeremy Hicks|access-date=6 March 2010}}</ref>
 
He helped the club to the [[Football League Third Division]] title in 1991–92, and earned a [[testimonial match]] against [[Tottenham Hotspur]] in 1993 following ten years as a professional.<ref name=assist>{{cite web|url=http://www.brentfordfc.co.uk/page/LatestNews/0,,10421~531137,00.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120226184603/http://www.brentfordfc.co.uk/page/LatestNews/0%2C%2C10421~531137%2C00.html |archive-date=26 February 2012 |title=Millen takes second in command |publisher=[[Brentford F.C.]] |access-date=6 March 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===Watford===
Millen joined Watford along with defensive partner [[Colin Foster]] in March 1994, helping them escape relegation that season.<ref name=jones/> Although Watford were eventually relegated in 1996, Millen later played a part in the club achieving successive promotions under [[Graham Taylor (footballer)|Graham Taylor]] between 1997 and 1999. In total, Millen made 165 appearances for Watford, scoring 5 goals.

===Bristol City===
Millen joined [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] in November 1999. In the [[2000 Football League Trophy final]] at Wembley, Millen captained Bristol City in a 2–1 defeat to [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]]. He played his last competitive game in August 2002, a 2–0 home win over [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]] before retiring in 2003,<ref name=assist/> and later joining the club's coaching staff.<ref name="Haynes">{{Cite book|title = Timeless Bees: Brentford F.C. Who's Who 1920-2006|last = Haynes|first = Graham|publisher = Yore Publications|year = 2006|isbn = 978-0955294914|location =Harefield |page =109|first2 = Frank|last2 = Coumbe}}</ref>

==Coaching and managerial career==
===Bristol City===
Following his retirement from football, Millen became under-17 manager at Bristol City.<ref name=u17>{{cite web|url=http://www.bcfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10327~388204,00.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100412150305/http://www.bcfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0%2C%2C10327~388204%2C00.html |archive-date=12 April 2010 |title=PFA list revealed in full |publisher=[[Bristol City F.C.]] |date=17 November 2004 |access-date=6 March 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref> When [[Brian Tinnion]] was promoted to [[player-coach#Player-manager|player-manager]] in 2004, he appointed Millen as his assistant. Under Tinnion's management City won 22 of 61 matches,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.soccerbase.com/managers/manager.sd?manager_id=1909 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161224164231/http://www.soccerbase.com/managers/manager.sd?manager_id=1909 |archive-date=24 December 2016 |title=Brian Tinnion's managerial career |publisher=[[Soccerbase]] |access-date=24 October 2023 |url-status=live }}</ref> and Millen was appointed as caretaker when Tinnion left the club. However, he publicly ruled himself out of the running to become the permanent manager.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_city/4238088.stm|title=Millen rules out Bristol City job|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|access-date=6 March 2010|date=12 September 2005}}</ref> He was made [[caretaker manager]] of City when [[Gary Johnson]] left the club in March 2010, and became manager on a full-time basis on a three-year contract when Johnson's replacement, Steve Coppell, departed on 12 August 2010.<ref name="BBC-Coppel-Resign">{{cite news |url= https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_city/8908308.stm |title= Steve Coppell quits as Bristol City manager |publisher=BBC Sport |date=12 August 2010 |access-date=12 August 2010 }}</ref> Millen left the manager's position on 3 October 2011.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20120106202205/http://www.bcfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0%2C%2C10327~2470826%2C00.html Keith leaves Ashton Gate] Bristol City F.C., 3 October 2011 after the 5–0 defeat to Blackpool FC . Retrieved 3 October 2011.</ref>

===Crystal Palace===
After a brief spell as head of coaching and development at [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]]<ref name=bfc2848635>{{cite web|url=http://www.blackpoolfc.co.uk/news/article/seasiders-appoint-millen-in-development-role-235636.aspx |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180429024205/https://www.blackpoolfc.co.uk/news/2012/july/seasiders-appoint-millen-in-new-role |archive-date=29 April 2018 |title=Seasiders appoint Millen in development role |publisher=[[Blackpool F.C.]] |access-date=24 October 2023 |url-status=dead }}</ref> earlier in the year, Millen was appointed assistant manager at [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] in November 2012, following former [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]] manager [[Ian Holloway]]. On 23 October 2013, Millen was appointed as caretaker manager after Holloway left by mutual consent.<ref>{{cite news |title=Ian Holloway: Crystal Palace boss departs Premier League strugglers |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/24643421 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=23 October 2013 |access-date=23 October 2013}}</ref>

On 26 November 2013, newly appointed Crystal Palace manager [[Tony Pulis]] announced that Millen would be kept as backroom staff.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.croydonadvertiser.co.uk/Keith-Millen-staying-Crystal-Palace/story-20219557-detail/story.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923211701/http://www.croydonadvertiser.co.uk/Keith-Millen-staying-Crystal-Palace/story-20219557-detail/story.html |archive-date=23 September 2015 |title=Keith Millen will be staying at Crystal Palace |publisher=Croydon Advertiser |access-date=23 October 2023 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

After Tony Pulis' surprise departure from Crystal Palace on 14 August 2014 (only 2 days before the start of the new [[Premier League]] season) Millen was again appointed caretaker manager.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.croydonadvertiser.co.uk/Crystal-Palace-caretaker-boss-Keith-Millen/story-22738253-detail/story.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923211459/http://www.croydonadvertiser.co.uk/Crystal-Palace-caretaker-boss-Keith-Millen/story-22738253-detail/story.html |archive-date=23 September 2015 |title=Crystal Palace caretaker boss Keith Millen 'shocked' by Tony Pulis departure |publisher=Croydon Advertiser|access-date=24 October 2023 |url-status=dead }}</ref> However, when [[Neil Warnock]] was appointed permanent [[Manager (association football)|manager]] on 27 August 2014, Millen's future was not made clear.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.croydonadvertiser.co.uk/Keith-Millen-s-Crystal-Palace-future-uncertain/story-22842708-detail/story.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141113153810/http://www.croydonadvertiser.co.uk/Keith-Millen-s-Crystal-Palace-future-uncertain/story-22842708-detail/story.html |archive-date=13 November 2014 |title=Keith Millen's Crystal Palace future uncertain as Warnock targets new backroom staff|publisher=Croydon Advertiser |access-date=24 October 2023 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On 12 September 2014 it was reported that [[Neil Warnock]] was happy for Millen to continue as assistant manager at [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]].<ref name="RefA"/>

On 27 December 2014, Millen took over as caretaker manager of Crystal Palace for a third time following Warnock being sacked.<ref>{{cite news |title=Neil Warnock: Crystal Palace sack manager after Southampton defeat |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/30610309 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=27 December 2014 |access-date=27 December 2014}}</ref> When [[Alan Pardew]] was appointed to replace Warnock on 2 January 2015 Millen reverted to assistant manager.

On 9 January 2017, subsequent to the appointment of [[Sam Allardyce]] as manager of Crystal Palace, it was announced that Millen had left the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cpfc.co.uk/news/2017/january/club-statement/|title=Club Statement|website=cpfc.co.uk|date=9 January 2017|access-date=9 January 2017}}</ref> He then worked with the Tottenham Hotspur development squad while seeking a new first team role.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kilpatrick|first1=Dan|title=Spurs' Steve Hitchen responsible for Liverpool signing Luis Suarez – Comolli|url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/tottenham-hotspur/story/3057332/spurs-steve-hitchen-responsible-for-liverpool-signing-luis-suarez-says-comolli|publisher=ESPN|access-date=1 July 2017|date=10 February 2017}}</ref>

===Milton Keynes Dons===
On 23 January 2018, after a brief spell working on the coaching staff at [[Portsmouth F.C.|Portsmouth]], Millen joined [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Milton Keynes Dons F.C.|Milton Keynes Dons]] as [[Manager (association football)|assistant manager]].<ref name="BBC Sport">{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/42789224 |title=Dan Micciche: MK Dons appoint ex-England youth coach as new manager |publisher=BBC Sport |date=23 January 2018 |access-date=23 January 2018 }}</ref>

On 22 April 2018, following the mutual contract termination of first team manager [[Dan Micciche]], Millen was appointed caretaker manager of the club.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43856639 |title=Dan Micciche: MK Dons part company with manager |publisher=BBC Sport |date=22 April 2018 |access-date=22 April 2018 }}</ref>

===Halmstads BK===
[[Roy Hodgson|Roy Hogdson]] helped [[Halmstads BK]] recruit Millen as assistant manager to Igor Krulj and joined the side in January 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Officiellt: Keith Millen blir assisterande tränare i Halmstads BK |url=https://fotbolltransfers.com/nyheter/officiellt-keith-millen-blir-assisterande-tranare-i-halmstads-bk/102202 |access-date=2025-02-14 |website=fotbolltransfers.com}}</ref> After Igor Krulj was sacked, Millen continued as assistant manager under [[Magnus Haglund]] until leaving the club at the end of the 2019 season after the team failed to advance to the Swedish top division [[Allsvenskan]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tack för tiden i HBK, Keith Millen - Halmstads BK |url=https://www.hbk.se/nyheter/2019/november/tack-for-tiden-i-hbk-keith-millen |access-date=2025-02-14 |website=www.hbk.se}}</ref>

=== Örgryte IS ===
After leaving Halmstads BK, Millen was appointed manager of [[Superettan]] side [[Örgryte IS]], which he led for the 2020 season until being sacked in October the same year.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Öis-tränaren sparkad - ordföranden: "Behövde fundera efter den pinsamma förlusten mot Lödde" |url=https://www.fotbollskanalen.se/superettan/ois-tranaren-sparkad---ordforanden-behovde-fundera-efter-den-pinsamma-forluste/ |access-date=2025-02-14 |website=fotbollskanalen |language=sv}}</ref>

===Carlisle United===
On 26 October 2021, Millen was appointed manager of League Two side [[Carlisle United F.C.|Carlisle United]] on an 18-month contract following the sacking of [[Chris Beech (footballer, born 1974)|Chris Beech]] three weeks previously.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.carlisleunited.co.uk/news/2021/october/club-new-manager-appointed/|title=CLUB: New manager appointed|website=carlisleunited.co.uk|date=26 October 2021}}</ref> On 23 February 2022, with the club sitting in the relegation zone in 23rd position, Millen left the club by mutual consent.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.carlisleunited.co.uk/news/2022/february/club-statement-23-feb/|title=CLUB STATEMENT: 23 February 2022|website=carlisleunited.co.uk|date=23 February 2022|access-date=23 February 2022}}</ref>

===Gillingham===
On 7 April 2023, Millen was appointed head of academy coaching at League Two side [[Gillingham FC|Gillingham]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Cawdell |first=Luke |date=2023-04-07 |title=Gillingham appoint former Bristol City, Crystal Palace, MK Dons and Carlisle manager Keith Millen as head of academy coaching |url=https://www.kentonline.co.uk/medway/sport/gillingham-strengthen-their-coaching-department-285058/ |access-date=2023-04-23 |website=[[Kent Online]] |language=en}}</ref> On 5 October 2023, following the sacking of [[Neil Harris (footballer, born 1977)|Neil Harris]], Millen took charge of the first team on an interim basis.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-08-03 |title=Club Statement: Neil Harris |url=https://www.gillinghamfootballclub.com/news/2023/october/club-statement/ |access-date=2023-08-03 |website=www.gillinghamfootballclub.com |language=en}}</ref>

===AFC Croydon Athletic===
On 18 November 2024, Millen was appointed manager of [[Isthmian League]] South East Division side [[AFC Croydon Athletic]].<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.croydonathletic.com/croydon-athletic-appoint-former-crystal-palace-coach-keith-millen-as-new-manager/|title=CROYDON ATHLETIC APPOINT FORMER CRYSTAL PALACE COACH KEITH MILLEN AS NEW MANAGER|website=www.croydonathletic.com|date=18 November 2024|accessdate=14 December 2024}}</ref> He departed the club on 14 February 2025 after recording just three wins from his 14 games in charge.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.croydonathletic.com/croydon-athletic-announce-keith-millen-to-step-down/|title=Croydon Athletic Announce Keith Millen to Step Down|website=www.croydonathletic.com|date=13 February 2025|accessdate=16 February 2025}}</ref>
