'''Christopher Leroy Ramsey''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|size=100%|MBE}} (born 28 April 1962) is a former professional [[Association football|footballer]] who is [[The Football Association]]'s Professional Development Phase (PDP) Lead across [[England national football team|England teams]]. He played as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]], most often as a [[Defender (association football)#Full back|right back]], for [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]], [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]], [[Swindon Town F.C.|Swindon Town]], [[Southend United F.C.|Southend United]], [[Naxxar Lions F.C.|Naxxar Lions]] and [[Cocoa Expos]]. Ramsey coached for [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] working with their academy and was appointed as first team coach where he assisted [[Tim Sherwood]] and [[Les Ferdinand]]. He was placed in temporary charge of Queens Park Rangers, in February 2015 following the resignation of manager, [[Harry Redknapp]]. After being temporary coach of Queens Park Rangers and following their relegation to the [[Football League Championship|Championship]], Ramsey was named head coach on a permanent basis in May 2015. He is nicknamed "Rambo".<ref name="Rambo">{{cite web | url=http://www.swindon-town-fc.co.uk/Person.asp?PersonID=RAMSEYCH | title=Player Profile | publisher=www.swindon-town.fc.uk | accessdate=4 February 2015}}</ref>

He was appointed [[Member of the Order of the British Empire]] (MBE) in the [[2019 Birthday Honours]] for services to football and diversity in sport.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=62666|supp=y|page=B20|date=8 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-48543826|title=Honours for Olivia Colman and Bear Grylls|date=2019-06-07|access-date=2019-06-07|language=en-GB}}</ref>

The London Football Awards 2025 announced that Ramsey is set to be awarded the Outstanding Contribution to London Football Award.  

==Playing career==
He began his career as an apprentice at Bristol City before moving on to Brighton and Hove Albion.<ref name="Player Profile 1">{{cite web |url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player8/chrisramsey.html |title=Chris Ramsey |work=Post War English & Scottish Football League A–Z Players Database |publisher=Neil Brown |accessdate=9 February 2015}}</ref> He played for Brighton in the [[1983 FA Cup Final]]<ref name=Rambo/> against [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]]. With Brighton leading, Ramsey was injured by a bad tackle from [[Norman Whiteside]], and was still hobbling as [[Frank Stapleton]] got ahead of him to equalise for [[Manchester United F.C.|United]]. Moments later, Ramsey received further treatment for his injury and was promptly substituted. After a drawn final at [[Wembley Stadium (1923)|Wembley]], Ramsey missed the replay due to the injury and Brighton lost 4–0.<ref name="When Brighton Almost Won The Cup">{{cite news | url=http://footballspeak.com/post/2012/04/14/When-Brighton-Almost-Won-The-Cup.aspx | publisher=Football Speak | title=When Brighton Almost Won The Cup | date=14 April 2012 | accessdate=13 February 2015 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150214035814/http://footballspeak.com/post/2012/04/14/When-Brighton-Almost-Won-The-Cup.aspx | archive-date=14 February 2015 | url-status=dead }}</ref>

Ramsey went on loan to Swindon Town from Brighton on 30 August 1984, who were then under the management of Lou Macari. He signed on a permanent contract on 13 December 1984 and stayed at Swindon for three years until joining [[Southend United F.C.|Southend United]] on 14 August 1987.<ref name=Rambo/>
Whilst at Swindon he won a [[Football League Fourth Division|Fourth Division]] championship medal in the 85–86 Swindon team, playing 43 of the 46 league games.<ref name=Rambo/> He also played 25 games the following season when Swindon won back-to-back promotions by beating [[Gillingham F.C.|Gillingham]] in the [[1987 Football League Third Division play-off Final|Third Division play-off final]] replay at [[Selhurst Park]] on 29 May 1987.<ref>{{cite AV media | url=http://www.swindon-town-fc.co.uk/MediaPlayer/MediaPlayer.asp?ID=V8pPtH70LTI | title=Play-off Final Gillingham Vs Swindon | publisher=www.swindon-town-fc.co.uk}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
Ramsey was also a player-coach for the [[Naxxar Lions]] in the [[Maltese Premier League]], and in the United States was a player, assistant coach and coach for the [[USL PDL]] team [[Cocoa Expos]].

In 1998 Ramsey began working for [[the Football Association]] (FA), holding the position of Regional Director of Coaching. While working for the FA he served as the head coach of the [[England national under-20 football team]]. During his tenure as the U-20 coach, Ramsey guided the team to the [[1999 FIFA World Youth Championship]] in Nigeria. He was also the assistant coach of the [[England national under-16 football team]] at the [[2000 UEFA European Under-16 Football Championship]] in Israel. In addition to his coaching responsibilities while at the FA, Ramsey was a scout for the [[England national football team]] under [[Kevin Keegan]].<ref name="Chris Ramsey to assist John Peacock with England U17s">{{cite web|title=Chris Ramsey to assist John Peacock with England U17s|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/england/development/2014/aug/chris-ramsey-u17-assistant-manager-international-tournament-john-peacock-phil-neville|publisher=Thefa.com|accessdate=27 August 2014|date=27 August 2014}}</ref> After leaving the FA Ramsey was the assistant coach at [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] until November 2000. Ramsey holds a [[UEFA]] coaching licence and a FA Coach Education Diploma.

From 2001 to 2004 he managed American club [[Charleston Battery]], during which time the club won the 2003 [[USL A-League]] championship. After leaving Charleston in 2004, Ramsey worked as Head of Player Development with the [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C. Reserves and Academy]]. In 2008, he was listed amongst the 30 most influential black people in football by ''The Voice'', a newspaper for the black community.<ref name="The footballing Black List">{{cite news|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/7305569.stm | publisher=BBC Sport | title=The footballing Black List | date=24 March 2008 | access-date=10 January 2015}}</ref>

Ramsey left Tottenham Hotspur on 19 June 2014, following ten years of service to the club.<ref name="Ramsey leaves Spurs">{{cite news|url=http://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/club-announcement-190614/|title=The Club can announce that Les Ferdinand and Chris Ramsey have left the Club|date=19 June 2014|publisher=Tottenham Hotspur|accessdate=19 June 2014}}</ref>

In August 2014, he was appointed as assistant manager of the England U17 national team.<ref name="Chris Ramsey to assist John Peacock with England U17s"/>

===Queens Park Rangers===
On 1 November 2014, he was appointed Head of Player Development and Academy Manager at [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.qpr.co.uk/news/article/301014-qpr-les-ferdinand-chris-ramsey-academy-2052035.aspx | title=Les Ferdinand welcomes Chris Ramsey academy role. | publisher=Queens Park Rangers F.C. | date=30 October 2014 | accessdate=4 February 2015}}</ref> Following the resignation of manager Harry Redknapp on 3 February 2015, Ramsey was placed in temporary charge, first with [[Les Ferdinand]] and [[Kevin Bond (English footballer)|Kevin Bond]], then solely with Bond.<ref name="caretakers">{{cite web | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/31117098 | title=Harry Redknapp: QPR manager resigns because of knee operation | publisher=BBC Sport | access-date=4 February 2015}}</ref> Ramsey's first competitive match as QPR head coach was on 7 February, losing to a 92nd minute, added-time winner, a 1–0 defeat to [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] at [[Loftus Road]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/31071178 | publisher=BBC Sport | title=QPR 0–1 Southampton | date=7 February 2015 | access-date=14 February 2015}}</ref> His first [[Premier League]] win came three days later, on 10 February, a 0–2 victory against [[Sunderland F.C.|Sunderland]] at the [[Stadium of Light]], in QPR's first away victory and first away points of the season.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/31106341 | publisher=BBC Sport | title=Sunderland 0–2 QPR | date=10 February 2015 | access-date=14 February 2015}}</ref> On 12 February, Ramsey was given the job until the end of the 2014–15 season.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11711/9713690/chris-ramsey-confirmed-as-qpr-head-coach-until-end-of-season | publisher=Sky Sports | title=Chris Ramsey confirmed as QPR head coach until end of season | date=13 February 2015 | accessdate=13 February 2015}}</ref> On 10 May, QPR were relegated to the [[Football League Championship|Championship]], after a 6–0 away defeat to [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]].<ref name="qprrelegation201415">{{cite web | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32571671 | title=Manchester City 6–0 QPR | publisher=BBC Sport | access-date=10 May 2015}}</ref> He was appointed head coach on a permanent basis on 19 May, signing a three-year contract.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/32797812 |title=Chris Ramsey appointed permanent QPR head coach |publisher=BBC Sport |date=19 May 2015 |accessdate=20 May 2015}}</ref> On 4 November, he was sacked as QPR head coach, with the club in thirteenth place in the league.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/34728650|title=Chris Ramsey sacked as Queens Park Rangers head coach|publisher=BBC Sport|date=4 November 2015}}</ref> On 5 January 2016, Ramsey returned to QPR to become technical director.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35234938|title=Chris Ramsey: Former head coach returns to QPR in technical role|publisher=BBC Sport|date=5 January 2016}}</ref>

==The Football Association==
On 13 August 2025, it was confirmed that Ramsey had returned to The Football Association as Professional Development Phase (PDP) Lead across England's U17 to U20 squads.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.englandfootball.com/articles/2025/Aug/13/england-mens-development-team-coaching-line-ups-confirmed-20251308|title=England men's development team coaches confirmed for 2025-26 season|publisher=England Football|date=13 August 2025}}</ref>
