'''Christopher Hutchings''' (born 5 July 1957) is an English former [[association football|footballer]] and [[coach (sport)|manager]]. He played for a number of clubs, including [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] and played more than 100 games for [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]] and [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]]. He has managed in the [[Premier League]] with [[Bradford City A.F.C.|Bradford City]] and [[Wigan Athletic F.C.|Wigan Athletic]], while his most recent tenure was at [[Walsall F.C.|Walsall]]. He left [[Ipswich Town]] in November 2012 following [[Paul Jewell]]'s departure.<ref name=ips>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/20048196|title=Paul Jewell leaves as Ipswich Town manager|date=24 October 2012|access-date=2012-10-24|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref>

==Playing career==
As a player, Hutchings spent time at [[Harrow Borough F.C.|Harrow Borough]], [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]], [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]], [[Walsall F.C.|Walsall]] and [[Rotherham United F.C.|Rotherham United]]. He made a total of 468 senior league appearances.

Chris Hutchings was a part-time player (and former bricklayer) with Harrow Borough. 
Chelsea signed him in July 1980 for £5,000. He made his debut in October 1980 against Cardiff City, playing midfield, and scored the winning goal after just 15 mins. He converted to full-back, where his best assets were his aggressive approach and his speed.
Chelsea transferred him to Brighton and Hove Albion for £50,000 in November 1983.<ref>Chelsea Football Club The Full Statistical Story 1905–1986 by Scott Cheshire and Ron Hockings {{ISBN|0-9511640-0-7}}</ref>

==Management career==
===Bradford City===
His first coaching role came at Rotherham, where he was youth team coach. He moved to [[Bradford City A.F.C.|Bradford City]] as assistant manager under [[Chris Kamara]] and continued in the role under [[Paul Jewell]]. In his time at Bradford, the club was promoted from the [[Football League Second Division|Second Division]] to the [[Premier League]].

Hutchings took charge as Bradford's manager during the [[2000–01 in English football|2000–01]] season after Jewell moved to [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]].<ref name="Hutchings appointed new Bantams boss">{{cite news
| title = Hutchings appointed new Bantams boss

| publisher = BBC Sport

He took Bradford to the semi-finals of the [[UEFA Intertoto Cup|Intertoto Cup]] and won the club's first Premier League home game of the season with a 2–0 win over [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]]. However, he lasted a total of only 21 competitive games, and his win against Chelsea was the only one in 12 Premier League games, before being sacked on 6 November 2000.<ref name="Bradford sack Hutchings">{{cite news

| access-date = 2007-05-14}}</ref> Wigan made an impressive start to the season, going top for a short period in August. However, after six consecutive defeats Wigan sacked Hutchings on 5 November 2007 after the club dropped into the Premier League relegation zone after just 12 Premiership games – almost seven years to the day he was sacked at Bradford. His last game in charge was a 2–0 home defeat to Chelsea.<ref name="Wigan sack Hutchings">{{cite news

===Walsall===
On 20 January 2009, he was named manager of [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Walsall F.C.|Walsall]] with [[Martin O'Connor (footballer)|Martin O'Connor]] as his assistant taking over from [[Jimmy Mullen (footballer, born 1952)|Jimmy Mullen]], who had been sacked earlier in the month. ===Ipswich Town===
Hutchings was named as Jewell's assistant at [[Ipswich Town F.C.|Ipswich Town]] on 17 January 2011.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/i/ipswich_town/9364040.stm | publisher = BBC Sport| access-date=17 January 2011 | date = 17 January 2011 | title = Chris Hutchings joins Paul Jewell at Ipswich Town}}</ref> Hutchings was named Ipswich Town caretaker manager after Paul Jewell left the club in October 2012.<ref name=ips/>

===Subsequent clubs===
In July 2013, Hutchings was named as assistant manager to Lee Ebden at [[Northern Premier League Division One South]] side [[Market Drayton Town F.C.|Market Drayton Town]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.shropshirestar.com/sport/2013/07/08/chris-hutchings-joins-staff-at-market-drayton-town/|title=Chris Hutchings joins staff at Market Drayton Town|website=shropshirestar.com|accessdate=21 March 2021}}</ref> He left the club on 8 November 2013.

He became a coach at [[Shrewsbury Town F.C.|Shrewsbury Town]] in February 2014, leaving in March 2014 to join [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]], where he worked until February 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.barnsleychronicle.com/article/7035/wilson-sacked-by-barnsley|title=Wilson sacked by Barnsley|website=Barnsley Chronicle|accessdate=21 March 2021}}</ref>
