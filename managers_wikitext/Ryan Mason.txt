'''Ryan Glen Mason''' (born 13 June 1991) is an English professional [[Association football|football]] coach and former player who is the head coach of [[EFL Championship]] club [[West Bromwich Albion]]. 

A [[midfielder]], Mason progressed through the youth ranks at [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] and made his first team debut in 2008. He was loaned to [[Yeovil Town F.C.|Yeovil Town]], [[Doncaster Rovers F.C.|Doncaster Rovers]], [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]], [[FC Lorient|Lorient]] and [[Swindon Town F.C.|Swindon Town]], and played his first [[Premier League]] match for Tottenham in 2014. After establishing himself in the Tottenham first team squad, he made his full international debut for [[England national football team|England]] in March 2015. Mason moved to [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] in 2016 for a club record fee, where he suffered a fractured skull in January 2017 in a match against [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]]. After a prolonged treatment and on the advice of medical specialists, he retired from professional football in February 2018.

Mason was interim head coach of Tottenham in 2021 and 2023, becoming the youngest coach in Premier League history, at 29. He was appointed Head Coach of West Bromwich Albion in June 2025.

===Hull City and retirement===
[[File:Hull vs Arsenal Sep 2016.jpg|thumb|Mason (in orange and black, number 25), playing for Hull against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in 2016]]

Mason underwent a successful operation on the head injury,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/22/sports/soccer/ryan-mason-skull-fracture-hull-city.html |title=Ryan Mason of Hull City Is in 'Stable Condition' After Surgery on Fractured Skull |agency=Reuters |newspaper=The New York Times |date=22 January 2017}}</ref> and throughout the rest of 2017 went through a rehabilitation process in an attempt to return to playing. After the operation he had 14 metal plates in his skull, with 28 screws holding them in place. He also had 45 staples and a six-inch scar across his head.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.fourfourtwo.com/features/ryan-mason-i-have-14-metal-plates-my-skull-28-screws-holding-them-place-and-45-staples-and |title=Ryan Mason: "I have 14 metal plates in my skull, with 28 screws holding them in place, and 45 staples. Since his retirement through injury, Mason has called for [[the Football Association]] to ban children from heading regulation-weight footballs, and for players to be protected from the risks of concussion.<ref>{{cite news |title=Ryan Mason: Children should be banned from heading footballs |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47232257 |access-date=6 June 2025 |work=BBC Sport |date=13 February 2019}}</ref>

Mason received his first call up to the [[England national under-19 football team|England under-19 team]] for the match against [[Russia national under-19 football team|Russia]], but only made the stand-by list and did not take part in the match.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.ciderspace.co.uk/ASP/news/news.asp?NewsItemId=10300 |title=No room for Mason as England under 19s win |date=8 September 2009 |website=Ciderspace |archive-date=4 March 2022 |access-date=21 March 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220304073807/http://www.ciderspace.co.uk/ASP/news/news.asp?NewsItemId=10300 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was called up for [[2010 UEFA European Under-19 Championship qualification|European Championship qualification]] in Slovenia in October 2009.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.thefa.com/England/mens-u19s/News/2009/qualifying_squad_170909 |title=Qualifying squad named |date=17 September 2009 |publisher=The Football Association}}</ref> Mason played in two of the three matches and grabbed the second England goal in the 2‚Äì0 win over [[Slovakia national under-19 football team|Slovakia]] to help to ensure England's 100% record.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.thefa.com/England/mens-u19s/News/2009/Finland091009 |title=England 3‚Äì1 Finland |date=9 October 2009 |publisher=The Football Association}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.thefa.com/England/mens-u19s/News/2009/slovakia_141009 |title=England 2‚Äì0 Slovakia |date=14 October 2009 |publisher=The Football Association}}</ref> Mason achieved his third and fourth caps for the U19s in friendlies against [[Turkey national under-19 football team|Turkey]] and the [[Netherlands national under-19 football team|Netherlands]] respectively.

On 23 March 2015, Mason received his first call up to the senior [[England national football team|England]] team for a European qualifier against [[Lithuania national football team|Lithuania]] and a friendly against [[Italy national football team|Italy]], after [[Adam Lallana]] withdrew due to injury.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.thefa.com/news/england/2015/mar/england-squad-update-230315 |title=Ryan Mason gets England call as Lallana is ruled out |publisher=The Football Association |date=23 March 2015 |access-date=23 March 2015}}</ref> He made his debut in the latter match on the 31st, replacing [[Jordan Henderson]] for the last 16 minutes of a 1‚Äì1 draw at [[Juventus Stadium]], assisting [[Andros Townsend]]'s late equaliser.<ref>{{cite news |title=Italy 1‚Äì1 England |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32028730 |access-date=31 March 2015 |website=BBC Sport |date=31 March 2015}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
===Tottenham Hotspur===
Mason joined the coaching staff of Tottenham Hotspur in April 2018.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11675/11325415/ryan-mason-begins-coaching-at-former-club-tottenham |title=Ryan Mason begins coaching at former club Tottenham|first=David|last=Menon|date=10 April 2018|website=Sky Sports |access-date=13 February 2019}}</ref> In February 2019, he suggested that heading should be banned for children.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47232257|title=Ryan Mason: Children should be banned from heading footballs|date=13 February 2019|website=BBC Sport|access-date=14 February 2019}}</ref> He was made the official academy coach for the Under-19 [[UEFA Youth League]] side in July 2019,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/tottenham/tottenham-hand-ryan-mason-coaching-role-as-part-of-academy-reshuffle-a4184941.html |title=Tottenham hand Ryan Mason coaching role as part of academy reshuffle|first=Jack|last= Rosser|date=8 July 2019 |newspaper=Evening Standard |access-date=8 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48915198|title=Ryan Mason returns to Tottenham in youth coach role|date=8 July 2019|website=BBC Sport|access-date=9 July 2019}}</ref>
and then the head of player development (U-17 to U-23) in August 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2020/august/academy-coaching-update/ |title=Academy coaching update |date=13 August 2020 |publisher=Tottenham Hotspur F.C. |access-date=15 December 2020}}</ref>

On 20 April 2021, following the dismissal of [[Jos√© Mourinho]] as [[head coach]], Mason was named [[Caretaker manager|interim head coach]] of Tottenham Hotspur until the end of the season.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11675/12281025/ryan-mason-appointed-tottenham-interim-head-coach-following-jose-mourinho-sacking |title=Spurs appoint Mason as interim head coach|date=20 April 2021|website=Sky Sports |access-date=20 April 2021}}</ref> At the age of 29, he became the youngest manager of a Premier League team; the previous record holder [[Attilio Lombardo]] of [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] was 32.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Colman |first1=Jon |title=Tottenham Hotspur's Ryan Mason, 29, is six years OLDER than Ivor Broadis when the legend took charge at Carlisle United |url=https://www.newsandstar.co.uk/sport/19245849.tottenham-hotspurs-ryan-mason-29-six-years-older-ivor-broadis-legend-took-charge-carlisle-united/ |access-date=26 April 2021 |newspaper=News & Star |date=20 April 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.90min.com/posts/youngest-managers-in-premier-league-history |title= The Youngest Managers in Premier League History |first= Tom |last=Gott |website=90min|date= 20 April 2021 }}</ref> Mason won his first match in charge of Tottenham, who beat [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] at home 2‚Äì1 on 21 April.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/56383512 |title=Tottenham Hotspur 2‚Äì1 Southampton |website=BBC Sport |date=21 April 2021 }}</ref> Four days later, the club lost the [[2021 EFL Cup Final]] by a single goal to [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Herman |first1=Martyn |title=Tottenham 'four or five years' behind Man City, says Ryan Mason after Carabao Cup final defeat |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/tottenham-man-city-carabao-cup-mason-b1837445.html |access-date=26 April 2021 |newspaper=The Independent |date=26 April 2021}}</ref> He oversaw five more games in charge, winning three and losing two with Tottenham finishing seventh at the end of the season, after a 4‚Äì2 victory over [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] on the final day of the season. His side finished one point above rivals [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]], and secured a [[UEFA Europa Conference League]] spot.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.premierleague.com/match/59270|title=Leicester v Spurs, 2020/21 &#124; Premier League|website=www.premierleague.com}}</ref>

On 4 November 2021, following the appointment of [[Antonio Conte]] as head coach, Mason was promoted to the role of first team coach after impressing Conte during his handling of leading training all week.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1456307081452077061|user=SpursOfficial|title=Head Coach: Antonio Conte. First Team Coach: Ryan Mason.|date=4 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|number=1456311662072504331|user=Daily_Hotspur|title=üö® LATEST: Antonio Conte made the decision to offer Ryan Mason a spot on his #thfc coaching staff having been impres‚Ä¶|date=4 November 2021}}</ref>

During Conte's health-related absences in February 2023, Mason worked closely alongside assistant head coach [[Cristian Stellini]] in the Tottenham dugout.<ref>{{cite news |last=Pitt-Brooke |first=Jack |title=Stellini reveals role in Spurs tactical switch |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/4172284/2023/02/09/tottenham-manchester-city-eric-dier/ |access-date=19 February 2023 |website=The Athletic }}</ref> On 26 March, after Conte left by mutual consent, Mason was made acting assistant head coach alongside Stellini as acting head coach until the end of the season.<ref>{{cite news |last=Pitt-Brooke |first=Jack |title=Conte leaves Spurs by mutual consent; Stellini, Mason take interim charge |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/4323309/2023/03/26/antonio-conte-leaves-tottenham/ |access-date=27 March 2023 |website=The Athletic }}</ref> On 24 April, Stellini was sacked after a 6‚Äì1 loss to [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]], and Mason took over as acting head coach.<ref>{{cite news |title=Cristian Stellini: Tottenham sack interim manager after Newcastle thrashing |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/65365163 |access-date=24 April 2023 |work=BBC Sport |date=24 April 2023}}</ref>

In his first game as acting head coach, on 27 April, his side drew 2‚Äì2 with [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]], including a comeback despite being two goals down.<ref>{{cite web |last=Mayo |first=Marc |date=27 April 2023 |title=Tottenham 2‚Äì2 Man Utd: Red Devils fluff lead as Mason conjures fightback on debut |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/football/tottenham-man-utd-premier-league-2023-mason-rashford-son-b1077428.html |access-date=30 April 2023 |website=Evening Standard }}</ref> He oversaw the Spurs' final six matches of the [[2022‚Äì23 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season|2022‚Äì23 season]], winning two, drawing one, and losing three games. Despite a 4‚Äì1 win away at [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] on matchday 38, Spurs missed out on European qualification for the first time since the [[2008‚Äì09 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season|2008‚Äì09 season]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Leeds relegated after heavy home loss to Spurs |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/65661818 |access-date=11 June 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Eccleshare |first=Charlie |title=What will having no European football next season really mean for Spurs? |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/4564228/2023/05/31/spurs-not-in-europe-money/ |access-date=11 June 2023 |website=The Athletic }}</ref>  On 27 June 2023, Mason was appointed as one of the assistant coaches to Tottenham's new head coach [[Ange Postecoglou]].<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2023/june/coaching-staff-update/| title=Coaching staff update| publisher=Tottenham Hotspur| date=27 June 2023| access-date=29 June 2023}}</ref>
