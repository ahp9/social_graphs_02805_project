'''Keith Gordon Downing''' (born 23 July 1965) is an English former [[Association football|footballer]] and football [[Manager (association football)|manager]] who is an assistant coach at {{English football updater|DerbyCou}} club [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]]. He made most of his appearances for [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]].

A former [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] [[Chelsea F.C. Reserves and Academy|youth team]] player, he moved from [[Mile Oak Rovers & Youth F.C.|Mile Oak Rovers]] into the [[English Football League]] with [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]] in 1984. Three years later, he joined Wolverhampton Wanderers. He spent six years with Wolves, winning the [[EFL Trophy|Football League Trophy]] in [[1988 Football League Trophy final|1988]] and successive [[Football League Fourth Division|Fourth Division]] and [[Football League Third Division|Third Division]] titles in 1987–88 and 1988–89. He joined [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]] in 1993 before moving on to [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] the following year. In 1995, he joined [[Hereford United F.C.|Hereford United]] via [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]], and retired in 1999.

Downing went on to become a [[Coach (sport)|coach]] and also spent September 2007 to November 2008 as [[Cheltenham Town F.C.|Cheltenham Town]] manager, and served [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] as [[caretaker manager]] for a brief spell in the 2013–14 [[Premier League]] season. He was appointed [[England national under-20 football team|England U20]] [[head coach]] in July 2015 and then took up the same role at the [[England national under-19 football team|England U19]]'s 13 months later, whom he led to the [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] title.

==Playing career==
===Early career===
Downing was a [[Chelsea F.C. Reserves and Academy|junior player]] at [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] but left [[Stamford Bridge (stadium)|Stamford Bridge]] at the end of the [[1982–83 Football League|1982–83]] season. He spent a season with [[Mile Oak Rovers & Youth F.C.|Mile Oak Rovers]]. He began his professional career in 1984 at [[Richie Barker (footballer, born 1939)|Richie Barker]]'s [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]], who went on to be [[Promotion and relegation|relegated]] out of the [[Football League Second Division|Second Division]] at the end of the [[1984–85 Football League|1984–85]] season. The "Magpies" finished eighth in the [[Football League Third Division|Third Division]] in the [[1985–86 Football League|1985–86]] season and then seventh in [[1986–87 Football League|1986–87]] under the stewardship of [[Jimmy Sirrel]].

===Wolverhampton Wanderers===
He left [[Meadow Lane]] and moved on to [[Graham Turner]]'s [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] in 1987. Wolves won [[Promotion and relegation|promotion]] as champions of the [[Football League Fourth Division|Fourth Division]] in [[1987–88 Football League|1987–88]], and Downing also played in the [[1988 Football League Trophy final|1988]] [[EFL Trophy|Football League Trophy]] final victory over [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]] at [[Wembley Stadium (1923)|Wembley Stadium]].<ref name="gokd">{{cite web |title=Keith Downing is the latest Old Gold Club inductee |url=https://www.wolves.co.uk/news/club/20190213-keith-downing-is-the-latest-old-gold-club-inductee/ |website=www.wolves.co.uk |access-date=14 June 2019 |language=en }}{{Dead link|date=April 2025 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Wolves won a second-successive promotion in the [[1988–89 Football League|1988–89]] season, winning the Third Division title by an eight-point margin.<ref name="gokd"/> The club made a push for promotion out of the Second Division in [[1989–90 Football League|1989–90]] but ended up in tenth place, seven points outside of the [[English Football League play-offs|play-off]] places. Downing appeared 35 times in both the [[1990–91 Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. season|1990–91]] and [[1991–92 Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. season|1991–92]] seasons, as Wolves finished in 12th and then 11th position. He played 37 times in the [[1992–93 Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. season|1992–93]] campaign before leaving [[Molineux Stadium|Molineux]] in the summer.

===Later career===
In July 1993, Downing moved to [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]]. However, he featured only twice for Blues in the [[1993–94 Birmingham City F.C. season|1993–94]] season and left [[St Andrew's (stadium)|St Andrew's]] in the summer. He remained in the Midlands and the [[Football League First Division|First Division]] for the [[1994–95 Stoke City F.C. season|1994–95]] season, playing 24 games for [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]]. He began the [[1995–96 Cardiff City F.C. season|1995–96]] season with [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]], before being reunited with Graham Turner at [[Hereford United F.C.|Hereford United]] in September 1995. The "Bulls" finished sixth in the Third Division in the [[1995–96 Football League|1995–96]] campaign, losing to [[Darlington F.C.|Darlington]] in the play-offs. He made 13 appearances in the [[1996–97 Football League|1996–97]] campaign as Hereford dropped out of the [[English Football League|Football League]] with a last-place finish. He became player-coach in 1998 and subsequently returned to Wolves as a [[Youth system|youth team]] [[Coach (sport)|coach]] in March 1999.<ref name="3 July 2004">{{cite news|title=Downing earns Robins post|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/c/cheltenham_town/3863639.stm|access-date=20 June 2015|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=3 July 2004}}</ref>

==Managerial career==
===Cheltenham Town===
Downing joined [[Cheltenham Town F.C.|Cheltenham Town]] as an assistant [[Manager (association football)|manager]] in July 2004, working alongside manager [[John Ward (footballer, born 1951)|John Ward]], who he had met through his time at [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] where Ward had been assistant manager.<ref name="3 July 2004"/> When Ward left the club to join [[Carlisle United F.C.|Carlisle United]] in October 2007, Downing stepped in as [[caretaker manager]], before being given the position on a full-time basis on 2 November. He endured a difficult start to his management career, but was aided by three [[Loan (sports)|loan]] signings from [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] in the January [[transfer window]] – [[Steve Brooker]], [[Alex Russell (footballer, born 1973)|Alex Russell]] and [[Richard Keogh]], as well as [[free agent]] defender [[Alan Wright]].<ref name="Jon"/> Cheltenham avoided relegation on the last day of the [[2007–08 Cheltenham Town F.C. season|2007–08]] season with a 2–1 victory over [[Doncaster Rovers F.C.|Doncaster Rovers]] at [[Whaddon Road]].<ref name="Jon">{{cite news |last1=Palmer |first1=Jon |title=Keith Downing on Cheltenham, West Brom and England |url=https://www.gloucestershirelive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/keith-downing-keeping-cheltenham-town-1620427 |access-date=14 June 2019 |work=gloucestershirelive |date=29 May 2018}}</ref> However, they struggled after striker [[Steven Gillespie]] was sold to [[Colchester United F.C.|Colchester United]] for £400,000.<ref name="Jon"/> Downing left the club by mutual consent on 13 September 2008 with the "Robins" bottom of [[EFL League One|League One]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/c/cheltenham_town/7614259.stm|title=Manager Downing leaves Cheltenham |date=13 September 2008|work=BBC Sport|access-date=27 February 2009}}</ref>

was a temporary appointment to help manager [[Dean Glover]], and both men left the club in May 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/p/port_vale/8031361.stm|title=Downing expecting to leave Vale|date=3 May 2009|work=BBC Sport|access-date=3 May 2009}}</ref> In July 2009, Downing was appointed the academy coach at [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/w/west_bromwich_albion/8144041.stm|title=Downing handed Baggies youth role|date=10 July 2009|work=BBC Sport|access-date=11 July 2009}}</ref> He served as joint-assistant [[head coach]] for the "Baggies" under [[Steve Clarke]] and in December 2013 temporarily took over first-team duties at the [[Premier League]] club after Clarke was sacked and before [[Pepe Mel]] was appointed as manager early the following month.<ref>{{cite news|title=Steve Clarke sacked as West Brom head coach|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/25385000|access-date=17 December 2013|publisher=BBC Sport|date=14 December 2013}}</ref> He was not a popular figure with fans at [[The Hawthorns]] due to his years of service at [[Black Country derby]] rivals Wolves.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Howell|first1=Bill|title=West Bromwich Albion: Under fire coaching duo Rob Kelly and Keith Downing placed in caretaker charge|url=http://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/west-bromwich-albion-under-fire-8359432|access-date=20 June 2015|work=Birmingham Mail|date=30 December 2014}}</ref> However, his [[UEFA Pro Licence]] and popularity with the players made him a serious candidate to replace Mel in the summer.<ref>{{cite news|title=Keith Downing backed for West Brom Irvine (footballer, born 1958)|Alan Irvine]] got the job however, and Downing left the club in January 2015 following a backroom reshuffle by new boss [[Tony Pulis]].<ref>{{cite news|title=West Brom: Rob Kelly, Keith Downing and Dean Kiely depart|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/30687215|access-date=20 June 2015|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=5 January 2015}}</ref>

===England youth===
Downing was appointed head coach of the [[England national under-20 football team|England U20]] team in July 2015, initially on an interim basis.<ref>{{cite news|title=Keith Downing appointed England under-20s boss|url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/west-bromwich-albion/2015/07/28/keith-downing-appointed-england-under-20s-boss/|access-date=22 July 2017|work=Express and Star|date=28 July 2015|language=en}}</ref> His appointed was made permanent in February 2016.<ref>{{cite news|title=Keith Downing named England U20 coach on permanent basis|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/11698/10162427/keith-downing-announced-england-u20-head-coach|access-date=22 July 2017|work=Sky Sports|date=10 February 2016|language=en}}</ref> He changed roles to become head coach of the [[England national under-19 football team|England U19]] team in August 2016.<ref>{{cite news|title=Keith Downing: England U19s 'have a definite focus'|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2016/aug/29/keith-downing-netherlands-preview-310816|access-date=22 July 2017|work=thefa.com|date=30 August 2016|language=en}}</ref> He led the under-19s to victory in the [[2017 UEFA European Under-19 Championship|2017]] [[UEFA European Under-19 Championship]] with a 2–1 victory over [[Portugal national under-19 football team|Portugal]].<ref name="uefa">{{cite news|title=Under-19 2017 - Portugal-England – UEFA.com|url=https://www.uefa.com/under19/match/2022852--portugal-vs-england/|access-date=22 July 2017|work=[[UEFA]]|date=15 July 2017|language=en}}</ref> He was a part of [[Gareth Southgate]]'s [[Scout (association football)|scout]]ing team at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]] in [[Russia]].<ref name="Jon"/> Downing left his post in August 2020 to join [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] as an assistant head coach to [[Dean Holden]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Association |first1=The Football |title=Young Lions coaches Paul Simpson and Keith Downing leave to join Bristol City |url=https://www.thefa.com/news/2020/aug/10/england-development-coaches-paul-simpson-and-keith-downing-leave-fa-for-bristol-city-100820 |access-date=10 August 2020 |work=www.thefa.com |date=10 August 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

===EFL coach===
Downing left Bristol City in October 2021.<ref>{{cite news |title=Downing leaves City |url=https://www.bcfc.co.uk/news/downing-leaves-city/ |access-date=31 October 2021 |work=Bristol City |date=27 October 2021 |language=en}}</ref> He was appointed as first-team head coach at [[Plymouth Argyle F.C.|Plymouth Argyle]] in December 2021, working under manager [[Steven Schumacher]], who clarified that "Keith has come in as my No 2, if you like".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Errington |first1=Chris |title=Argyle appoint Keith Downing as first team head coach |url=https://www.plymouthherald.co.uk/sport/football/plymouth-argyle-add-former-england-6394845 |access-date=23 December 2021 |work=PlymouthLive |date=23 December 2021 |language=en}}</ref> Downing left the club at the end of the [[2021–22 Plymouth Argyle F.C. season|2021–22]] season, much to director of football [[Neil Dewsnip]]'s disappointment.<ref>{{cite news |title=Downing departs |url=https://www.pafc.co.uk/news/downing-departs |access-date=15 May 2022 |work=Plymouth Argyle |date=14 May 2022 |language=en}}</ref> In July 2022, Downing returned to [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]] as one of two assistants to newly appointed head coach [[John Eustace]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/birmingham-city-eustace-taylor-downing-24396860 |title=Confirmed: John Eustace names his Birmingham City back-room staff |first=Brian |last=Dick |newspaper=Birmingham Mail |date=4 July 2022 |access-date=4 July 2022}}</ref> He left the club on 10 October 2023 after Eustace's dismissal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bcfc.com/matt-gardiner-and-keith-downing-leave-blues |title=Matt Gardiner and Keith Downing leave Blues |website=Birmingham City F.C. |date=10 October 2023 |access-date=10 October 2023}}</ref>

In February 2024, he reunited with Eustace again when appointed as an assistant coach alongside [[Matthew Gardiner (footballer)|Matt Gardiner]] at [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]].<ref name=asisi>{{cite news |title=Keith Downing joins Rovers staff |url=https://www.rovers.co.uk/news/2024/february/24/keith-downing-joins-rovers-staff/ |access-date=25 February 2024 |work=Blackburn Rovers FC |date=24 February 2024}}</ref> Eustace changed jobs to manage [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]] 12 months later and Downing followed him to [[Pride Park Stadium]] to become a first-team coach.<ref>{{cite news |title=COACHING STAFF: Matt Gardiner and Keith Downing in profile |url=https://www.dcfc.co.uk/news/2025/02/coaching-staff-matt-gardiner-and-keith-downing-in-profile |access-date=13 February 2025 |work=Derby County FC |date=13 February 2025 |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Downing married in 1993 and has two sons from that marriage.{{citation needed|date=October 2016}} He is a cousin of [[Judas Priest]] guitarist [[K. K. Downing]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/west-bromwich-albion-fc/2013/12/20/keith-downing-tipped-to-succeed-at-west-brom-by-music-star-cousin/ |title=Keith Downing tipped to succeed at West Brom by music star cousin |newspaper=Express & Star |location=Wolverhampton |date=20 December 2013 |access-date=24 October 2016}}</ref> His nephew, [[Paul Downing (footballer)|Paul Downing]], also played professional football.<ref name=asisi/> He has an interest in [[criminology]] and history, specifically the history of [[World War II]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/stoke/realmedia/2009/04/downing_pp_100409.ram|title=Keith Downing profile|date=April 2009|work=BBC Stoke & Staffordshire|access-date=17 April 2009}}</ref>
