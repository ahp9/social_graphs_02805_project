'''Régis Le Bris''' (born 6 December 1975) is a Breton professional [[Football coach (association football)|football manager]] and former [[football player|player]] who is currently the head coach of [[Premier League]] club [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |date=22 June 2024 |title=Sunderland appoint Le Bris as head coach |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/crgg059rn4vo |access-date=22 June 2024 |website=[[BBC Sport]]}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite news |last=Onyeagwara |first=Nnamdi |date=22 June 2024 |title=Sunderland appoint Le Bris as new manager |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/5568689/2024/06/22/sunderland-manager-appoint-regis-le-bris/ |access-date=23 June 2024 |work=[[The Athletic]] |publisher=[[The New York Times]] |language=en-US |issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

== Club career ==
Le Bris grew up in [[Plonéour-Lanvern]], a [[Communes of France|commune]] in the west of [[Brittany]]. He first began playing youth football for local side US Plonéour, before joining AS Ergué-Armel at the age of 12, and later [[Quimper Kerfeunteun FC|Quimper]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Le Mée |first=Dylan |date=6 November 2022 |title=FC Lorient. Régis Le Bris, une carrière d'entraîneur qui a pris racine dans le Finistère |url=https://www.ouest-france.fr/sport/football/fc-lorient/fc-lorient-regis-le-bris-une-carriere-d-entraineur-qui-a-pris-racine-dans-le-finistere-69b8e3d4-4898-11ed-9d2c-2b6045373a5c |access-date=30 September 2025 |work=[[Ouest-France]] |language=fr}}</ref> 

In 1991, he joined the youth ranks of [[Stade Rennais FC|Rennes]], where he would play primarily with the B team, but with whom he would also make his senior debut in 1994 and sign his first professional contract at the age of 21.<ref>{{Cite news |date=24 April 1997 |title=Stade rennais : Six premiers contrats professionnels |url=https://www.ouest-france.fr/premium/?page_id=MTk5NzA04E-DHYTQRsGAq0ZQ3UeLpw |url-access=subscription |work=[[Ouest-France]] |pages=22S02 |language=fr}}</ref> Le Bris made a total of 31 [[Ligue 1|Division 1]] appearances for Rennes, across five seasons with the first team.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Régis Le Bris |url=https://fbref.com/en/players/16ebe3fe/Regis-Le-Bris |access-date=30 September 2025 |website=fbref.com}}</ref>

In April 1999, despite still having two years left on his contract at Rennes, Le Bris opted to join nearby [[Ligue 2|Division 2]] side [[Stade Lavallois|Laval]], signing a three-year deal with the club and joining up with previous teammates [[Ulrich Le Pen]] and [[Mickaël Buzaré]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=27 April 1999 |title=Régis Le Bris au Stade lavallois |url=https://www.ouest-france.fr/premium/?page_id=MTk5OTA0r5GlSpC2SZG5yQpgo28evg |url-access=subscription |work=[[Ouest-France]] |language=fr}}</ref> Le Bris struggled for consistent game time at Laval, only making a total of 52 league appearances across three seasons with the club,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Régis Le Bris |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/FootballFicheJoueur2766.html |access-date=30 September 2025 |website=lequipe.fr |language=fr |type=Official website of [[L'Équipe]]}}</ref> though it was there that he would meet future friend and colleague [[Franck Haise]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=31 August 2022 |title=Haise-Le Bris, retrouvailles en pleine ascension |url=https://ligue1.com/en/Articles/ACTU/2022/08/30/haise-le-bris-retrouvailles-en-pleine-ascension |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.wikiwix.com/cache/index2.php?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.ligue1.fr%2FArticles%2FACTU%2F2022%2F08%2F30%2Fhaise-le-bris-retrouvailles-en-pleine-ascension#federation=archive.wikiwix.com&tab=url |archive-date=18 December 2022 |website=ligue1.com |language=fr |type=Official website of [[Ligue 1]]}}</ref>

Le Bris played his last professional football with [[Belgian Second Division|Belgian second division]] side [[K.S.K. Ronse|KSK Ronse]], retiring in 2003 to focus on youth coaching.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |date=20 June 2022 |title=Régis Le Bris, de la formation au banc du FC Lorient |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Football/Actualites/Regis-le-bris-de-la-formation-au-banc-du-fc-lorient/1339461 |access-date=23 June 2024 |website= |publisher=[[L'Équipe]] |language=fr-FR}}</ref>

== International career ==
Le Bris played in multiple different France youth representative sides,<ref name=":4" /> including representing the [[France national under-16 football team|France under-16 team]] at the 1991 [[Montaigu Tournament]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tournoi de Montaigu 1991 - Effectifs nations |url=https://www.mondial-football-montaigu.fr/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/COMPOS-NATIONS-2.jpg |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220919200049/https://mondial-football-montaigu.fr/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/COMPOS-NATIONS-2.jpg |archive-date=19 September 2022 |website=mondial-football-montaigu.fr |language=fr}}</ref>

== Managerial career ==

=== Rennes and Lorient ===
Le Bris became a [[Brevet d'État d'éducateur sportif|certified sports coach]] in 2004,<ref>{{Cite web |date=12 June 2004 |title=Site Officiel de la F.F.F. – La formation |url=http://www.fff.fr/formation/technique/183913.shtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20051018095224/http://www.fff.fr/formation/technique/183913.shtml |archive-date=18 October 2005 |access-date=23 June 2024 |language=fr-FR}}</ref> and started his managerial career as a youth coach for [[Wasquehal Football|Wasquehal]], before joining the youth sector of Rennes in the same role.<ref name=":1" /> During his stint with the club's under-19 squad, he led the team to the victory of the [[Championnat National U19]] in 2007, before winning the [[Coupe Gambardella]] the following year.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4">{{Cite web |last=Gouzerh |first=Paul-Marie |date=27 June 2022 |title=Le FC Lorient ouvre une nouvelle page de son histoire : Régis Le Bris nommé entraineur |url=https://www.fclorient.bzh/le-fc-lorient-ouvre-une-nouvelle-page-de-son-histoire-regis-le-bris-nomme-entraineur/ |access-date=23 June 2024 |website= |publisher=FC Lorient |language=fr-FR}}</ref>

In July 2012, after rejecting an offer to renew his contract with Rennes,<ref name=":1" /> Le Bris was appointed as the head of youth development at [[FC Lorient|Lorient]].<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4" /> He also served as the coach of the under-17 squad, with whom he would win the [[Championnat National U17]] with in 2015, before being appointed as the manager of the club's [[reserve team]] later the same year.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4" /> While serving in the role, he coached several footballers who would go on to play for the first team, including [[Mattéo Guendouzi]], [[Illan Meslier]], [[Alexis Claude-Maurice]] and [[Enzo Le Fée]].<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4" />

=== Sunderland ===
On 22 June 2024, it was announced that Le Bris would be appointed as the head coach of [[EFL Championship]] club [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] on 1 July, signing a three-year contract in the process.<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=22 June 2024 |title=Welcome to Sunderland: Régis Le Bris |url=https://www.safc.com/news/team-news/2024/june/regis-le-bris |access-date=23 June 2024 |website= |publisher=Sunderland Association Football Club}}</ref> He made a dream start to his time in charge of the club, having a 100% record across the first month of the season, scoring ten goals and conceding only one, being awarded the [[EFL Championship Manager of the Month]] for August.<ref name="Aug24 MOTM">{{cite web|url=https://www.efl.com/news/2024/september/13/august-s-sky-bet-championship-manager---player-of-the-month-winners/|title=August's Sky Bet Championship Manager & Player of the Month winners|website=efl.com|date=13 September 2024|access-date=13 September 2024}}</ref> Le Bris ultimately guided Sunderland back to the [[Premier League]] after an eight years absence, winning the [[2025 EFL Championship play-off final|play-off final]] 2–1 against [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] on 24 May 2025.

== Personal life ==
Le Bris is the brother of fellow former footballer [[Benoît Le Bris]],<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2">{{Cite web |date=22 February 2020 |title=Chez Théo Le Bris, le foot c'est de famille ! |url=https://www.ouest-france.fr/sport/football/fc-lorient/fc-lorient-chez-theo-le-bris-le-foot-c-est-de-famille-6747686 |access-date=23 June 2024 |website= |publisher=[[Ouest-France]] |language=fr}}</ref> as well as the uncle of [[Théo Le Bris]], whom he managed at [[FC Lorient|Lorient]].<ref name=":2" />

In 2006, he obtained a [[doctorate]] in sport [[physiology]] and [[biomechanics]] at [[Rennes 2 University]],<ref name=":1" /> having presented a study on [[Accelerometer#Medical applications|accelerometer]]-based running as his [[thesis]].<ref>{{Cite thesis |last=Le Bris |first=Régis |date=2006 |title=Etude biomécanique de la course à pied par une méthode accélérométrique : comparaisons méthodologiques et applications |url=https://theses.fr/2006REN20008 |journal=[[Rennes 2 University]] |language=fr-FR |via=theses.fr}}</ref> He graduated with a [[diplôme universitaire]] (DU) in the [[mental training]] of high-level athletes, from the same university in 2010.<ref name=":1" />
