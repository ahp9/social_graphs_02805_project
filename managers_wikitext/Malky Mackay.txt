'''Malcolm George Mackay''' (born 19 February 1972) is a Scottish professional [[association football|football]] coach and former player, who is the sporting director at [[Scottish Premiership]] club [[Hibernian F.C.|Hibernian]]. 

Mackay, who played as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]], began his playing career in Scottish football with [[Queen's Park F.C.|Queen's Park]] and [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]]. He joined English side [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] in 1998, remaining there for six years. Between 2004 and 2006, he achieved consecutive promotions to the [[Premier League]] with Norwich, [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] and finally [[Watford F.C.|Watford]]. Mackay won five [[cap (sport)|caps]] for [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]] towards the end of his playing career.

After retiring as a player in 2008, he became manager of Watford in June 2009. He was manager of [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] between June 2011 and December 2013, achieving promotion to the Premier League in 2013 but was dismissed after a dispute with club owner [[Vincent Tan]]. Mackay was appointed manager of [[Wigan Athletic F.C.|Wigan Athletic]] in November 2014, and was dismissed the following April. After a spell working for the [[Scottish Football Association]], he was appointed Ross County manager in May 2021.

==Playing career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Bellshill]],<ref name="Hugman2008-09 276"/> Mackay began his career in his native Scotland, coming through the youth ranks of [[Queen's Park F.C.|Queen's Park]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.qpfc.com/people/mmackay.htm|title=People: Malcolm Mackay|date=1 August 2015|website=QPFC.com – A Historical Queen's Park FC Website|access-date=12 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.qpfc.com/appearances/m/mackaymg.htm|title=Mackay, Malcolm George |website=QPFC.com – A Historical Queen's Park FC Website|access-date=12 February 2018}}</ref> before joining [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] in the summer of 1993. He made his Celtic debut on 13 May 1995 in a 1–0 away win against [[Dundee United F.C.|Dundee United]].

His first Celtic goal was scored on 27 April 1996 in a 4–2 away win against [[Partick Thistle F.C.|Partick Thistle]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fitbastats.com/celtic/player.php?playerid=2824|title=Celtic Player Malky Macaky details|work=FitbaStats |publisher=Bobby Sinnet & Thomas Jamieson |access-date=7 April 2015}}</ref> He made 46 appearances in five years with the [[Glasgow]] club, and in September 1998 moved to England, joining [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] for a fee of £350,000 after a one-game loan spell.<ref name=soccerbase>{{cite web|url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players/player.sd?player_id=9397|title=Malky Mackay|work=Soccerbase |publisher=Racing Post |access-date=5 February 2011}}</ref>

===Norwich City===
At the end of the [[2001–02 in English football|2001–02]] season Norwich fans voted Mackay in second place behind [[Gary Holt (footballer)|Gary Holt]] in the voting for [[Norwich City player of the year]]. In the [[2003–04 in English football|2003–04]] season, his sixth at Norwich, the club was promoted to the [[FA Premier League|Premier League]] after winning the then [[Football League Championship|Division One]], however he was released by Norwich at the end of the season. He was named in the [[Football League First Division]] Team of the Year in the 2003–04 in which they got promoted. Mackay scored [[2 (number)#In sports|a brace]] for Norwich in an [[East Anglian derby]] against Ipswich Town.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.watfordfc.com/page/NewsDetail/0,,10400~1369450,00.html|title=Three's the magic number for Mackay|publisher=Watford F.C.|access-date=5 February 2011|date=18 August 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120908093624/http://www.watfordfc.com/page/NewsDetail/0,,10400~1369450,00.html|archive-date=8 September 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===West Ham United===
Mackay was signed for [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] by manager [[Alan Pardew]], for £300,000, on 10 September 2004.<ref name="Malkysigns">{{cite web|url=http://www.whufc.com/articles/20040910/malky-signs_2236884_1137649|title=Malky signs|publisher=West Ham United F.C.|date=10 September 2004|access-date=20 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131220035207/http://www.whufc.com/articles/20040910/malky-signs_2236884_1137649|archive-date=20 December 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> He played 18 league games for the Hammers, although none after March, as West Ham reached the play-offs, beating [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]] in the final to reach the Premier League. Mackay was released before playing a top flight game.<ref name=soccerbase/>

===Watford===
Mackay was picked up on a free transfer by [[Aidy Boothroyd]] as part of his rebuilding of [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] and soon become one of the key figures in Boothroyd's side. Mackay was involved in some excellent defensive displays as well as scoring some vital goals, including one against [[Luton Town F.C. and Watford F.C. rivalry|fierce rivals]] [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] at [[Kenilworth Road]] in a 2–1 victory.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_1/4560238.stm|title=Luton 1–2 Watford|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=5 February 2011|date=2 January 2006}}</ref> At the end of the [[2005–06 in English football|2005–06 season]] Mackay achieved the feat of being promoted to the [[Premier League]] for the third successive season, as [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] defeated [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] 3–0 in the [[2006 Football League Championship play-off Final|play-off final]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_1/4771857.stm|title=Leeds 0–3 Watford|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=5 February 2011|date=21 May 2006}}</ref>

After having been released following his two previous promotion campaigns, Mackay was assured by Boothroyd that he would play a part in Watford's Premier League campaign, citing his experience as an important asset to the team. Although he was not a first-team regular, Mackay made 14 Premier League appearances in [[2006–07 Watford F.C. season|2006–07]]. In January 2007, Mackay took his first step into coaching by being promoted to first team coach, following the departure of [[Dave Hockaday]].{{citation needed|date=September 2020}}

In the [[2007–08 in English football|2007–08]] season Mackay's only first team appearance came during an [[FA Cup]] match against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolves]] in January 2008. He took over as [[caretaker manager]] at Watford on 4 November 2008 following the departure of [[Aidy Boothroyd]].<ref name="Boothroyd needed break — Simpson">{{cite news|title=Boothroyd needed break – Simpson|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/w/watford/7707938.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=4 November 2008|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref>

===International career===
During his sixth and final season at Norwich, at the age of 32, Mackay became the oldest man to make his debut for [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]] in 37 years.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hughes |first1=Dewi |title=Malky Mackay: The man behind Cardiff City's promotion |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/22187777 |access-date=9 September 2020 |work=BBC Sport |date=18 April 2013}}</ref>

It came in a 1–0 defeat to [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]]; Mackay later played in a 1–0 victory over [[Estonia national football team|Estonia]] and a 4–1 win against [[Trinidad and Tobago national football team|Trinidad & Tobago]]. In total, Mackay won five caps for Scotland.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scottishfa.co.uk/football_player_profile.cfm?page=823&playerID=13629&CFID=1540811&CFTOKEN=30413448|title=Malky Mackay- A squad|publisher=Scottish Football Association|access-date=16 October 2009}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
===Watford===
After [[Brendan Rodgers]] resigned in June 2009, Mackay took over as the [[manager (association football)|manager]] of Watford. Watford drew 1–1 on their league visit to Reading on 26 September 2009, and by their second meeting late in the season Rodgers had been dismissed by Reading after only a few months in charge.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_1/8263718.stm|title=Reading 1–1 Watford|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=5 February 2011|date=26 September 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/r/reading/8417263.stm|title=Boss Brendan Rodgers leaves Reading by mutual consent|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=5 February 2011|date=17 December 2009}}</ref> As for Mackay and Watford, [[2009–10 Watford F.C. season|2009–10]] was a difficult season where their Championship status was under threat for a long time, but by the end of April survival had been achieved against Reading, ending the season 14th place in the league.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_1/8633300.stm|title=Watford 3–0 Reading|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=5 February 2011|date=24 April 2010}}</ref>

===Cardiff City===

[[File:Gerrard Mackay.jpg|thumb|upright|Mackay (right) after [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] were beaten in the [[2012 Football League Cup Final|2012 League Cup Final]]]]

Mackay signed a three-year contract as [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] manager on 17 June 2011, with Watford receiving an undisclosed fee in compensation.<ref name="Cardiff City appoint Watford's Malky Mackay as manager">{{cite news|title=Cardiff City appoint Watford's Malky Mackay as manager|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/13680791|publisher=BBC Sport|date=17 June 2011|access-date=31 March 2017}}</ref> His reign began with a 1–0 away win over [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]]. The side were undefeated in September, resulting in Mackay being nominated for the September [[Football League Championship Manager of the Month|Championship Manager of the Month]] in his first month managing the Welsh club.<ref>{{cite web |title=Malky shortlisted for Award|url=http://www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10335~2473943,00.html|publisher=Cardiff City F.C. |date=6 October 2011|access-date=10 October 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111007080949/http://www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0%2C%2C10335~2473943%2C00.html|archive-date=7 October 2011}}</ref> Another unbeaten month for Cardiff resulted in Mackay winning the award in November.<ref>{{cite news|title=Cardiff City manager Malky Mackay wins manager of the month award|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/16108325|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=9 December 2011}}</ref>

He guided Cardiff to their first ever [[2012 Football League Cup Final|League Cup]] final, following wins over [[Oxford United F.C.|Oxford United]], [[Huddersfield Town F.C|Huddersfield Town]], [[Leicester City F.C|Leicester City]], [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]], [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]] and [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] in earlier rounds.<ref>{{cite news|title=Cardiff 1&nbsp;– 0 Burnley|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/league_cup/15346240.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=25 October 2011|access-date=25 October 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Cardiff 1&nbsp;– 0 Crystal Palace|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/league_cup/16589189.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=24 January 2012}}</ref>

The week before the League Cup final, Mackay signed a three-and-a-half-year contract extension, tying him to the club until June 2016.<ref>{{cite news|title=Malky commits to Cardiff long term|url=http://www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10335~2620386,00.html|publisher=Cardiff City FC Official Site|date=22 February 2012|access-date=10 October 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120223172345/http://www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0%2C%2C10335~2620386%2C00.html |archive-date=23 February 2012}}</ref> Mackay's side were beaten by [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] in the Wembley final, losing 3–2 on penalties, with a 2–2 scoreline after extra time.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/17093419|title=Cardiff 2–2 Liverpool|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=7 April 2015}}</ref> Cardiff's season ended where it began, as they were beaten 5–0 on aggregate by [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] in the promotion playoffs.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/17890891|title=West Ham United 3–0 Cardiff City|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=7 April 2015}}</ref>

Following the success of his first season in Wales, Mackay was reportedly one of the candidates to replace [[Paul Lambert]] at [[Premier League]] side [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]]. He turned down the offer and said he was fully committed to Cardiff City.<ref name="Norwich rejected">{{cite news|title=Cardiff boss Mackay snubs Norwich |url=http://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/football/premier_league/9743219.Cardiff_boss_Mackay_snubs_Norwich|newspaper=Daily Echo|date=4 June 2012|access-date=4 June 2012}}</ref> He oversaw Cardiff's strongest ever home start to a league season, as they won each of their first ten fixtures at [[Cardiff City Stadium]], the previous club record being nine consecutive home wins. Mackay went on to lead Cardiff to promotion to the Premier League, and sealed the [[2012–13 Football League Championship|Championship]] title after a 1–1 draw away at [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/22133470|title=Burnley 1–1 Cardiff|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=7 April 2015}}</ref> Mackay was voted the [[League Managers Association]] (LMA) Championship Manager of the Year on 21 May 2013.<ref>{{cite news|title=Malky scoops LMA Manager gong|url=http://www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk/news/article/malky-scoops-lma-manager-gong-829699.aspx?|publisher=Cardiff City F.C.|access-date=21 May 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130811120033/http://www.cardiffcityfc.co.uk/news/article/malky-scoops-lma-manager-gong-829699.aspx|archive-date=11 August 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

During the [[2013–14 Cardiff City F.C. season|2013–14 season]], Mackay came under criticism from club owner [[Vincent Tan]] concerning his signings, transfer budgets, results and style of play.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Woodward|first=Hamish|date=2021-06-13|title=The Cardiff City Manager Who Threw His Career Away|url=https://atletifo.com/football/the-cardiff-city-manager-who-threw-his-career-away/|access-date=2021-06-13|website=Atletifo Sports|language=en-US}}</ref> In December 2013, Tan told Mackay to resign from the club or be dismissed.<ref>{{cite web |last=Ornstein |first=David |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/25457024 |title=Cardiff City: Malky Mackay told to quit or be sacked by Vincent Tan |publisher=BBC Sport |date=20 December 2013 |access-date=20 December 2013}}</ref> After a 3–0 home defeat to [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]], Mackay was sacked by Cardiff on 27 December 2013 after a meeting with the club's directors.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/wales/25526438 |title=Malky Mackay: Cardiff City sack manager |publisher=BBC Sport |date=27 December 2013 |access-date=28 December 2013}}</ref>

===Scottish Football Association===
In December 2016, the [[Scottish Football Association]] (Scottish FA) appointed Mackay as its performance director, responsible for overseeing the development of young players.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38313452|title=SFA: Malky Mackay to be appointed new performance director |first=Chris |last=McLaughlin |publisher=BBC |website=BBC Sport|date=15 December 2016|access-date=11 March 2017}}</ref> The appointment was criticised by some people, including politician [[Clare Haughey]], due to Mackay's previous behaviour.<ref name = "anti-racism"/> The anti-racism campaign group [[Kick It Out (organisation)|Kick It Out]] made comments that were supportive of Mackay, pointing out that he had since received equality and diversity training from the (English) FA.<ref name="anti-racism">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-38314905|title=Anti-racism groups back Malky Mackay for SFA job |website=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=14 December 2016|access-date=11 March 2017}}</ref>

During his tenure with the SFA, Mackay was appointed caretaker manager of the [[Scotland national football team|Scotland national team]].<ref name=ScotlandIn>{{cite news |first=Chris |last=McLaughlin |title=Scotland: Malky Mackay takes interim charge for Netherlands friendly |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41612064 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=13 October 2017 |access-date=13 October 2017}}</ref> He gave four players debuts in his only match, which the team lost 1–0 to the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] in a friendly at [[Pittodrie Stadium|Pittodrie]] on 9 November 2017.<ref>{{cite news |title=Scotland 0–1 Netherlands |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/41937222 |access-date=24 February 2020 |publisher=BBC |website=BBC Sport |date=9 November 2017}}</ref> Mackay resigned from his position as performance director in November 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/54993877 |title=Malky Mackay: Scottish FA performance director resigns |publisher=BBC |website=BBC Sport |date=18 November 2020 |accessdate=26 May 2021}}</ref>

===Ross County===
Mackay was appointed manager of [[Ross County F.C.|Ross County]] in May 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://news.stv.tv/sport/ross-county-appoint-malky-mackay-to-succeed-john-hughes?top |title=Ross County appoint Malky Mackay to succeed John Hughes |first=Andy |last=Coyle |website=STV Sport |date=26 May 2021 |accessdate=26 May 2021}}</ref> The move was once again met with fan backlash as a section of Ross County fans criticised the club's decision to appoint Mackay as manager.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.theguardian.com/football/2021/may/26/ross-county-face-fan-backlash-after-appointing-malky-mackay-as-manager |title= Ross County face fan backlash after appointing Malky Mackay as manager |website=Guardian|date=27 May 2021 |accessdate=27 May 2021}}</ref> In his first season as manager, County finished in sixth position in the [[2021–22 Scottish Premiership|Scottish Premiership]] despite being favourites for relegation. They fell to an 11th-place finish in [[2022–23 Scottish Premiership|2022–23]], and avoided relegation by winning a play-off against [[Partick Thistle F.C.|Partick Thistle]] on [[penalty shootout (association football)|penalties]].<ref name = countysack>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/67426270 |title=Ross County sack Malky Mackay after nine-game winless run |publisher=BBC Sport |date=15 November 2023 |access-date=15 November 2023}}</ref> Mackay was sacked by County on 15 November 2023, with the team on a nine-game winless run and sitting in 11th place in the [[2023–24 Scottish Premiership|Premiership]] table.<ref name = countysack/>

===Hibernian===
On 14 May 2024, [[Hibernian F.C.|Hibernian]] announced that they had appointed Mackay to a sporting director role.<ref name = hibs/> The news came on the same day as the sacking of head coach [[Nick Montgomery]], with the club saying that Mackay would lead the process for appointing his replacement.<ref name = hibs>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/cld009k2kv7o |title=Hibernian sack head coach Nick Montgomery and add Malky Mackay as sporting director |website=BBC Sport |date=14 May 2024 |access-date=16 May 2024}}</ref>

==Controversies==
Mackay was expected to become manager of Premier League club [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] in August 2014, but dropped out of contention after Cardiff City sent a dossier to [[the Football Association]] alleging misconduct by Mackay and sporting director Iain Moody.<ref name="dossier">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28860902|title=Malky Mackay: Crystal Palace will not appoint ex-Cardiff manager|publisher=BBC Sport|first=David|last=Ornstein|date=21 August 2014|access-date=21 August 2014}}</ref> It subsequently emerged that some of the allegations related to text messages considered to be racist, sexist and homophobic in nature.<ref name="dossier"/><ref name="moody">{{cite news|last1=Ornstein|first1=David|title=Malky Mackay text claims: Iain Moody leaves Crystal Palace|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28880830|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=21 August 2014}}</ref> Cardiff questioned the fees paid to agents and lawyers relating to transfer deals made while Mackay and Moody worked for the club.<ref name="palace guardian">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/aug/20/crystal-palace-malky-mackay-managerial-cardiff-city-glenn-hoddle|title=Crystal Palace left in limbo as Mackay talks end and Hoddle turns down job|first1=Dominic|last1=Fifield|first2=Stuart|last2=James|newspaper=The Guardian|date=20 August 2014|access-date=21 August 2014}}</ref> A source of tension between the two clubs was the alleged leaking in April 2014 of a Cardiff starting line-up to Crystal Palace, where Moody had become sporting director after leaving Cardiff.<ref name="moody"/><ref name="palace guardian"/> Moody resigned from his position at Palace after the allegations were publicised.<ref name="moody"/>

Via a statement published by the LMA, Mackay apologised for writing two messages that he admitted were disrespectful of other cultures, but denied stating anything of a homophobic or sexist nature.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28890833|title=Malky Mackay: Ex Cardiff boss sorry for two 'disrespectful' texts|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=21 August 2014|date=21 August 2014}}</ref> The LMA itself apologised for characterising the messages as "friendly banter" in the statement.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28896425|title=MalkyMackay: LMA apologises for statement over text 'banter'|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=22 August 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/cardiff-city/11050411/LMA-forced-to-apologise-for-apology-statement-after-calling-racist-sexist-Malkay-Mackay-texts-just-banter.html|title=LMA forced to apologise for apology statement after calling 'racist, sexist' Malkay Mackay texts just 'banter'|newspaper=The Daily Telegraph|access-date=22 August 2014}}</ref> After conducting an investigation, the Football Association announced in July 2015 that it would not charge Mackay or Moody with any offences.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.edp24.co.uk/sport/norwich-city/former_norwich_city_stalwart_malky_mackay_avoids_fa_punishment_1_4155549|title=Former Norwich City stalwart Malky Mackay avoids FA punishment|newspaper=Eastern Daily Press|location=Norwich|date=16 July 2015|access-date=25 February 2016|archive-date=19 July 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150719001013/http://www.edp24.co.uk/sport/norwich-city/former_norwich_city_stalwart_malky_mackay_avoids_fa_punishment_1_4155549|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Mackay's father (also named [[Malky Mackay (footballer, born 1942)|Malky Mackay]]) is a former footballer who played as a striker. The Mackay family are all supporters of Glasgow-based club [[Queen's Park F.C.|Queen's Park]]. Mackay Sr. is a member of the board at the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/footballnation/football-news/2012/02/25/the-big-interview-malky-mackay-snr-on-how-his-son-became-cardiff-city-s-leader-91466-30400510|title=The big interview: Malky Mackay Sr. on how his son became Cardiff City's leader|date=25 February 2012 |access-date=21 May 2012}}</ref> Mackay Jr. played for amateur side Queen's Park as a teenager, whilst working in a branch of the [[Bank of Scotland]].<ref>Shaw, Phil (12 August 2004). [https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/football-league/mackay-soars-with-the-highflying-canaries-6163953.html "Mackay soars with the high-flying Canaries"], ''The Independent''. Retrieved 12 May 2012.</ref>
