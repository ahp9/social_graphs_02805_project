'''David Robert Merrington''' (born 26 January 1945) is an English former professional [[association football|footballer]], manager and commentator. He served as a caretaker manager at [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] in 1978, and [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] in 1980. Merrington was manager of [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] from 1995 until his dismissal in 1996.

Before his career as a coach, Merrington played as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]], spending the majority of his playing career at [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]] before moving to [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]]. Merrington later worked as a commentator for [[BBC Radio Solent]]. He retired from commentating in 2022.

==Playing career==
Merrington played for [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]], appearing in 98 league games (1 goal), including a spell as [[Captain (association football)|captain]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |date=8 September 2004 |title=How Merrington made young Saints march |url=https://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/news/5820343.merrington-made-young-saints-march/ |access-date=7 June 2023 |website=Lancashire Telegraph}}</ref> Whilst at Burnley, he was blighted continually by serious injuries.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ynw62.dial.pipex.com/burnley.htm |title=Burnley |access-date=22 May 2007 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070219123140/http://www.ynw62.dial.pipex.com/burnley.htm |archive-date=19 February 2007}}</ref> He later played for [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]] before retiring and going into coaching.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2 October 1971 |title=Swindon Town v Bristol City: Official Programme |url=http://www.stfcnewspapers.co.uk/images/Programmes/1971-1972/PDF/1971-10-02%20Swindon%20Town%20Vs%20Bristol%20City.pdf |access-date=7 June 2023}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
Merrington was a brief caretaker manager for [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] following the departure of [[Jimmy Adamson]] in 1978, and in 1980 he again took over from Adamson as caretaker manager (for 1 game only) at [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rayner |first=Stuart |date=3 November 2017 |title=Sunderland's post-War caretaker managers: Assessing the men who minded the shop |url=https://www.chroniclelive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/sunderlands-post-war-caretaker-managers-13850531 |access-date=7 June 2023 |website=Chronicle Live}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Scholes |first=Tony |date=15 June 2015 |title=Dave Merrington |url=https://www.clarets-mad.co.uk/feat/ed35/dave_merrington_343082/index.shtml |access-date=7 June 2023 |website=Clarets Mad}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=19 May 1998 |title=CLARETS: Merrington relishes chance of Turf Moor return |url=https://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/news/6153238.clarets-merrington-relishes-chance-turf-moor-return/ |access-date=7 June 2023 |website=Lancashire Telegraph}}</ref>

He became youth coach at [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] in 1983, before taking over as manager for the [[1995-96 in English football|1995–96 season]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Moore |first1=Glenn |title=Saint with an understanding of the sinners Southampton's trust in faith and reason |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/saint-with-an-understanding-of-the-sinners-southamptons-trust-in-faith-and-reason-1578710.html |access-date=24 November 2018 |work=[[The Independent]] |date=21 October 1995}}</ref> He was [[Premier League|Premiership]] [[FA Premier League Manager of the Month|Manager of the Month]] in April 1996, his only full season in management.<ref name=":2" /> Despite securing Southampton's top flight status on goal difference, he was dismissed on 14 June 1996 and succeeded by [[Graeme Souness]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Duxbury |first=Nick |date=14 June 1996 |title=Merrington 'shocked' by Southampton dismissal |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/merrington-shocked-by-southampton-dismissal-1337226.html |access-date=7 June 2023 |website=The Independent}}</ref> During the final weeks of the 1995–96 season, he guided Southampton to two crucial wins which played a big part in their survival - they first beat [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] (champions that season) 3–1 at [[The Dell (Southampton)|The Dell]], and then won their penultimate game 1–0 at [[Bolton Wanderers F.C.|Bolton Wanderers]], a result which confirmed the other side's relegation.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.southampton-mad.co.uk/footydb/loadtmrs.asp?ssnno=125&teamno=479 |title=Results Fixtures 1995-1996 Southampton - Southampton FC - Saints Mad |website=www.southampton-mad.co.uk |access-date=27 January 2022 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120912021200/http://www.southampton-mad.co.uk/footydb/loadtmrs.asp?ssnno=125&teamno=479 |archive-date=12 September 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

A year later he returned to the club as a coach under next manager [[Dave Jones (football manager)|Dave Jones]] but left again three years later after Jones was succeeded by [[Glenn Hoddle]].<ref name=":0" /> His next stop was a brief spell working as [[Walsall F.C.|Walsall]]'s first-team coach alongside manager [[Colin Lee]] in 2002, but later resigned for personal reasons.<ref>{{Cite web |date=31 July 2002 |title=Walsall assistant Merrington resigns |url=https://www.irishexaminer.com/sport/soccer/arid-30062116.html |access-date=7 June 2023 |website=Irish Examiner}}</ref>

==Media career==
Merrington worked for [[BBC Radio Solent]], commentating on [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] matches.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/21576543 |title = Rickie Lambert backed for England by Dave Merrington - BBC Sport| work=BBC Sport }}</ref> Merrington retired from commentary at the end of the [[2021–22 Southampton F.C. season|2021–22]] season.<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 May 2022 |title=Merrington ready for "emotional" last commentary |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/news/2022-05-16/dave-merrington-final-bbc-solent-commentary-feature |access-date=17 May 2022 |website=Southampton FC}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=20 May 2022 |title=Southampton football pundit Dave Merrington retires |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-hampshire-61513569 |access-date=23 May 2022 |website=23 May 2022}}</ref>
