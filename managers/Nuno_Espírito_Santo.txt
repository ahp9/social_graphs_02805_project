'''Nuno Herlander Simões Espírito Santo''' (born 25 January 1974), known as '''Nuno Espírito Santo''' or simply '''Nuno''', is a Portuguese [[Manager (association football)|football manager]] and former [[Football player|player]] who played as a [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] and is currently head coach of [[Premier League]] club [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]].

During his career, Espírito Santo first made a name for himself in Spain, playing for three teams in five years. He later returned to Portugal to represent [[FC Porto|Porto]], and also played professionally in Russia. He was part of the [[Portugal national football team|Portuguese squad]] at [[UEFA Euro 2008]], but never won a [[Cap (association football)|cap]] for the national team.

Espírito Santo started his coaching career at Greek club [[Panathinaikos F.C.|Panathinaikos]] as an assistant. He became a coach in 2012, leading Portuguese club [[Rio Ave F.C.|Rio Ave]] to both domestic cup finals in 2014. After brief spells at [[Valencia CF|Valencia]] in Spain's [[La Liga]], and a return to Porto, he managed [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] for four years. In 2021, he took over as manager of [[Tottenham Hotspur]], but was relieved of his duties after four months in charge. He became manager of [[Al-Ittihad Club (Jeddah)|Al-Ittihad Club]] in July 2022 where he won a [[2022–23 Saudi Pro League|league title]] and [[Saudi Super Cup]] before being dismissed in November 2023. The following month, he returned to the Premier League as head coach of [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]]. He was dismissed in September 2025 and later that month was appointed head coach of West Ham United.

==Club career==
===Early career / Deportivo===
Espírito Santo was born in [[São Tomé]], Portuguese São Tomé and Príncipe, before moving to Portugal at the age of 8.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/c30vj26n6vdo|title=Nuno Espirito Santo: Being Premier League's only black manager 'a privilege'|date=6 November 2025|website=BBC Sport}}</ref> He started his football career with [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória Guimarães]]. After a meeting with the then [[Porto]] nightclub owner [[Jorge Mendes]], Espírito Santo became the agent's first client in 1996.<ref>{{cite news |title=Soccer exchange: How a super-agent and a Chinese billionaire planned to trade in players |url=https://www.reuters.com/investigates/special-report/soccer-files-fosun/ |access-date=21 August 2019 |publisher=Reuters |date=3 June 2009}}</ref> Mendes brokered a $1 million transfer the following January to [[La Liga]]'s [[Deportivo de La Coruña|Deportivo La Coruña]],<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lowe |first1=Sid |title='I saw a lot from the bench': how Porto's reserve goalkeeper became manager |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/sep/27/porto-leicester-champions-league-nuno-espirito-santo |access-date=21 August 2019 |work=The Guardian |date=27 September 2016}}</ref> although Espírito Santo spent three of his six seasons at the club out on [[Loan (association football)|loan]], backing up [[Jacques Songo'o]] (1996–1998) and [[José Francisco Molina]] ([[2001–02 La Liga|2001–02]]) when he was part of the team.{{citation needed|date=December 2023}} He was the preferred goalkeeper for the [[2001–02 Copa del Rey|winning campaign]] in the [[Copa del Rey]] in the latter season, although [[Javier Irureta]] played Molina in the [[2002 Copa del Rey Final|final]] victory over [[Real Madrid CF|Real Madrid]].<ref name=cent>{{cite news |last1=Méndez |first1=Dani |title=Centenariazo: Los artífices de la conquista|trans-title=Centenary shock: The authors of the conquest|url=https://www.riazor.org/centenariazo-los-artifices-de-la-conquista/ |access-date=31 October 2021 |publisher=Riazor.org |language=es}}</ref>

In [[1999–2000 Segunda División|1999–2000]], as he represented [[CP Mérida|Mérida]] in the [[Segunda División]], Espírito Santo won the [[Ricardo Zamora Trophy]] and helped the team finish sixth, but the club would be relegated to [[Segunda División B]] due to irregularities.<ref name=zamora>{{cite news|url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2012/05/17/mas_futbol/1337236018_850215.html|title=El primer fichaje de la factoría Jorge Mendes|trans-title=First signing of Jorge Mendes factory|newspaper=[[Diario AS]]|first=Manu|last=Sainz|language=es|date=17 May 2012|access-date=18 April 2014}}</ref> He was loaned to [[CA Osasuna|Osasuna]] the [[2000–01 La Liga|following season]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rcdeportivo.es/noticia/nuno-cedido-al-osasuna|title=Nuno, cedido al Osasuna|trans-title=Nuno, loaned to Osasuna|publisher=Deportivo La Coruña|language=es|date=11 July 2000|access-date=3 January 2019}}</ref> going on to rank seventh in the Zamora as his team finished only one point above the relegation zone in the top tier.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nacion.com/puro-deporte/mexicano-aguirre-logra-salvar-del-descenso-a-osasuna/VYJKTW2UZZFWNLGV7U4XIRRQCI/story/|title=Mexicano Aguirre logra salvar del descenso a Osasuna|trans-title=Mexican Aguirre leads Osasuna out of relegation|newspaper=[[La Nación (San José)|La Nación]]|first=Fermín|last=Zariquiegui|language=es|date=23 June 2002|access-date=4 January 2019}}</ref>

===Porto===
[[José Mourinho]]'s [[FC Porto|Porto]] paid €3 million to sign Espírito Santo in July 2002, as part of the deal that saw [[Jorge Andrade]] join Deportivo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcporto.pt/IncFCP/PDF/Investor_Relations/FactosRelevantes/VendaJAndrade22072002.pdf|title=Venda do passe do jogador Jorge Andrade|trans-title=Player Jorge Andrade's pass sold|publisher=FC Porto|language=pt|date=22 July 2002|access-date=4 October 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101205171120/http://www.fcporto.pt/IncFCP/PDF/Investor_Relations/FactosRelevantes/VendaJAndrade22072002.pdf|archive-date=5 December 2010}}</ref> During a 2003 [[Taça de Portugal]] match against [[Varzim S.C.|Varzim]], he was allowed by Mourinho to convert a [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty kick]], scoring the club's last goal in a [[2002–03 Taça de Portugal|7–0 home win]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://jn.sapo.pt/PaginaInicial/Desporto/Interior.aspx?content_id=1023740|title=F.C. In January 2007, Espírito Santo returned to Portugal for a stint with [[C.D. Aves|Aves]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/d-aves-nuno-espirito-santo-reforca-leque-de-opcoes-de-neca|title=D. He returned to Porto in July, backing up Brazilian [[Helton Arruda|Helton]] during most of his spell.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/fc-porto/detalhe/nuno-promete-dar-luta-a-helton.html|title=Nuno promete dar luta a Helton|trans-title=Nuno promises to challenge Helton|newspaper=[[Record (Portuguese newspaper)|Record]]|language=pt|date=13 July 2007|access-date=12 January 2018}}{{Dead link|date=September 2025 |bot=InternetArchiveBot }}</ref> Despite his limited involvement on the pitch – earning him the nickname ''O Substituto'' – he was considered a leader at the club.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bates |first1=Pearce |title="O Substituto", Jorge Mendes nightclub encounter, a Wolves love affair, the story of Nuno Espirito Santo |url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/o-substituto-jorge-mendes-nightclub-14932075 |access-date=21 August 2019 |work=Birmingham Mail |date=31 July 2018}}</ref>

Espírito Santo again played second-fiddle to Helton during the [[2008–09 Primeira Liga|2008–09 season]], appearing in only four games, but was the starter throughout the [[2008–09 Taça de Portugal|domestic cup campaign]], including the 1–0 final win against [[F.C. Paços de Ferreira|Paços de Ferreira]].<ref name=quem>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/helton-quem-sou-eu-para-dar-conselhos-ao-nuno-video|title=Helton: "Quem sou eu para dar conselhos ao Nuno?" ==International career==
Born in São Tomé and Príncipe, Espírito Santo moved to Portugal at a young age and hold dual Portuguese and Santomean citizenship.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://memoriaporto.blogspot.com/2016/06/nuno-espirito-santo-regresso-ao-fc.html|title=Memória Portista: Nuno Espírito Santo: Regresso ao FC Porto dum Homem da Casa|first=Armando|last=Pinto|date=2 June 2016}}</ref> He represented [[Portugal Olympic football team|Portugal]] at the [[1996 Summer Olympics]], playing four matches for the [[Football at the 1996 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|fourth-placed team]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/dossiers/desporto/jogos-olimpicos/historico/interior/atlanta1996---os-portugueses-1008872.html|title=Atlanta1996 – Os portugueses|trans-title=Atlanta1996 – The Portuguese|newspaper=[[Diário de Notícias]]|language=pt|date=3 September 2008|access-date=18 April 2018|archive-date=19 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180619063407/https://www.dn.pt/dossiers/desporto/jogos-olimpicos/historico/interior/atlanta1996---os-portugueses-1008872.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> He also played for [[Portugal national football B team|the nation's B team]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Torneio "Vale do Tejo" (Portugal) |website=[[RSSSF]] |url=https://www.rsssf.org/tablesv/valedotejo-b.html |access-date=31 October 2021}}</ref> [[Cap (association football)|Uncapped]], he was called to the [[UEFA Euro 2008 squads#Portugal|senior squad]] competing in [[UEFA Euro 2008]], replacing the injured [[Quim (footballer, born 1975)|Quim]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.xl.pt/arquivo/interior.aspx?content_id=345461|title=Nuno é o substituto de Quim|trans-title=Nuno replaces Quim|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=7 June 2008|access-date=31 July 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131110190044/http://www.record.xl.pt/arquivo/interior.aspx?content_id=345461|archive-date=10 November 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> but did not play a match.

==Coaching career==
===Beginnings===
On 21 June 2010, Porto announced that Espírito Santo's contract would not be renewed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcporto.pt/Noticias/Futebol/noticiafutebol_futcomunicadosad_210610_53339.asp|title=Comunicado da FC Porto – Futebol, SAD|trans-title=FC Porto announcement – Football, PLSC|publisher=FC Porto|language=pt|date=21 June 2010|access-date=4 July 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100625021741/http://www.fcporto.pt/Noticias/Futebol/noticiafutebol_futcomunicadosad_210610_53339.asp|archive-date=25 June 2010}}</ref> The 36-year-old said he would always support Porto as he left.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcporto.pt/Noticias/Futebol/noticiafutebol_futnunodec_210610_53347.asp|title=Nuno Espírito Santo: 'Sou e serei Porto!'|trans-title=Nuno Espírito Santo: 'I am and will be Porto!'|publisher=FC Porto|language=pt|date=21 June 2010|access-date=4 July 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100625021747/http://www.fcporto.pt/Noticias/Futebol/noticiafutebol_futnunodec_210610_53347.asp|archive-date=25 June 2010}}</ref> After his retirement, he rejoined former Porto manager [[Jesualdo Ferreira]], moving to [[Málaga CF|Málaga]] as a goalkeeping coach, before the pair signed for [[Panathinaikos FC|Panathinaikos]] in November.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.jn.pt/desporto/interior/o-tecnico-que-ja-o-era-antes-de-o-ser-5199328.html|title=O técnico que já o era antes de o ser|trans-title=The manager who was one before ever being so|newspaper=Jornal de Notícias|language=pt|date=30 May 2016|access-date=16 April 2018}}</ref>

===Rio Ave===
In May 2012, [[Rio Ave F.C.|Rio Ave]] dismissed manager [[Carlos Brito (footballer, born 1963)|Carlos Brito]] and announced the appointment of Espírito Santo.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abola.pt/nnh/ver.aspx?id=331416|title=Nuno Espírito Santo é o novo treinador|trans-title=Nuno Espírito Santo is the new manager|newspaper=[[A Bola]]|language=pt|date=15 May 2012|access-date=24 May 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120518075950/http://www.abola.pt/nnh/ver.aspx?id=331416|archive-date=18 May 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> In his first match in charge on 18 August, the team lost 1–0 at home to [[C.S. Marítimo|Marítimo]] in the first game of the [[2012–13 Primeira Liga]] season.<ref>{{cite news |title=Marítimo vence no reduto do Rio Ave |trans-title=Marítimo win on Rio Ave's ground|url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/maritimo/maritimo-vence-no-reduto-do-rio-ave_d580239 |access-date=19 June 2023 |publisher=[[Rádio e Televisão de Portugal]] |date=18 August 2012 |language=pt}}</ref> He recorded his first win nine days later, by the same margin away to [[Sporting CP]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Nuno Espírito Santo: "Sentimento de missão cumprida"|trans-title=Nuno Espírito Santo: "Feeling of mission accomplished" |url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-bwin/rio-ave/detalhe/nuno-espirito-santo-sentimento-de-missao-cumprida-775379 |access-date=19 June 2023 |work=Record |date=27 August 2012 |language=pt}}</ref>

In [[2013–14 Primeira Liga|his second season]] in charge, Espírito Santo's team reached both the [[2013–14 Taça de Portugal|Taça de Portugal]] and [[Taça da Liga]] finals, therefore [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League|leading them]] to the [[UEFA Europa League]] for the first time in their history.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.xl.pt/Futebol/Nacional/1a_liga/rio_ave/interior.aspx?content_id=878365|title=Nuno Espírito Santo feliz com 2.ª final da época|trans-title=Nuno Espírito Santo happy with second final of season|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=17 April 2014|access-date=18 April 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141017134039/http://www.record.xl.pt/Futebol/Nacional/1a_liga/rio_ave/interior.aspx?content_id=878365|archive-date=17 October 2014}}</ref>

On 1 June 2016, Espírito Santo signed a two-year contract with Porto, replacing former head coach [[José Peseiro]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcporto.pt/en/noticias/Pages/Nuno-Espirito-Santo-e-o-novo-treinador-do-FC-Porto.aspx|title=Nuno Espírito Santo is the new coach at FC Porto|publisher=FC Porto|date=1 June 2016|access-date=3 June 2016}}</ref> His first game on 12 August was a return to the [[Estádio dos Arcos]], where his team came from behind to beat Rio Ave 3–1.<ref>{{cite news |title=Porto dá a volta e vence Rio Ave em Vila do Conde|trans-title=Porto pull off a comeback and defeat Rio Ave in Vila do Conde|url=https://pt.uefa.com/news-media/news/0230-0f8e309a8c24-64cc9548ee8b-1000--porto-da-a-volta-e-vence-rio-ave-em-vila-do-conde/ |access-date=19 June 2023 |publisher=UEFA |date=12 August 2016 |language=pt}}</ref> However, after [[2016–17 FC Porto season|a season]] devoid of silverware which included a [[2016–17 Primeira Liga|second-place finish in the league]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/porto-nuno-espirito-santo-primeira-liga/1wj6pmi3nefwu1b3nmdd9hy2ta|title=Porto boss Nuno steps down|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|first=Dom|last=Farrell|date=22 May 2017|access-date=15 August 2018}}</ref> he was relieved of his duties on 22 May 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ojogo.pt/futebol/1a-liga/porto/noticias/interior/nuno-espirito-santo-deixa-comando-tecnico-do-fc-porto-8497194.html|title=Nuno Espírito Santo deixa o FC Porto|trans-title=Nuno Espírito Santo leaves FC Porto|newspaper=[[O Jogo]]|first=José Manuel|last=Ribeiro|language=pt|date=22 May 2017|access-date=22 May 2017}}</ref>

===Wolverhampton Wanderers===
On 31 May 2017, Espírito Santo was named as the new head coach of [[EFL Championship]] club [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]], signing a three-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/40105588|title=Nuno Espirito Santo: Wolves appoint former Porto boss as head coach|publisher=BBC Sport|date=31 May 2017|access-date=31 May 2017}}</ref> He was voted the competition's [[EFL Championship Manager of the Month|Manager of the Month]] in November as his team won all four of their games, scoring 13 goals.<ref name=EFL>{{cite web|url=https://www.efl.com/news/2017/december/manager-of-the-month-nuno---wolverhampton-wanderers/|title=Championship Manager of the Month: Nuno – Wolverhampton Wanderers|publisher=[[English Football League]]|date=8 December 2017|access-date=16 December 2017}}</ref> Espírito Santo led the club to the [[Premier League]] after a six-year absence, achieving promotion with four matches remaining in [[2017–18 EFL Championship|the season]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/43771511|title=Wolves: Championship leaders promoted to Premier League after Fulham draw with Brentford|work=BBC Sport|date=14 April 2018|access-date=16 April 2018}}</ref> and being confirmed as champions with two games to spare.<ref name=CHAMPIONSHIP>{{cite news|url=https://www.expressandstar.com/sport/football/wolverhampton-wanderers-fc/2018/04/21/champions-wolves-win-the-league-after-breezing-past-bolton/|title=Champions! Wolves win the league after breezing past Bolton|newspaper=[[Express & Star]]|date=21 April 2018|access-date=21 April 2018}}</ref> On 10 July 2018, it was announced that his contract had been extended until 2021.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/wolverhampton-wanderers/story/3561324/wolves-extend-manager-nuno-espirito-santo-contract-until-2021|title=Wolves extend manager Nuno Espirito Santo contract until 2021|publisher=[[ESPN]]|date=10 July 2018|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref>

Espírito Santo was awarded the [[Premier League Manager of the Month]] title in his second month managing in the English top division<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/877735|title=Nuno claims Barclays Manager of the Month|publisher=Premier League|date=12 October 2018 |access-date=12 October 2018}}</ref> after his team went unbeaten in September 2018, accruing ten points from four matches and only conceding one goal. It was the first time that a Wolves manager had secured the award, in the club's fifth season in the competition.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/45985099|title=Wolves: The science behind Premier League newcomers' record-breaking start|date=26 October 2018|access-date=5 December 2019 |work=BBC Sport}}</ref> Wolves finished seventh in the [[2018–19 Premier League|2018–19 league season]]; it was the club's highest Premier League ranking, and their highest in the English top-flight since the [[1979–80 Football League First Division|1979–80 season]] when they finished sixth.{{citation needed|date=December 2023}} Wolves also qualified for a European competition for the first time since the [[1980–81 UEFA Cup]], reaching the UEFA Europa League.<ref>{{cite news |title=Wolves v Braga facts |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/0255-0e99edf6408d-367d2041c55b-1000--wolves-v-braga-facts/ |access-date=10 March 2020 |publisher=UEFA |date=11 September 2019}}</ref>

Espírito Santo was awarded the Premier League Manager of the Month title for a second time on 10 July 2020 for a run of five fixtures unbeaten between the beginning of March and the end of June, sandwiching the temporary suspension of the [[2019–20 Premier League]] due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom]].<ref name="Premier League">{{cite web |url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/1715425 |title=Nuno claims Barclays Manager of the Month award |publisher=Premier League |date=10 July 2020 |access-date=10 July 2020}}</ref> The run included four wins and four clean sheets. The season saw Espírito Santo's team achieve a second consecutive seventh-place finish in the Premier League (with a record points total for Wolves in the Premier League of 59), and reach the quarter-finals of the [[2019–20 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]], the club's best such performance since being [[1972 UEFA Cup final|finalists]] in the [[1971–72 UEFA Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Doyle |first1=Paul |title=Late Sevilla winner sees Wolves crash out of Europe |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/sport/soccer/late-sevilla-winner-sees-wolves-crash-out-of-europe-1.4327902 |access-date=11 August 2020 |newspaper=[[The Irish Times]] |date=11 August 2020}}</ref>

On 13 September 2020, at the outset of the [[2020–21 Premier League|2020–21 season]], Espírito Santo's contract at the club was extended until summer 2023.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wolves.co.uk/news/first-team/20200913-nuno-signs-new-wolves-contract/|title=Nuno signs new Wolves contract|date=13 September 2020 |publisher=Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C}}</ref> He was named the Premier League Manager of the Month for October with a run of four fixtures unbeaten, including three wins without conceding; this was the third time he had received the award.<ref name="premierleague.com">{{cite web |url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/1894986|title=Nuno earns Barclays Manager of the Month award |publisher=Premier League |date=13 November 2020 |access-date=13 November 2020}}</ref> On 27 February 2021, he took charge of his 102nd Premier League game as Wolves head coach as his team played out a 1–1 draw with [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] at [[St James' Park]], surpassing [[Mick McCarthy]] as the longest-serving Wolves head coach in the Premier League era.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/56135048|title=Newcastle United 1–1 Wolverhampton Wanderers: Ruben Neves denies hosts the win|last=Bysouth |first=Alex |publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|access-date=27 February 2021|date=27 February 2021}}</ref> On 21 May, Wolves announced that Espírito Santo would leave the club by mutual consent at the end of the season.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Nuno set to leave Wolves |url=https://www.wolves.co.uk/news/club/20210521-nuno-set-to-leave-wolves/ |publisher=Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. |date=21 May 2021 |access-date=27 May 2021}}</ref>

On 30 June 2021, [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] announced Espírito Santo as their new head coach on a two-year contract with an option to extend for a third year.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Nuno Espírito Santo appointed new Head Coach |url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2021/june/nuno-espirito-santo-appointed-new-head-coach | work=Tottenham Hotspur F.C. |date=30 June 2021 |access-date=30 June 2021}}</ref> On his debut on 15 August, the team won 1–0 at home against reigning champions [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] through a [[Son Heung-min]] goal.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Grounds |first1=Ben |title=Tottenham 1-0 Man City: Heung-Min Son strike secures statement win for Spurs |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/tottenham-vs-man-city/report/446296 |access-date=15 August 2021 |publisher=Sky Sports |date=15 August 2021}}</ref> On 29 August, he achieved the best start to a Premier League season for Tottenham after beating [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] to secure three wins from their first three matches. ===Nottingham Forest===

==== 2023–24: Relegation battle ====
On 20 December 2023, Espírito Santo was appointed head coach of Premier League club [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]], following the dismissal of [[Steve Cooper (football manager)|Steve Cooper]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-12-20 |title=Nottingham Forest: Nuno Espirito Santo appointed as head coach |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/articles/cj7gey0y7kyo |website=BBC Sport |access-date=2023-12-20}}</ref> His appointment at Nottingham Forest also reunited him with former players at Wolves, such as [[Morgan Gibbs-White]] and [[Willy Boly]].

The side's first win under his tenure came on 26 December as they beat [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] 3–1.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-12-26 |title=Newcastle 1-3 Nottingham Forest: Chris Wood hits hat-trick against old club as Eddie Howe's troubles deepen |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/newcastle-vs-n-forest/report/482779 |website=Sky Sports}}</ref> This was followed by a 2–1 home win against [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]], which was Forest's first Premier League victory over the club in 29 years.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Late Gibbs-White goal earns Forest win over Man Utd |language=en-GB |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/67809573 |access-date=2023-12-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Nottingham Forest 2-1 Manchester United: Morgan Gibbs-White's late goal brings Erik ten Hag's side back down to earth |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/13038710/nottingham-forest-2-1-manchester-united-morgan-gibbs-whites-late-goal-brings-erik-ten-hags-side-back-down-to-earth |access-date=2023-12-30 |website=Sky Sports |language=en}}</ref> 

On the final day of [[2023–24 Nottingham Forest F.C. season|the season]], Espírito Santo led Forest to a 2–1 away win over [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]] to secure the club's survival from relegation with a 17th-placed finish in the league.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Burnley 1-2 Nottingham Forest: Wood double secures Forest's Premier League safety |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cxr3rxevw4qt |access-date=2024-09-15 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref>

==== 2024–25: Pushing up the table and departure ====
On 14 September 2024, Espírito Santo led Forest to a 1–0 away win over [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] at [[Anfield]], which was the first time the club had won at Liverpool in any competition since February 1969,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Forest stun Liverpool with Hudson-Odoi's winner |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cn02lxjnyknt |access-date=2024-09-15 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref> ending a 25-game winless run at Anfield, and securing Espírito Santo's first ever Premier League victory against Liverpool.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-09-14 |title=Nottingham Forest: Stat on victory over Liverpool |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/cwyl7g840gj|access-date=2024-09-15 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}{{dead link|date=September 2025|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> [[Callum Hudson-Odoi]] scored the winning goal in the 72nd minute.

Espírito Santo led Nottingham Forest to their first win at [[Old Trafford]] since 1994 on 7 December 2024, securing a 3–2 victory over [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Premier League (Sky Sports) |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/man-utd-vs-n-forest/505954 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241203233640/https://www.skysports.com/football/man-utd-vs-n-forest/505954 |archive-date=3 December 2024 |access-date=2024-12-13 |website=Sky Sports |language=en-GB |url-status=live }}</ref>

After securing a sixth consecutive Premier League win with a 3–0 away victory over [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] at [[Molineux Stadium|Molineux]] on 6 January 2025, Nottingham Forest sat third in the Premier League table on the same 40-point total as [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in second place.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Wolves 0-3 Nottingham Forest: Chris Wood and Morgan Gibbs-White score as Forest up to second |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/c6239033kext |access-date=2025-01-10 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref> This defied the expectations of many journalists and pundits, who had predicted at the start of the season that Forest would struggle and get relegated to the [[EFL Championship]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Clarke |first=Lee |date=2024-12-01 |title=Anti Nottingham Forest pundit changes his tune with 'sickening' two-word comment after Ipswich win |url=https://www.nottinghamforest.news/2024/12/01/anti-nottingham-forest-pundit-changes-his-tune-with-sickening-two-word-comment-after-ipswich-win/ |access-date=2024-12-13 |website=Nottingham Forest News |language=en-GB}}</ref> Subsequently, Espírito Santo won the Premier League Manager of the Month award for his oversight of Forest's performances in December 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Nuno wins December Barclays Manager of the Month award |url=https://www.premierleague.com/video/single/4220641 |access-date=2025-01-13 |website=www.premierleague.com |language=en}}</ref>

In April 2025, Espírito Santo was announced as the Premier League Manager of the Month for March. This marks the third time he has won the award this season, having previously claimed it in October and December 2024. He became the first Nottingham Forest manager to win the award three times in a single season.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-04-04 |title=Nottingham Forest news: Nuno Espirito Santo wins manager of the month for March |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/ckg23n769kwo |access-date=2025-04-05 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref> He ultimately led the club to a [[2024–25 Nottingham Forest F.C. season|seventh-place]] finish and qualification for the [[2025–26 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]], marking their first participation in European football in 30 years.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2025/may/25/chelsea-report/ |title=Forest end season with narrow home loss |publisher=Nottingham Forest F.C. |date=25 May 2025 }}</ref>

On 21 June 2025, Nuno signed a new contract to stay with Nottingham Forest until 2028.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2025/june/21/nuno-signs-new-deal/ |title=Nuno signs new deal |publisher=Nottingham Forest F.C. |date=21 June 2025 }}</ref> However, on 9 September 2025, he was dismissed from his duties only three Premier League games into the [[2025–26 Premier League|new season]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=9 September 2025|title=Club Statement: Nuno Espírito Santo |url= https://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/news/2025/september/08/club-statement--nuno-esp-rito-santo/ |access-date=9 September 2025|publisher=Nottingham Forest F.C.}}</ref> His departure was primarly due to his relationship with owner [[Evangelos Marinakis]] breaking down over the summer, with Nuno also publically expressing his displeasure with the club's activity in the transfer market.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/12040/13427489/why-has-nuno-espirito-santo-been-sacked-the-view-from-nottingham-forest-owner-evangelos-marinakis |title=Why has Nuno Espirito Santo been sacked? The view from Nottingham Forest owner Evangelos Marinakis |publisher=Sky Sports |date=9 September 2025 }}</ref>

=== West Ham United ===
On 27 September 2025, Espírito Santo was appointed head coach of Premier League club [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] on a three-year contract, following the dismissal of [[Graham Potter]]. His arrival meant that he would reunite with his only former Wolves player [[Maximilian Kilman]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-09-27 |title=West Ham United appoint Nuno Espírito Santo as Head Coach |url=https://www.whufc.com/news/west-ham-united-appoint-nuno-espirito-santo-head-coach |website=West Ham United F.C. |access-date=2025-09-27}}</ref> He started his tenure as manager with a 1–1 draw at the [[Hill Dickinson Stadium]] against [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] on 29 September.<ref>{{cite web | title=Everton 1–1 West Ham United |website=BBC Sport | date=29 September 2025 | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cy4rxwzkz3lt#WestHamUnited | access-date=30 September 2025}}</ref> On 1st November, his first win came in a 3-1 win over Newcastle United with goals from [[Lucas Paquetá]], [[Tomáš Souček]] and an own goal from [[Sven Botman]] and it was the first time the Hammers won at home since beating [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] 2-0 in February.<ref>{{cite web | title=West Ham United 3-1 Newcaslte United |website=BBC Sport | date=1 November 2025 | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cvgknk9ey08t| access-date=7 November 2025}}</ref>
