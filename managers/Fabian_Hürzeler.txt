'''Fabian Marc Hürzeler''' (born 26 February 1993) is a German professional [[Manager (association football)|football manager]] and former [[Football player|player]]. He is the current head coach of {{English football updater|Brigh&HA}} club [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]].  He became the youngest permanent head coach in Premier League history at age 31.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Hurzeler makes history with Barclays Manager of the Month award |url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/4110693 |access-date=2024-11-11 |website=www.premierleague.com |language=en |archive-date=13 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240913110618/https://www.premierleague.com/news/4110693 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Playing career==
Hürzeler joined [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]]'s [[FC Bayern Munich Junior Team|youth academy]] at the age of ten and went through all of the club's youth teams. He also represented [[Germany national youth football team|Germany]] at several youth levels, collecting 21 appearances for his country. His career as a professional footballer took place mainly in the lower ranks of German football, playing with [[FC Bayern Munich II|Bayern Munich II]], [[TSG 1899 Hoffenheim II|TSG Hoffenheim II]], and [[TSV 1860 Munich II|1860 Munich II]]. He effectively ended his professional playing career at the age of 22, but continued to play at an amateur level with [[FC Pipinsried]], as a [[player-coach]]. After joining the coaching staff of [[FC St. Pauli]] in 2020, Hürzeler joined [[Hamburg]]-based sixth division club [[Eimsbütteler TV]] and was promoted with the club to the [[Oberliga Hamburg]], the fifth-highest level, in 2022.

==Coaching career==
===Early career===
In 2016, Hürzeler, who had been eyeing a transition into a managerial role for many years and had been predicted a "great career as a coach" by former Bayern Munich II teammate [[Emre Can]], moved down to the amateur ranks of German football, joining [[FC Pipinsried]] in a joint player-head coach role.<ref name="bundesliga">{{cite web |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/2bundesliga/news/fabian-hurzeler-who-is-st-pauli-s-us-born-coach-former-bayern-munich-youth-player-22956 |title=Fabian Hürzeler: Who is the former Bayern Munich youth player outdoing Jürgen Klopp and reviving St Pauli's fortunes? |date=4 April 2023 |publisher=Bundesliga |access-date=6 April 2023 |archive-date=27 May 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240527175647/https://www.bundesliga.com/en/2bundesliga/news/fabian-hurzeler-who-is-st-pauli-s-us-born-coach-former-bayern-munich-youth-player-22956 |url-status=live }}</ref> In 2018, he also took on the job as assistant manager in charge of the [[Germany national under-20 football team|Germany U20s]] and [[Germany national under-18 football team|Germany U18s]].<ref name="bundesliga" /> Hürzeler took Pipinsried up from [[Bayernliga]] Süd, the fifth-highest level in Germany, to [[Regionalliga Bayern]], the fourth division. FC Pipinsried avoided relegation to Bayernliga Süd in the first season, but went down the following season. Hürzeler stayed at Pipinsried for one further year.

===Brighton & Hove Albion===
On 15 June 2024, Hürzeler became the new head coach of [[Premier League]] club [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|Brighton & Hove Albion]], replacing [[Roberto De Zerbi]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.|url=https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/4039117/albion-appoint-hurzeler-as-new-head-coach|title=Albion appoint Hürzeler as new head coach|date=15 June 2024|access-date=15 June 2024|archive-date=17 June 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240617044126/https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/4039117/albion-appoint-hurzeler-as-new-head-coach|url-status=live}}</ref> He is the youngest permanent head coach in Premier League history.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Premier League|url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/4035442|title=Brighton appoint Hurzeler as head coach|date=15 June 2024|access-date=15 June 2024|archive-date=15 June 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240615161043/https://www.premierleague.com/news/4035442|url-status=live}}</ref> In his first competitive match with Brighton, Hürzeler's team won 3–0 away at [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] in the Premier League.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Premier League|url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/4082458#:~:text=Seagulls%20win%203%2D0%20at,from%20Mitoma%2C%20Welbeck%20and%20Adingra&text=Fabian%20Hurzeler%20made%20a%20fantastic,man%20Everton%20at%20Goodison%20Park.|title=Brighton ease past Everton as Hurzeler starts in style|date=17 August 2024|access-date=23 August 2024}}</ref> Hürzeler oversaw an unbeaten start to Brighton's season, which also included a home win against [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] and an away draw at [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]], earning him the [[Premier League Manager of the Month]] award for August.<ref name="MOTMSEP"/> In his first season, Brighton finished 8th in the Premier League after they beat [[UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] winners [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] 4–1, achieving a first ever league double over them. Despite this, Brighton missed out on European qualification due to rivals [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] winning the [[FA Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cx2ee3l23ezt|title=Brighton finish in the top half|date=25 May 2025|access-date=28 May 2025}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hürzeler was born in [[Houston]], Texas, to a Swiss father and a German mother while they were working in the United States.<ref name="bundesliga" /> His family moved back to Germany when he was two years old, where he grew up in the [[Munich]] district of [[Bogenhausen]]. He holds German, Swiss, and American citizenship.<ref>{{cite web |date=30 January 2014 |title=Fabian Hürzeler: Der Internationale |url=https://www.tsg-hoffenheim.de/aktuelles/news/2014/01/fabian-huerzeler-der-internationale/ |publisher=TSG Hoffenheim |language=de |access-date=7 June 2024 |archive-date=24 December 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221224215851/https://www.tsg-hoffenheim.de/aktuelles/news/2014/01/fabian-huerzeler-der-internationale/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
