'''Daniel Farke''' ({{IPA|de|ˈdaːni̯eːl ˈfaʁkə, -ni̯ɛl -}}; born 30 October 1976) is a German professional football manager and former player who is the manager of {{English football updater|LeedsUni}} club [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]].

Farke spent most of his playing career with [[SV Lippstadt 08|SV Lippstadt]], where he also began his management career. After a spell at [[Borussia Dortmund II]], he led [[Norwich City]] for four years, twice winning the [[EFL Championship]]. In January 2022 he was appointed as head coach of [[Russian Premier League]] club [[FC Krasnodar|Krasnodar]], but resigned two months later without managing a game due to the [[2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine|Russian invasion of Ukraine]]. He then managed [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] for a season before being appointed manager of Leeds United.

==Playing career==
Farke started his career with hometown club SV Steinhausen.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.wp.de/sport/lokalsport/warstein/daniel-farke-hat-sich-beim-sv-lippstadt-ein-denkmal-gesetzt-id11250346.html |title=Daniel Farke hat sich beim SV Lippstadt ein Denkmal gesetzt |trans-title=Daniel Farke has made a memorial at SV Lippstadt |first=Hartwig |last=Sellmann |newspaper=Westfalenpost |location=Hagen |date=3 November 2015 |access-date=28 May 2019}}</ref> He spent the majority of his playing career with [[SV Lippstadt 08|SV Lippstadt]], with whom he had three spells.<ref>[https://www.fupa.net/spieler/daniel-farke-251577.html German career stats] – FuPa</ref> Farke said that he "knew how to score" but was probably "the slowest striker in the whole of Western Europe".<ref>{{Citation|title=Why does Norwich's manager dislike horses? {{!}} Tubes Meets Daniel Farke|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BMzizwhUp8M|language=en|access-date=7 December 2019}}</ref> He played his whole career in the lower levels of German football.<ref name=edp23may17/>

==Managerial career==
===Early managerial career===
Farke began his managerial career with [[SV Lippstadt 08|SV Lippstadt]]. He was in charge for six years and took Lippstadt from the sixth tier of German football to the fourth, with his time in charge considered by some to be the most successful for Lippstadt football.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Bryant |first=Toby |date=2023-06-26 |title=Daniel Farke’s career record compared with past Leeds United managers as rumours grow |url=https://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/sport/football/leeds-united/daniel-farkes-career-record-compared-with-past-leeds-united-managers-as-ex-norwich-boss-sparks-fan-intrigue-4197298 |access-date=2025-04-22 |website=Yorkshire Evening Post |language=en}}</ref> He stepped down after six years, planning to take a year's sabbatical, before accepting the opportunity to take charge of [[Borussia Dortmund II]], the reserve side of German club [[Borussia Dortmund]]. He managed the team for two seasons before he was recruited by [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]], having failed to agree a new contract at Dortmund.<ref name=edp23may17/> He finished with a record of 29 wins, 21 draws and six losses.<ref name="Borussia Dortmund II record">{{cite web |url=https://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/1-bundesliga/borussia-dortmund-ii-1291/trainer.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190526214553/https://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/1-bundesliga/borussia-dortmund-ii-1291/trainer.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=26 May 2019 |title=Borussia Dortmund II – Trainerhistorie |website=Kicker |publisher=Olympia-Verlag |access-date=26 May 2019 |language=de}}</ref>

Borussia Dortmund II boss as head coach">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40013726 |title=Daniel Farke: Norwich City appoint Borussia Dortmund II boss as head coach |publisher=BBC [[Carrow Road]] saw Norwich beat [[Swindon Town F.C.|Swindon Town]] 3–2 in the [[2017–18 EFL Cup|EFL Cup]].<ref>{{cite web 2018}}</ref> Farke completed his inaugural season in the Championship winning 15 games, drawing 15 and losing 16 – finishing 14th in the league table.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/oct/30/norwich-daniel-farke-bournemouth-carabao-cup-championship-football |title=Norwich showing signs of promise as Daniel Farke's methods hit home The following season saw Norwich promoted as Championship title winners. Farke won the Manager of the Month award for November<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/News/2018/december/daniel-farke-manager-of-the-month-november/ |title=Daniel Farke wins Sky Bet Championship Manager of the Month award |publisher=Norwich City F.C. |date=7 December 2018 |access-date=28 May 2019 |archive-date=3 August 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210803064139/https://www.canaries.co.uk/News/2018/december/daniel-farke-manager-of-the-month-november/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> and in March 2019 signed a three-year contract extension, tying him to the club until June 2022.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/News/2019/march/daniel-farke-signs-new-contract-at-norwich-city/ |title=Daniel Farke signs new contract at Norwich City |publisher=Norwich City F.C. |date=13 March 2019 |access-date=28 May 2019}}</ref> The team secured promotion to the [[Premier League]] on their penultimate match of the [[2018–19 EFL Championship|2018–19 season]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47994427 |title=Norwich City 2–1 Blackburn Rovers |first=James |last=Law |publisher=BBC Sport |date=27 April 2019 |access-date=28 May 2019}}</ref> before securing the league title after the last match of the season.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48081773 |title=Aston Villa 1–2 Norwich City |publisher=BBC Sport |date=5 May 2019 |access-date=28 May 2019}}</ref>

Norwich were relegated from the Premier League following a 4–0 home loss to [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] on 12 July 2020.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Darwen |first1=Chris |title=REVIEW Premier League review: Long live Jose Mourinho, Chelsea blunted by Blades and do Liverpool care about 100 points? |url=https://talksport.com/football/731815/premier-league-review-jose-mourinho-chelsea-liverpool-100-points/ |access-date=14 July 2020 |publisher=[[Talksport]] |date=13 July 2020}}</ref> In 2020–21, Norwich won the Championship and secured an immediate return to the Premier League with a club record 97 points.<ref name=efl21>{{cite news |title=Norwich City 4&ndash;1 Reading |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/56869183 |access-date=1 May 2021 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=1 May 2021}}</ref> Farke was subsequently named [[2020–21 EFL Championship#Annual|EFL Championship Manager of the Season]].<ref>{{cite news|title= 2021 EFL Awards winners announced|url=https://www.efl.com/news/2021/april/2021-efl-awards-winners-announced/|access-date= 26 August 2021 |date=29 April 2021}}</ref> On 21 July 2021, Farke signed a new four-year contract with the club.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Daniel Farke pens contract extension|url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/News/2021/july/farke-pens-contract-extension/|access-date=2021-07-21|website=www.canaries.co.uk|language=en-gb}}</ref>

In September 2021, Farke lost his 15th consecutive Premier League game in charge of Norwich  (including the [[2019–20 Norwich City F.C. season|2019-20 season]]), a record for a club or a manager in English top-flight history.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kilpatrick |first1=James |title=Norwich 1-3 Watford: Ismaila Sarr scores twice for Hornets to keep Canaries winless |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/norwich-vs-watford/report/446334 |access-date=19 September 2021 |work=Sky Sports |date=18 September 2021}}</ref> On 6 November, Norwich recorded their first league win of the season with a 2–1 away victory over [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]]. However, on the same day Farke was dismissed after the game and later replaced by former Aston Villa head coach [[Dean Smith (footballer, born 1971)|Dean Smith]] on 15 November 2021.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.canaries.co.uk/content/club-statement-daniel-farke|title=Club statement: Daniel Farke|publisher=Norwich City F.C.|date=6 November 2021|access-date=6 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/59194369 |title=Daniel Farke sacked by Norwich City despite first league win of season |website=BBC Sport |date=6 November 2021 |access-date=6 November 2021}}</ref> He finished with a record of 87 wins, 49 draws and 72 losses.<ref name="farke-sb">{{cite web |url=https://www.soccerbase.com/managers/manager.sd?manager_id=4396 |title=Managers: Daniel Farke |website=Soccerbase |publisher=Centurycomm |access-date=17 August 2019}}</ref>

===Krasnodar===
On 13 January 2022, [[FC Krasnodar|Krasnodar]] announced that Farke had been appointed as the club's new head coach on a contract until 30 June 2024.<ref>{{cite web |title=ГЛАВНЫМ ТРЕНЕРОМ "КРАСНОДАРА" СТАЛ ДАНИЭЛЬ ФАРКЕ |url=https://fckrasnodar.ru/team/news/object/?object_id=141031 |website=fckrasnodar.ru/ |publisher=FC Krasnodar |access-date=13 January 2022 |language=Russian |date=13 January 2022}}</ref> He left the club on 2 March, along with the rest of his coaching staff, due to the [[2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine|Russian invasion of Ukraine]].<ref>{{cite web |title="КРАСНОДАР" И ДАНИЭЛЬ ФАРКЕ РАСТОРГЛИ КОНТРАКТ ПО ВЗАИМНОМУ СОГЛАСИЮ |url=https://www.fckrasnodar.ru/team/news/object/?object_id=141381 |website=fckrasnodar.ru/ |publisher=FC Krasnodar |access-date=2 March 2022 |language=Russian |date=2 March 2022}}</ref> Due to the winter break and [[Krasnodar International Airport]]'s military use, he did not take charge of any matches for the club.<ref name=BBCKrasnodar>{{cite news |title=Daniel Farke: Former Norwich City boss leaves Russian club Krasnodar |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/60592283 |access-date=2 March 2022 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 March 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=TNT Sports is not available in your region |url=https://www.tntsports.co.uk/geoblocking.shtml |access-date=2025-09-03 |website=www.tntsports.co.uk}}</ref>

===Borussia Mönchengladbach===
On 4 June 2022, Farke was appointed head coach of [[Borussia Mönchengladbach]] on a three-year deal.<ref name="Borussia Mönchengladbach job">{{cite web |url=https://www.borussia.de/en/news/news/2022-06-04-daniel-farke-takes-over-as-head-coach-of-borussia-moenchengladbach|title=Daniel Farke takes over as head coach of Borussia Mönchengladbach |date=4 June 2022 |website=[[Borussia Mönchengladbach|borussia.de]] |access-date=4 June 2022}}</ref> His first match was a 9–1 win at amateurs [[SV Oberachern]] in the [[DFB-Pokal]] first round;<ref>{{cite news |title=Farkes Sonderlob für Thuram - und für Huber |url=https://www.kicker.de/farkes-sonderlob-fuer-thuram-und-fuer-huber-911410/artikel |access-date=23 October 2022 |work=kicker |date=1 August 2022 |language=German}}</ref> six days later he won 3–1 at home to [[1899 Hoffenheim]] in the first [[Bundesliga]] match of his entire career.<ref>{{cite news |title=Marcus Thuram and Ramy Bensebaini on target as Borussia Mönchengladbach beat 10-man Hoffenheim |url=https://www.bundesliga.com/en/bundesliga/news/borussia-monchengladbach-hoffenheim-live-team-news-match-stats-sommer-thuram-20742 |access-date=23 October 2022 |publisher=Bundesliga |date=7 October 2022}}</ref> Farke was sacked on 2 June 2023 after one season with the team, having finished 10th.<ref name="Farke Aus in Mönchengladbach">{{cite web |title=Farke-Aus in Mönchengladbach - Seoane ist Top-Favorit |url=https://www.kicker.de/farke-aus-in-moenchengladbach-seoane-ist-top-favorit-954330/artikel |website=[[Kicker (magazine)|Kicker]] |publisher=Olympia Verlag |access-date=28 June 2023 |language=German |date=2 June 2023}}</ref> He finished with a record of 12 wins, 10 draws and 14 losses.<ref name="Mönchengladbach record">{{cite web |title=Bor. Mönchengladbach Trainer |url=https://www.kicker.de/bor-moenchengladbach/team-trainer/bundesliga/2022-23 |website=[[Kicker (magazine)|Kicker]] |publisher=Olympia Verlag |access-date=28 June 2023}}</ref>

=== Leeds United ===
[[File:Daniel Farke at Lesds United's championship winners parade 5th May 2025.jpg|thumb|right|183px|Farke at Leeds' Championship title parade in 2025.]]
On 4 July 2023, Farke was appointed as the manager of newly relegated [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] on a four-year contract.<ref>{{cite news |title=Daniel Farke: Leeds United appoint former Norwich City boss |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/66088120 |access-date=13 October 2024 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=4 July 2023}}</ref> His debut on 6 August was a 2–2 draw at home to [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]], having trailed 2–0.<ref>{{cite news |title=Leeds United 2-2 Cardiff City: Late Crysencio Summerville goal rescues point for hosts |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/66352124 |access-date=13 October 2024 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=6 August 2023}}</ref> Having accumulated 90 points in his first season as manager, Leeds missed out on automatic promotion to the Premier League, before going on to lose the [[2024 EFL Championship play-off final|play-off final]] to [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Woodcock |first=Ian |date=26 May 2024 |title=Leeds United 0–1 Southampton |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cd11vdnwwjdt |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> Farke led Leeds to promotion to the [[Premier League]] in the following season, winning the Championship title on [[goal difference]] with 100 points.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Woodcock |first=Ian |date=2025-04-21 |title=Leeds United promoted back to Premier League |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/czjnvzx02v9o |access-date=2025-04-22 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref>
