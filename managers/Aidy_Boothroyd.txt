'''Adrian Neil Boothroyd''' (born 8 February 1971) is an English former [[Association football|footballer]] who was most recently the manager of [[Indian Super League]] club [[Jamshedpur FC|Jamshedpur]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Aidy Boothroyd set to be the new manager of Jamshedpur FC|url=https://www.90min.com/posts/isl-2022-23-jamshedpur-fc-to-appoint-aidy-boothroyd-new-manager|date=10 June 2022|website=90min.com}}</ref>

At club level, he was [[Coach (sport)|manager]] of [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] from 2005 to 2008. During his time with Watford, he was regarded as one of the best young managers in England.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11739/8625463/Aidy-s-aim | title=Aidy's aim | publisher=www1.skysports.com | date=8 April 2013 | access-date=19 July 2013}}</ref> At the time of his departure, he was the youngest and the second longest serving manager in the division.

Boothroyd then had short spells at [[Colchester United F.C.|Colchester United]] and [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]]. In 2011, he replaced [[Gary Johnson (footballer, born 1955)|Gary Johnson]] at [[Northampton Town F.C.|Northampton Town]]. After saving the club from relegation in the 2011–12 season, he took Northampton to the [[EFL League Two|League Two]] Playoff final in May 2013, only to be defeated in the final. After a poor start to the 2013–14 season, he was relieved of his duties on 21 December 2013, with the club bottom of the Football League.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/25479770 | title=Aidy Boothroyd: Northampton Town sack manager | work=BBC News | access-date=22 December 2013}}</ref>

==Playing career==
Boothroyd was born in [[Eccleshill, West Yorkshire|Eccleshill]], [[West Riding of Yorkshire]]. He started as a youth team player at [[Huddersfield Town A.F.C.|Huddersfield Town]] and made his first team debut for the club in 1989. He played 10 league matches before moving on to [[Bristol Rovers F.C.|Bristol Rovers]] in 1990, where he played 16 games. In November 1992, Boothroyd was granted a release by Bristol Rovers, leading to a trial with [[Heart of Midlothian F.C.|Heart of Midlothian]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Brady set for SFA rap|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/12579517.brady-set-for-sfa-rap/|website=The Herald Scotland|date=26 November 1992|access-date=8 May 2025}}</ref> After a successful trial at the Jam Tarts, he signed a 18 month contract with the club.<ref>{{cite news|title=Jess back as Dons prepare for United challenge|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/12576190.jess-back-as-dons-prepare-for-united-challenge/|website=The Herald Scotland|date=12 December 1992|access-date=8 May 2025}}<br>{{cite news|title=Falkirk sign Jefferies for a further four years|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/12577635.falkirk-sign-jefferies-for-a-further-four-years/|website=The Herald Scotland|date=19 December 1992|access-date=8 May 2025}}</ref> Boothroyd scored two goals against [[Huntly F.C.|Huntly]] in a 6–0 win in the [[Scottish Cup]] on 11 January 1993.<ref>{{cite news|title=Adrian bounces back|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/12629830.adrian-bounces-back/|website=The Herald Scotland|date=11 January 1993|access-date=8 May 2025}}<br>{{cite web|title=Anthony Brown: Pulling power of spell in Scotland with Heart of Midlothian, he signed for [[Mansfield Town]], where he played 102 matches, scoring 3 goals in the process.<ref>{{cite news|title=Golac convinced that Rangers will be Boothroyd picked up a career-ending injury after playing 26 games and scoring a single goal. He retired from the game in 1998.

==Coaching career==
After ending his playing career at [[Peterborough United F.C.|Peterborough United]], Boothroyd was appointed coach of the Under 17, Under 19 and reserve teams at the club. His tactical nous and drive impressed, and he left Peterborough in 2001 to join [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]] as their youth team coach under [[Nigel Worthington]]. After two years at [[Carrow Road]], Boothroyd assumed the positions of Youth Development Officer and Technical Director at [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]], joining in October 2003.<ref>{{cite news|date=28 October 2004 |url=http://www.wba.premiumtv.co.uk/page/News/0,,10366~443856,00.html |title=Boothroyd becomes a Baggie |publisher=West Bromwich Albion F.C. |access-date=24 August 2007 }}{{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> A short spell at [[The Hawthorns]] ended, when he was appointed first-team coach at [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] in July 2004,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.wba.premiumtv.co.uk/page/News/0,,10366~539381,00.html |title=Boothroyd set for Leeds role |publisher=West Bromwich Albion F.C. |access-date=24 August 2007 }}{{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

===Watford===
[[File:Aidy Boothroyd.JPG|thumb|upright|Boothroyd as Watford manager, 2005]]
Boothroyd was appointed manager of [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] in March 2005 at the age of 34.<ref>{{cite news|title=Watford appoint Boothroyd as boss|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/w/watford/4389421.stm|work=BBC Sport|date=29 March 2005|access-date=2 November 2007}}</ref> The board backed their decision despite initial scepticism by Watford fans and the club entered a new era under Boothroyd. He managed to steer the club clear of relegation, with 1–0 victories over [[Stoke City F.C.|Stoke City]] and [[Rotherham United F.C.|Rotherham United]].

At the beginning of the [[2005–06 Watford F.C. season|2005–06 season]], Boothroyd said that his aim for the forthcoming campaign would be promotion to the [[Premier League]], although many pundits regarded the club as likely to be relegated. As the season progressed, though, the side gained more and more confidence, and finished in third place to secure a place in the play-offs. After overcoming [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]] in the two-legged semi-final, Watford beat [[Leeds United]] 3–0 in the final to secure promotion to the Premier League. The rapid improvement in Watford's results in his first season in charge led to Boothroyd winning the Championship Manager of the Month in February 2006.<ref>{{cite news|title=Boothroyd earns managerial honour|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/w/watford/4766884.stm|work=BBC Sport|date=2 March 2006|access-date=2 November 2007}}</ref> Boothroyd continued as Watford manager for the duration of the [[2006–07 Watford F.C. season|2006–07 season]], but Watford finished 20th and were relegated. Nevertheless, they reached the [[FA Cup]] semi-finals, and Boothroyd was awarded a new three-year contract.<ref>{{cite news|title=Boothroyd signs new Watford deal|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/w/watford/6636441.stm |work=BBC Sport |date=8 May 2007 |access-date=10 May 2008}}</ref> In July 2006, he completed his UEFA Pro Licence in coaching.<ref name="class2006">{{cite news|title=The Class of 2006|url= http://www.thefa.com/GetIntoFootball/FALearning/NewsAndFeatures/2006/Pro_Licence|date= 3 July 2006|publisher=The Football Association|access-date  = 26 April 2012}}</ref>

===Colchester United===
On 2 September 2009, Boothroyd was announced as the new manager of League One side [[Colchester United]], succeeding [[Paul Lambert]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/c/colchester_united/8233403.stm|title=Boothroyd handed Colchester job |date=2 September 2009|work=BBC Sport|access-date=2 September 2009| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20090902101223/http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/c/colchester_united/8233403.stm| archive-date= 2 September 2009 | url-status= live}}</ref> In his first game as Colchester boss the team drew 0–0 with [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] and the team won five of the first nine games in his charge. The first defeat came in his 10th game in a 2–1 defeat to [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]].

His first signing as Colchester boss was the loan of [[John-Joe O'Toole]] and his first permanent signing was [[Kayode Odejayi]]. O'Toole was subsequently signed on a permanent basis and he also signed [[David Prutton]] from Leeds United, [[Ian Henderson (footballer)|Ian Henderson]] from [[MKE Ankaragücü|Ankaragücü]] and Morten Knudsen from Norway. Boothroyd added another loan signing with [[Franck Queudrue]] arriving on loan from [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]].

Colchester, under Boothroyd, were in the top 6 for over half of the campaign. However, a poor run of form towards the end of the season which saw them win only 2 games of their last 14 games, and this saw them drop just outside the play-offs and finish in 8th position in League One. On 20 May 2010, Boothroyd left the club to take over Championship side [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]].

===Coventry City===
On 20 May 2010, Boothroyd was announced as the new manager of Championship side [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]], succeeding [[Chris Coleman (footballer)|Chris Coleman]].<ref>{{cite news| title = Aidy Boothroyd name Coventry City boss| url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/c/coventry_city/8693222.stm| work = BBC Sport| date = 20 May 2010}}</ref> His first game in charge was on 7 August, in which Boothroyd led Coventry City to an opening day victory over [[Portsmouth F.C.|Portsmouth]] with both goals from [[Freddy Eastwood]].

On 14 March 2011, Boothroyd was sacked by Coventry City after a run of poor results, which saw the club win only once in sixteen league games.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/c/coventry_city/9423992.stm|title=Coventry City sack manager Aidy Boothroyd |date=14 March 2011|work=BBC Sport|access-date=14 March 2011}}</ref>

===Northampton Town===
Boothroyd was appointed as manager of [[Northampton Town F.C.|Northampton Town]] on 30 November 2011 on a one-year, rolling contract.<ref>{{cite web|title=Aidy Boothroyd named Northampton Town boss|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/15959397|work=BBC Sport|access-date=30 November 2011}}</ref> Northampton had slipped to the foot of the [[Football League Two|League Two]] table, and six points from safety, before Boothroyd guided them to a 20th-place finish in the [[2011–12 Northampton Town F.C. season|2011–12 season]]. In the [[2012–13 Northampton Town F.C. season|2012–13 season]], Northampton finished 6th, making the playoffs with 73 points. They beat [[Cheltenham Town F.C.|Cheltenham Town]] 2-0 (on aggregate) in the two-legged semi-finals before losing 3–0 to [[Bradford City F.C.|Bradford City]] in the final at Wembley on 18 May 2013.<ref name="2013 playoff">{{cite web|title=Bradford 3-0 Northampton|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/22491339|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=19 May 2013}}</ref> On 21 December 2013, Boothroyd was sacked by Northampton after a 4–1 defeat by [[Wycombe Wanderers F.C.|Wycombe Wanderers]]. The club were bottom of [[Football League Two|League Two]].<ref name="CobblersOut">{{cite web | url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/25479770 | title=Aidy Boothroyd: Northampton Town sack manager | publisher=BBC Sport | access-date=22 December 2013}}</ref>

===England youth teams===
On 28 February 2014, Boothroyd was appointed manager of the [[England national under-20 football team]],<ref>{{cite web|title=FA appoints Aidy Boothroyd as new national coach|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/england/development/2014/feb/aidy-boothroyd-appointed-as-national-coach-280214#hsI3BowWaoORBIm6.99|publisher=The Football Association|date=28 February 2014|access-date=7 October 2016|first=Nicholas|last=Veevers}}</ref> but was replaced the following year in a reshuffle that saw him coach the [[England national under-19 football team|under-19 side]] instead.<ref>{{cite web|title=Four new interim England national coaches appointed|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/england/development/2015/jul/four-new-interim-coaches-appointed-280715#CqF0W0Zg7TSWGcTI.99|publisher=The Football Association|date=28 July 2016|access-date=2 October 2016}}</ref> He was re-appointed under-20s manager in August 2016, with [[Keith Downing]] taking charge of the under-19s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/england/development/2016/aug/under-20s-clash-with-brazil-set-for-bt-sport-020816#rclrRMfwKVMyUt0y.99|title=England Under-20s' clash with Brazil set for BT Sport coverage|publisher=The Football Association|date=2 August 2016|access-date=2 October 2016}}</ref>

Boothroyd was named caretaker manager of the [[England national under-21 football team]], after [[Gareth Southgate]] was named interim manager of the [[England national football team|senior team]].<ref name="england u21">{{cite web|title=Aidy Boothroyd set to take on England Under-21s position|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/england/under-21/2016/sep/aidy-boothroyd-to-take-u21s-280916|publisher=[[The Football Association]]|date=28 September 2016|access-date=2 October 2016|first=Nicholas|last=Veevers}}</ref> He would manage the side for their remaining [[2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification Group 9|2017 UEFA Euro Championship qualifiers]] against [[Kazakhstan national under-21 football team|Kazakhstan]] and [[Bosnia and Herzegovina national under-21 football team|Bosnia and Herzegovina]].<ref name="england u21"/> His first match in charge of the under-21s ended in a 1–0 win over Kazakhstan,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37538857|title=Kazakhstan U21 0–1 England U21|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=7 October 2016}}</ref> and the second, a 5–0 win over Bosnia and Herzegovina.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37538805|title=England U21 5–0 Bosnia and Herzegovina U21|publisher=BBC Sport|access-date=11 October 2016}}</ref>

On 9 June 2018, Boothroyd managed England to success at the [[2018 Toulon Tournament]], beating [[Mexico national under-20 football team|Mexico]] 2–1 in the final.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=BBC Sport|date=9 June 2018|access-date=9 June 2018|title=England win third straight Toulon Tournament title after victory over Mexico|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/live/football/44396993}}</ref>

On 16 April 2021, Boothroyd announced he would leave his role with [[The Football Association|The FA]] to focus on a return to club football.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=The FA|date=16 April 2021|access-date=18 April 2021|title=AIDY BOOTHROYD TO LEAVE ENGLAND COACHING ROLE|url=https://www.thefa.com/news/2021/apr/16/aidy-boothroyd-to-leave-england-coaching-role-20210416}}</ref>

===Northern Ireland===
On 17 May 2022 it was confirmed that Boothroyd would join [[Northern Ireland national football team|Northern Ireland]]'s coaching staff on a short-term basis due to the absence of [[Adam Sadler]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=BBC Sport|date=17 May 2022|access-date=13 July 2022|title=Aidy Boothroyd: Ex-Watford and Coventry City manager joins Northern Ireland backroom team|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/61487605}}</ref>

===Jamshedpur===
On 10 July 2022, Boothroyd was appointed as head coach of [[Jamshedpur FC|Jamshedpur]]. He said: "Jamshedpur FC are the Champions of India currently and the city has a tremendous football legacy. We want to continue this upward trajectory and make the club reach places and win honours that our fans are dreaming of. We want to take the club to the next level and to compete really with the top teams of Asia."<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Indian Super League|date=10 July 2022|access-date=13 July 2022|title=Jamshedpur FC name Englishman Aidy Boothroyd as their new head coach|url=https://www.indiansuperleague.com/press-releases/jamshedpur-fc-name-englishman-aidy-boothroyd-as-their-new-head-coach}}</ref>

==Personal life==
On 26 March 2025, Boothroyd publicly revealed that he had been diagnosed with [[Parkinson's disease]] in November 2021 during a routine health check.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/cddy14n9ypmo|title='I won't let this define me'- Boothroyd on Parkinson's diagnosis|publisher=BBC Sport|date=26 March 2025|accessdate=26 March 2025}}</ref>
