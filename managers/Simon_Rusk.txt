'''Simon Edward Rusk''' (born 17 December 1981) is an English professional [[manager (association football)|football manager]] and former professional player who most recently was interim head coach at [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]].

He holds a UEFA pro licence. Previously manager of [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. Reserves and Academy|Brighton & Hove Albion Under 23s]], Rusk was responsible for the progression of many young talents to the Brighton first-team in recent years, including [[Aaron Connolly (Irish footballer)|Aaron Connolly]], [[Steven Alzate]], [[Solly March]] and [[Ben White (footballer)|Ben White]].<ref>{{cite news |title=This is why Cambridge United are so determined to land Brighton under-23s manager Simon Rusk |url=https://www.brightonandhoveindependent.co.uk/sport/football/brighton-and-hove-albion/why-cambridge-united-are-so-determined-land-brighton-under-23s-manager-simon-rusk-1385454 |accessdate=15 October 2020 |work=Brighton & Hove Independent |date=5 February 2020}}</ref> As a player, he played as a [[midfielder]], notably for [[Boston United F.C.|Boston United]].

==Coaching career==
===Brighton & Hove Albion U23s===
Rusk was appointed U23s Head coach in March 2015, stepping up from his role as U18s coach.<ref name="Brighton appoint Rusk">{{Cite news | title = Brighton appoint Rusk | url = https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/article/brighton-hove-albion-appoint-under-21s-coach-simon-rusk-2329762.aspx | publisher = Brighton & Hove Albion FC | date = 27 May 2009 | accessdate = 28 January 2020 }}{{Dead link|date=April 2024 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

In February 2016, he was promoted to first-team coach by [[Chris Hughton]] until the end of the season, replacing the outgoing [[Nathan Jones (Welsh footballer)|Nathan Jones]]. In the sixteen remaining games of the season, they lost just once as they sealed third-place in [[2015-16 Football League Championship|The Championship]], missing out on automatic promotion by goal difference.<ref name="Rusk on board">{{Cite news | title = Rusk on board | url = https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/14290391.simon-rusk-on-board-for-promotion-push/ | newspaper = The Argus | location = Brighton | date = 20 February 2016 | accessdate = 28 January 2020 | archive-date = 28 January 2020 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20200128155209/https://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/14290391.simon-rusk-on-board-for-promotion-push/ | url-status = live }}</ref>

In the first year of Premier League 2's current format - he guided the Seagulls to an 8th-placed finish and reached the last sixteen of the EFL Trophy.<ref name="Brighton appoint Rusk"/>

The following year, his side finished third in the league and won promotion to Premier League 2 Division 1 - the highest level for U23s football - for the first time ever, following a 2–0 victory over Aston Villa in the play-offs.<ref name="Brighton earn P2 promotion">{{Cite news | title = Brighton earn P2 promotion | url = https://www.premierleague.com/news/685531 | publisher = Premier League | date = 13 May 2018 | accessdate = 28 January 2020 | archive-date = 28 January 2020 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20200128140738/https://www.premierleague.com/news/685531 | url-status = live }}</ref>

The 2018–2019 season saw a string of impressive results, including a 5–0 win over Manchester City<ref name="Brighton 5-0 Man City">{{Cite news |title = Brighton 5-0 Man City |url = https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/1268962/u23-report-albion-5-manchester-city-0 |publisher = Brighton & Hove Albion |date = 31 August 2018 |accessdate = 28 January 2020 |archive-date = 28 January 2020 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20200128140739/https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/news/1268962/u23-report-albion-5-manchester-city-0 |url-status = live }}</ref>
, as they went unbeaten in their first six games. Rusk's side finished the season in third-place, higher than a number of established academies, including [[Chelsea FC|Chelsea]], [[Liverpool FC|Liverpool]], [[Tottenham Hotspur FC|Tottenham]] and [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]].<ref name="Premier League 2 2018/19 Standings">{{Cite news |title = Premier League 2 2018/19 Standings |url = https://www.oddsportal.com/soccer/england/premier-league-2-2018-2019/standings/bwNKX1w2/ |publisher = Odds Portal |date = 31 August 2018 |accessdate = 28 January 2020 |archive-date = 28 January 2020 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20200128140747/https://www.oddsportal.com/soccer/england/premier-league-2-2018-2019/standings/bwNKX1w2/ |url-status = live }}</ref>

Despite losing a number of key players to the senior squad in 2019–20, the side continued their good form and reached the knockout stages of the EFL Trophy for a second time under Rusk's management.<ref name="Connolly scores brace on debut.">{{Cite news |title = Aaron Connolly |url = https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/teams/u23s-team/players/21653/aaron-connolly |publisher = Brighton & Hove Albion FC |date = 18 October 2019 |accessdate = 28 January 2020 |archive-date = 18 May 2021 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20210518115020/https://www.brightonandhovealbion.com/teams/u23s-team/players/21653/aaron-connolly |url-status = dead }}</ref>

On 27 January 2021, Rusk became the manager of [[National League (division)|National League]] side [[Stockport County F.C.|Stockport County]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.stockportcounty.com/rusk-mcghee-and-conlon-form-new-management-team/ |title=Rusk, McGhee and Conlon form new Management Team |publisher=Stockport County FC |date=27 January 2021 |accessdate=28 January 2021 |language=en-GB |archive-date=27 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210127192732/https://www.stockportcounty.com/rusk-mcghee-and-conlon-form-new-management-team/ |url-status=live }}</ref> On 27 October 2021, the club confirmed they had parted ways with Rusk.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.stockportcounty.com/club-statement-simon-rusk// |title=Club Statement: Simon Rusk |publisher=Stockport County FC |date=27 October 2021 |accessdate=27 October 2021 |language=en-GB |archive-date=27 October 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211027105855/https://www.stockportcounty.com/club-statement-simon-rusk/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Dundee ===
On 17 February 2022, Rusk was announced as assistant manager of [[Scottish Premiership]] side [[Dundee F.C.|Dundee]] under his assistant manager at Stockport, [[Mark McGhee]] until the end of the season.<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 February 2022|title=Mark McGhee appointed manager|url=https://dundeefc.co.uk/news/mark-mcghee-appointed-manager/|access-date=17 February 2022|website=Dundee Football Club - Official Website|language=en-US|archive-date=17 February 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220217203649/https://dundeefc.co.uk/news/mark-mcghee-appointed-manager/|url-status=live}}</ref> After the side was relegated with just one win in the duo's time there, it was confirmed in May 2022 that Rusk would take up a role with the [[English FA]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Cran |first=George |title=Dundee assistant Simon Rusk set to leave Dens Park after landing England youth role |url=https://www.thecourier.co.uk/fp/sport/football/dundee-fc/3295193/dundee-assistant-simon-rusk-to-leave-england/ |access-date=14 May 2022 |website=The Courier |date=13 May 2022 |language=en-GB |archive-date=14 May 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220514062217/https://www.thecourier.co.uk/fp/sport/football/dundee-fc/3295193/dundee-assistant-simon-rusk-to-leave-england/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=14 May 2022 |title=Club Statement {{!}} Mark McGhee |url=https://dundeefc.co.uk/news/club-statement-mark-mcghee/ |access-date=14 May 2022 |website=Dundee Football Club - Official Website |language=en-US |archive-date=14 May 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220514163907/https://dundeefc.co.uk/news/club-statement-mark-mcghee/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

===England===
On 13 May 2022, Rusk was appointed a national coach with [[England national football team|England men's teams]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Ryan and Rusk join as national coaches |url=https://www.englandfootball.com/articles/2022/May/13/neil-ryan-and-simon-rusk-appointed-as-england-football-national-coaches-20221305 |publisher=England Football |date=13 May 2022 |accessdate=16 May 2022 |archive-date=13 May 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220513172306/https://www.englandfootball.com/articles/2022/May/13/neil-ryan-and-simon-rusk-appointed-as-england-football-national-coaches-20221305 |url-status=live }}</ref> On 16 August 2022, it was confirmed that Rusk would take charge of the [[England national under-19 football team|England U19s]].<ref>{{cite web|date=16 August 2022|title=England youth team coaches appointed for 2022-23 season|url=https://www.englandfootball.com/articles/2022/Aug/16/england-mens-development-coaching-line-up-2022-23-season-confirmed-20221508|access-date=16 August 2022|archive-date=16 August 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220816100911/https://www.englandfootball.com/articles/2022/Aug/16/england-mens-development-coaching-line-up-2022-23-season-confirmed-20221508|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Southampton ===
On 1 July 2024, he was announced as head coach of [[Southampton F.C. Under-21s and Academy|Southampton U21s]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=1 July 2024 |title=Simon Rusk appointed U21s Head Coach |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/en/news/article/simon-rusk-appointed-u21s-head-coach |access-date=1 July 2024 |website=Southampton FC}}</ref> Following the sacking of [[Russell Martin (footballer)|Russell Martin]] on 15 December 2024, Rusk was appointed interim manager of [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=15 December 2024 |title=A statement from the board of Southampton FC |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/en/news/article/a-statement-from-the-board-of-southampton-fc |access-date=16 December 2024 |website=Southampton FC}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Rees-Julian |first=George |date=16 December 2024 |title=Who is Southampton interim boss Simon Rusk and what can we expect? |url=https://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/24796428.southampton-interim-boss-simon-rusk-can-expect/ |access-date=16 December 2024 |website=Southern Daily Echo}}</ref> Rusk took charge of two matches for the club before the appointment of new manager [[Ivan Jurić]], a 2–1 home defeat against [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] in the [[EFL Cup]] on 18 December and a 0–0 draw against [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] in the [[Premier League]] on 22 December.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Brotherton |first=Alex |date=18 December 2024 |title=Southampton 1–2 Liverpool |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cx2wjjk2678t |access-date=26 December 2024 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Emons |first=Michael |date=22 December 2024 |title=Fulham 0–0 Southampton |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/clygjz9y4zlt |access-date=26 December 2024 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref> On 24 February 2025, Rusk joined Jurić's backroom staff as a first team coach.<ref>{{Cite web |date=24 February 2025 |title=Rusk joins Men's First Team staff in Academy coaching restructure |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/en/news/article/rusk-joins-mens-first-team-staff-in-academy-coaching-restructure |access-date=24 February 2025 |website=Southampton FC}}</ref> 

Following the departure of Jurić on 7 April, Rusk agreed to become interim manager for the remainder of the season with [[Adam Lallana]] joining him as assistant.<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 April 2025 |title=A statement from the board of Southampton FC |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/en/news/article/a-statement-from-the-board-of-southampton-fc-apr-2025 |access-date=7 April 2025 |website=Southampton FC}}</ref> On 19 April, Rusk led Southampton to a 1–1 draw against West Ham United which saw them reach 11 points, and thus avoid breaking [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]]'s record for the lowest points tally in Premier League history.<ref>{{Cite web |title=West Ham 1–1 Southampton: Lesley Ugochukwu scores stoppage-time equaliser for Saints |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/czrv61j7g1lt |access-date=2025-04-24 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB}}</ref> On 25 June, Southampton confirmed that Rusk had left the club.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 June 2025 |title=New additions boost Still's coaching team |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/en/news/article/new-additions-boost-stills-coaching-team |access-date=25 June 2025 |website=Southampton FC}}</ref>
