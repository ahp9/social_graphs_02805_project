'''Robin Michael Lawrence''', commonly known as '''Lennie Lawrence''', (born 12 December 1947) is an English [[Manager (association football)|football manager]]. He is assistant manager and non-executive director at [[National League (division)|National League]] side [[Hartlepool United F.C.|Hartlepool United]].

Lawrence was a semi-professional at [[Croydon F.C.|Croydon]], [[Carshalton Athletic F.C.|Carshalton Athletic]] and [[Sutton United F.C.|Sutton United]] before becoming caretaker manager of [[Plymouth Argyle F.C.|Plymouth Argyle]] in 1978. He went on to manage [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]], [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]] (during their debut season in the [[Premier League]]), [[Bradford City A.F.C.|Bradford City]], [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] and [[Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town]] and [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_rovers/8677262.stm |title=Bristol Rovers terminate Lennie Lawrence contract |work=BBC Sport |date=12 May 2010 |access-date=12 May 2010}}</ref> He is one of a select few managers to have managed over 1,000 games.<ref name="Profile">{{cite news | title = Lennie Lawrence manager profile | publisher = League Managers Association | url = http://www.leaguemanagers.com/manager/profile-175.html | access-date = 14 December 2007 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20100507122922/http://www.leaguemanagers.com/manager/profile-175.html | archive-date = 7 May 2010 | url-status = dead }}</ref>

Since 2005 Lawrence has worked at a number of clubs as either part of the coaching staff or in a [[Director of football|Director of Football]] role. He joined [[Stevenage F.C.|Stevenage]] in June 2020 as a managerial advisor, to begin at either the restart of the 2019â€“20 season or the start of the 2020â€“21 season,<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|date=1 June 2020|title=Lennie Lawrence: Newport County lose management consultant to struggling Stevenage|language=en-gb|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/52881513|access-date=1 June 2020}}</ref> following three years as the management consultant to [[Newport County F.C.|Newport County]], Lawrence left this role in early November 2020.

==Playing career==
Lawrence turned out at non-league level for [[Croydon F.C.|Croydon]], [[Carshalton Athletic F.C.|Carshalton Athletic]] and [[Sutton United F.C.|Sutton United]] during his semi-professional career.

==Managerial career==
===Plymouth Argyle and Lincoln City===
He came to Plymouth as Assistant Manager to Mike Kelly and when Kelly was sacked he got his first managerial appointment at [[Plymouth Argyle F.C.|Plymouth Argyle]] as caretaker and then worked alongside Malcolm Allison as Assistant. He later worked as a coach at [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]], helping them win promotion from [[Football League Fourth Division|Division Four]] in the [[1980â€“81 in English football|1980â€“81]] season.<ref name="Profile"/>

===Charlton Athletic===
Lawrence moved to [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]] as reserve-team manager, and was later promoted to full-time manager in 1982. appointing Leicester City first team coach Eddie May, as his assistant.<ref name="Profile"/> During his first few years in charge, the club was in a poor financial situation and came close to going out of business in 1984, and even left its home ground, [[The Valley (London)|The Valley]], in 1985. Despite this, Lawrence and May, not only kept Charlton in the [[Football League Second Division|Second Division]] but gained promotion to the [[Football League First Division|First Division]] in 1986, ending Charlton's 29-year absence from the top-flight. With limited resources, he kept Charlton at this level for four years, before being relegated in 1990. He remained in charge for a further season.<ref name="Profile"/>

===Middlesbrough===
Before the start of the [[1991â€“92 in English football|1991â€“92]] season, Lawrence moved on to manage [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]]. Under his stewardship, Middlesbrough finished Second Division runners-up in his first season as manager and gained promotion to the new [[Premier League]] but they struggled in [[1992â€“93 in English football|1992â€“93]] and were relegated at the end of the season.<ref name="Profile"/> Lawrence remained in charge until the end of the following season, his departure being confirmed on 2 May 1994.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football-lawrence-leaves-middlesbrough-1433290.html | location=London | work=The Independent | first=Phil | last=Shaw | date=3 May 1994 | title=Football: Lawrence leaves Middlesbrough}}</ref> Upon leaving he helped young chairman [[Steve Gibson (chairman)|Steve Gibson]] contact his replacement [[Bryan Robson]].<ref>{{cite news| title = Lennie Lawrence (July 1991 to May 1994)| work = MFC| access-date = 6 November 2008| url = http://www.mfc.premiumtv.co.uk/page/ManagersHistoryDetail/0,,1~395742,00.html| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20081006114309/http://www.mfc.premiumtv.co.uk/page/ManagersHistoryDetail/0,,1~395742,00.html| archive-date = 6 October 2008| url-status = dead}}</ref>

===Bradford City and move to Luton Town===
Lawrence joined [[Bradford City A.F.C.|Bradford City]] at the beginning of the [[1994â€“95 in English football|1994â€“95]] season. After a mid-table finish in his first season, Bradford were challenging for promotion in his second season in charge before he left to manage struggling [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] in the division above. Lawrence was unable to save Luton from relegation to Division Two at the end of the [[1995â€“96 in English football|1995â€“96]] season, which saw them replaced by former club Bradford. He remained in charge at [[Kenilworth Road]] until 2000 when he was sacked by the new incoming Luton chairman, Mike Watson Challis,<ref>{{cite news | url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/l/luton_town/819300.stm |title=Lennie leaves Luton |work=BBC Sport |date=4 July 2000 |access-date=3 August 2010}}</ref> and replaced with former Luton fans favourite, Ricky Hill. 

===Grimsby Town===
He made a quick return to management shortly after the beginning of the [[2000â€“01 in English football|2000â€“01]] season with Division One side [[Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town]].<ref name="Profile"/> They avoided relegation and made a reasonable start to the [[2001â€“02 in English football|2001â€“02]] season, and caused an upset in the [[Football League Cup|League Cup]] third round by knocking out holders [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] 2â€“1 after extra time at [[Anfield]], scoring with a [[Phil Jevons]] wonder-goal in the last minute.<ref>{{Cite news| title = Grimsby stun Liverpool| work = BBC Sport| access-date = 14 December 2007| date = 9 October 2001| url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/worthington_cup/1584478.stm}}</ref> However, he was sacked later in the season after Grimsby dropped to the bottom of the table.<ref>{{Cite news| title = Grimsby sack boss Lawrence| work = BBC Sport| access-date = 14 December 2007| date = 28 December 2001| url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_1/1731896.stm}}</ref>

During the opening weeks of the 2001â€“02 season, his Grimsby side had briefly led Division One, sparking hopes that he could repeat the promotion success he achieved at Charlton Athletic and Middlesbrough with a much smaller club. However, these hopes were quickly extinguished as Grimsby's form plunged.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.wsc.co.uk/content/view/1604/29/ | work=When Saturday Comes | title=Grimsby Town 2001 | date=21 July 2005 | access-date=6 October 2010}}</ref>

===Cardiff City===
After a short while out of the game he joined Division Two side [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]] as a consultant. Owner [[Sam Hammam]] had made it clear to manager [[Alan Cork]] that he expected back-to-back promotions, which it did not look like Cork was going to deliver. Eventually Cork was sacked, and Lawrence took over the manager's chair.<ref name="Profile"/> In his first full season in charge, Lawrence took Cardiff to promotion after beating [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] in the 2003 [[Football League Second Division]] play-off final.<ref name="POW03">{{Cite news| title = Cardiff seal promotion| work = BBC Sport| access-date =25 November 2024| date = 25 May 2003| url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_2/3051017.stm}}</ref> He remained at [[Ninian Park]] for a further two years before being replaced in May 2005.<ref>{{Cite news| title = Cardiff boss Lawrence steps down| work = BBC Sport| access-date = 14 December 2007| date = 23 May 2005| url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/c/cardiff_city/4573173.stm}}</ref>

===Hartlepool United===
Following the sacking of [[Darren Sarll]] in October 2024, Lawrence was appointed caretaker manager of [[Hartlepool United F.C|Hartlepool United]]. He was also previously caretaker manager the previous season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hartlepoolunited.co.uk/club-statement-darren-sarll/|title=Club Statement: Darren Sarll|website=www.hartlepoolunited.co.uk|date=16 October 2024|accessdate=16 October 2024}}</ref> On 8 November 2024, he was permanently appointed manager of Hartlepool until the end of the season.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Pools announce new management team |url= https://www.hartlepoolunited.co.uk/pools-announce-new-management-team/ |access-date=2024-11-08 |publisher=Hartlepool United F.C.}}</ref> On 3 February 2025, Lawrence announced that he had stepped back from his role to allow head coach [[Anthony Limbrick]] to take over as manager.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://x.com/BBCTeesSport/status/1886439457903046675 |title=ðŸ”µ BREAKING: Anthony Limbrick is now in charge at Hartlepool United, with Lennie Lawrence taking a step backwards. |website=X |date=3 February 2025 |access-date=3 February 2025}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
After acting as a consultant at Cardiff for a while, he was appointed [[Director of Football]] at Bristol Rovers, working alongside coach [[Paul Trollope]] in a two-tier managerial structure.<ref>{{Cite news | title = Rovers unveil Trollope & Lawrence| work = BBC Sport | access-date = 14 December 2007 | date = 7 November 2005 | url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_rovers/4413544.stm }}</ref> In the pair's first full season in charge, Bristol Rovers won promotion into [[Football League One|League One]] after winning the [[Football League Two|League Two]] play-offs.<ref>{{Cite news | title = Trollope & Lawrence pen new deals | work = BBC Sport | access-date = 14 December 2007 | date = 10 August 2007| url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_rovers/6934860.stm }}</ref> He remained in this position until leaving the club on 12 May 2010, with their League One status still intact.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_rovers/8677262.stm |title=Bristol Rovers terminate Lennie Lawrence contract |date=12 May 2010| work= BBC Sport}}</ref>

In August 2010 Lawrence joined non-league [[Carshalton Athletic F.C.|Carshalton Athletic]] in a football consultancy role, assisting manager Mark Butler. Four months later he was appointed technical director at [[Hereford United F.C.|Hereford United]] in League Two, to assist new manager [[Jamie Pitman]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.herefordtimes.com/sport/thebulls/league_two/news/8743687.Lennie_Lawrence_arrives_as_Jamie_Pitman_confirmed_as_Hereford_United_manager/ |newspaper=Hereford Times |title=Lennie Lawrence arrives as Jamie Pitman confirmed as Hereford United manager |date=16 December 2010 |access-date=17 December 2010}}</ref>

On 20 January 2011, he was appointed assistant manager at [[Crystal Palace F.C.|Crystal Palace]], to support new rookie manager, [[Dougie Freedman]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/c/crystal_palace/9368557.stm | work=BBC Sport | title=Crystal Palace name Lennie Lawrence as assistant | date=20 January 2011 | access-date=20 January 2011}}</ref> In October 2012 Freedman became manager at [[Bolton Wanderers]], and Lawrence was named as a joint-caretaker manager at Crystal Palace with [[Curtis Fleming]], but within a week Lawrence rejoined Freedman at Bolton.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/20154616 | work=BBC Sport | title=Lennie Lawrence & Dougie Freedman reunited at Bolton | date=1 November 2012 | access-date=21 June 2015}}</ref>

Lawrence's first game at Bolton was a victory over former club Cardiff at the [[Reebok Stadium]], in which a [[Martin Petrov]] penalty and a further goal from [[David Ngog]] gave Wanderers a 2â€“1 victory.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/20107686 | work=BBC Sport | title=Bolton 2â€“1 Cardiff | date=3 November 2012 | access-date=21 June 2015}}</ref> At the end of the season, Bolton missed out on a Play Off place to [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]]

On 3 October 2014, he left Bolton along with fellow coach [[Curtis Fleming]] following the sacking of [[Dougie Freedman]] as manager. He later joined Freedman as assistant manager at [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] in early 2015.

During the Summer of 2016 Lawrence became assistant manager to Paul Trollope at Cardiff City. However, a poor start to the 2016/2017 season saw Paul Trollope sacked in the October at which time Lawrence also departed from the club.

==Consultancy roles==
On 21 March 2017, Lawrence joined [[Newport County A.F.C.|Newport County]] as First Team Management Consultant to new manager [[Michael Flynn (footballer)|Mike Flynn]] with Newport 11 points adrift at the bottom of League Two.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39343288 Lawrence joins Newport]</ref> A remarkable turnaround in the remaining 12 league games saw Newport complete the Great Escape and avoid relegation with a win against Notts County with a 89th-minute winner on the final day of the 2016â€“17 season.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39753914 Newport seal League Two status]</ref> Lawrence departed County in June 2020 at the end of the [[2019â€“20 EFL League Two]] season.<ref name=":0" />
Lawrence announced his move to Stevenage in June 2020 and is set to start employment there "either the recommencement of the 2019â€“20 season or the start of the 2020â€“21 season", with the club in the relegation spot at the end of the 2019â€“20 EFL League Two season. He serves as the Managerial Advisor to [[Alex Revell]]. In December 2020, Lawrence left the club. <ref name=":0" />
