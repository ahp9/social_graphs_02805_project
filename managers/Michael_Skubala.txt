'''Michael Skubala''' (born 31 October 1982) is an English [[Association football|football]] coach who is current the head coach for [[EFL League One]] club [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]].

==Coaching career==
===Early career===
Skubala started his coaching career whilst teaching PE at [[Lutterworth College]] in [[Leicestershire]]. Alongside his job at the school, he had a number of coaching roles including at non-league club [[Barwell F.C.|Barwell]] as well as [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]] and [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]] academies.

He subsequently became director of football at [[Loughborough University]], renowned for sporting excellence, before being headhunted by the [[The Football Association|FA]], where he became head coach of [[England national futsal team]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coachesvoice.com/england-futsal-head-coach-talks-pep-guardiola/|title=Pressure cooker|date=23 September 2018 |publisher=coachesvoice.com}}</ref> Between 2017 and 2020, he guided them to their highest world ranking in two decades and played a key role in the development of future Premier League player and Wolves captain [[Max Kilman]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Miller |first=Nick |title=Skubala has trodden an unusual path - it makes him a fascinating coach to watch |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/4212988/2023/02/17/michael-skubala-leeds-futsal/ |access-date=2023-11-12 |website=The Athletic}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/former-leicestershire-school-teacher-takes-8125250/|title=Former Leicestershire school teacher takes charge of Leeds United at Manchester United|publisher=www.leicestermercury.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.thefa.com/news/2017/jan/18/mike-skubala-appointed-new-england-futsal-coach-180117/|title=Mike Skubala appointed as new England Futsal head coach|publisher=www.thefa.com}}</ref> Skubala then progressed to work with the [[England U18]]s, whilst also working at [[UEFA]] as a football advisor.

===Leeds United===
In July 2022, he was appointed head coach of [[Leeds United Academy|Leeds United U21s]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/academy/30143/michael-skubala-appointed-under-21s-head-coach/|title=Michael Skubala appointed U-21's Head Coach|date=28 July 2022 |publisher=www.leedsunited.com}}</ref>

In February 2023, Skubala was appointed interim head coach of English [[Premier League]] side [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]], alongside coaches [[Paco Gallardo]] and [[Chris Armas]], after the sacking of head coach [[Jesse Marsch]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.leedsunited.com/news/team-news/30998/trio-to-take-charge-of-manchester-united-fixture/|title=Trio to take chare of Manchester ===Lincoln City===
On 13 November 2023, he was appointed the head coach of League One club [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.weareimps.com/news/2023/november/skubala-is-citys-new-head-coach/|title=Skubala is City's new head coach|work=Lincoln City FC}}</ref> His first game ended in a 1–0 defeat to [[Stevenage F.C.|Stevenage]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/67388198|title=Stevenage 1–0 Lincoln City |work=BBC Sport}}</ref> His first Lincoln City win came the next game, beating [[Leyton Orient F.C.|Leyton Orient]] 1–0.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/67219335|title=Leyton Orient 0–1 Lincoln City|work=BBC}}</ref> He was nominated for the February 2024 Manager of the Month, picking up 16 points from 6 unbeaten games.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.efl.com/news/2024/march/06/sky-bet-league-one-manager-and-player-of-the-month-nominations/|title=Sky Bet League One Manager and Player of the Month nominations|publisher=EFL|date=5 March 2024|access-date=5 March 2024}}</ref> His unbeaten run would continue into March, taking 13 points from the next 5 games, including a 5–1, 6–0 and 5–0 win, getting him another manager of the month nomination.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.efl.com/news/2024/april/10/sky-bet-league-one--manager---player-of-the-month-nominees/|title= Sky Bet League One: Manager & Player of the Month nominees|publisher=EFL|date=10 April 2024|access-date=10 April 2024}}</ref> Lincoln City missed out on a play-off place following a 2–0 defeat to League One champions [[Portsmouth F.C.|Portsmouth]] on the final day of the season.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/68862403|title=Lincoln City 0-2 Portsmouth |work=BBC Sport}}</ref>

On the eve of the [[2024–25 Lincoln City F.C. season|2024–25 season]], Skubala signed a contract extension until the end of the 2027–28 season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Skubala signs contract extension|url=https://www.weareimps.com/news/skubala-signs-contract-extension|access-date=9 August 2024|publisher=Lincoln City Official Site|date=9 August 2024}}</ref> Three wins from four and the most goals in League One scored, saw him nominated for August manager of the month.<ref name="Sky Bet League One Manager & Player of the Month nominees">{{cite web |title=Sky Bet League One Manager & Player of the Month nominees|url=https://www.efl.com/news/2024/september/11/sky-bet-league-one-manager---player-of-the-month-nominees/|publisher=EFL |access-date=11 September 2024}}</ref> He finished his first full season at the club, finishing 11th with 61 points.<ref>{{cite news|title=Wrexham round off season in style with Lincoln win|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/live/cj45g0pqqe0t|access-date=10 August 2025|publisher=BBC|date=3 May 2025}}</ref>

He started the [[2025–26 Lincoln City F.C. season|2025–26 season]] in fine form - in September he picked up 10 points from his four games, moving the club up to 3rd place - this form saw him win League One manager of the month.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sky Bet League One Manager and Player of the Month nominees|url=https://www.efl.com/news/2025/october/08/sky-bet-league-one-manager-and-player-of-the-month-nominees/|access-date=8 October 2025|publisher=EFL|date=8 October 2024}}</ref><ref name="MOTM1">{{cite web |title=Sky Bet League One: Manager and Player of the Month September Winners|date=10 September 2025 |url=https://www.efl.com/news/2025/october/10/sky-bet-league-one--manager-and-player-of-the-month-september-winners/|publisher=EFL |access-date=10 September 2025}}</ref>
