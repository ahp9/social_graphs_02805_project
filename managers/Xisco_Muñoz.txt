'''Francisco Javier Muñoz Llompart''' (born 5 September 1980), known as '''Xisco''', is a Spanish [[Association football|football]] [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and former player who played mainly as a [[winger (association football)|left winger]]. He is the head coach of [[Malaysia Super League]] club [[Johor Darul Ta'zim F.C.|Johor Darul Ta'zim]].

He played 194 matches and scored 20 goals over nine seasons in [[La Liga]], spending four years at [[Real Betis|Betis]], also representing [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], [[CD Tenerife|Tenerife]], [[Recreativo de Huelva|Recreativo]] and [[Levante UD|Levante]] and winning the [[2004 UEFA Cup final|2004 UEFA Cup]] with Valencia. Starting in 2011, he played four seasons in Georgia with [[FC Dinamo Tbilisi|Dinamo Tbilisi]].

In 2019, Xisco rejoined Dinamo Tbilisi as part of the technical team, and in 2020 became their head coach. In December 2020, he was appointed at [[Watford F.C.|Watford]], achieving promotion to the [[Premier League]] in his first season but being sacked in October 2021 despite the club sitting 15th. That same month, he became manager of [[SD Huesca|Huesca]], before being replaced in June 2022. He signed for [[Anorthosis Famagusta FC|Anorthosis]] in October that year, but left three months later. In July 2023, he joined [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]] as head coach before being dismissed in October after failing to win any matches in charge.

==Playing career==
===Valencia===
Xisco was born in [[Manacor]], [[Balearic Islands]]. He began in the youth ranks of local [[RCD Mallorca|Mallorca]], making ten senior appearances for the [[RCD Mallorca B|B team]] in the [[Segunda División B]] in 1998. After two years with [[Valencia CF|Valencia]]'s [[Valencia CF Mestalla|equivalent]], he moved on loan to [[Recreativo de Huelva]] of [[Segunda División]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://as.com/futbol/2002/07/31/mas_futbol/1028066426_850215.html|title=El Valencia cede a Xisco al Recreativo de Huelva|trans-title=Valencia loan Xisco to Recreativo de Huelva|newspaper=[[Diario AS]]|language=es|date=31 July 2002|access-date=4 January 2021}}</ref> scoring ten goals in his [[2000–01 Segunda División|first season]]. He then joined [[CD Tenerife|Tenerife]] for [[2001–02 La Liga|2001–02]], before returning (always on loan) to his previous club for the [[2002–03 La Liga|2002–03 campaign]], still in [[La Liga]].<ref name=LEVANTE>{{cite web|url=https://www.levanteud.com/es/info/el-levante-ud-cierra-su-plantilla-con-el-fichaje-de-xisco-munoz|title=El Levante UD cierra su plantilla con el fichaje de Xisco Muñoz|trans-title=Levante UD complete their squad with signing of Xisco Muñoz|publisher=Levante UD|language=es|date=1 September 2009|access-date=21 January 2021}}</ref>

Xisco returned to Valencia in summer 2003 for two additional seasons, making 22 appearances as the ''Che'' conquered the [[2003–04 La Liga|2004 league title]]; he scored once as a [[Substitute (association football)|substitute]] on 2 November 2003 to conclude a 5–0 away win against Mallorca.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://elpais.com/diario/2003/11/03/deportes/1067814007_850215.html|first=Cayetano|last=Ros|title=Oliveira se destapa en Son Moix|trans-title=Oliveira runs wild at ''Son Moix''|newspaper=[[El País]]|language=es|date=3 November 2003|access-date=4 January 2021}}</ref> He added eight matches in their [[2003–04 UEFA Cup|victorious run]] in the [[UEFA Europa League|UEFA Cup]] also in that year, but remained on the bench in [[2004 UEFA Cup final|the final]] against [[Olympique de Marseille|Marseille]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/europe/3724215.stm|title=Valencia 2–0 Marseille|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=19 May 2004|access-date=4 January 2021}}</ref>

===Betis===
Xisco signed with [[Real Betis]] in 2005 on a six-year deal,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/025a-0eaa0c5064f6-9a751475cb72-1000--xisco-the-kid-for-betis/|title=Xisco the kid for Betis|publisher=UEFA|date=4 July 2005|access-date=6 March 2013}}</ref> often delivering in various attacking positions and displaying all-around teamwork. He netted three times in [[2006–07 La Liga|2006–07]], including a couple of late goals against [[Racing de Santander]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=203953&cc=5739|title=Real Betis 1–1 Racing Santander|publisher=[[ESPN FC|ESPN Soccernet]]|date=21 January 2007|access-date=6 March 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121026013943/http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=203953&cc=5739|archive-date=26 October 2012}}</ref> and [[Gimnàstic de Tarragona]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=204957&cc=5739|title=Real Betis 1–1 Gimnastic de Tarragona|publisher=ESPN Soccernet|date=19 May 2007|access-date=6 March 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121026013948/http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=204957&cc=5739|archive-date=26 October 2012}}</ref> as the [[Andalusia]]ns only avoided top-flight relegation in the last matchday.

In a rocky [[2008–09 La Liga|2008–09 season]], which ended in relegation,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.diariodesevilla.es/realbetis/plantilla-Betis_0_265173693.html|title=La plantilla del Betis, uno a uno|trans-title=The Betis squad, one by one|newspaper=[[Diario de Sevilla]]|first=Javier|last=Mérida|language=es|date=3 June 2009|access-date=21 January 2021}}</ref> Xisco only featured in nine games, scoring once.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.20minutos.es/deportes/noticia/valladolid-betis-liga-444014/0/|title=El Betis revive de la mano de Sergio García ante un Valladolid impreciso (1–3)|trans-title=Betis come to life courtesy of Sergio García against iffy Valladolid (1–3)|newspaper=[[20 minutos]]|language=es|date=18 January 2009|access-date=21 January 2021}}</ref> Dark spots included being replaced after having himself been used as a substitute in a 2–1 home defeat to [[Málaga CF|Málaga]], on 11 January 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=253063&cc=5739|title=Real Betis 1–2 Málaga|publisher=ESPN Soccernet|date=11 January 2009|access-date=6 March 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120715123334/http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=253063&cc=5739|archive-date=15 July 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://sevilla.eldesmarque.com/real-betis/27394--sp-1544613525|title=Xisco, Babic y Juanlu, apartados; el caso llega a Jabugo|trans-title=Xisco, Babic and Juanlu, ousted; affair reaches Jabugo|publisher=El Desmarque|first=Álvaro|last=Moscoso|language=es|date=12 January 2009|access-date=2 January 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304083146/http://sevilla.eldesmarque.com/real-betis/27394--sp-1544613525|archive-date=4 March 2016}}</ref>

===Levante and later years===
In the dying minutes of the August 2009 [[transfer window]], Xisco rescinded his Betis contract and joined [[Levante UD|Levante]] also in the second division, for two seasons.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/es/news/21/fichajes/2009/09/01/1474420/mercado-el-levante-ficha-al-b%C3%A9tico-xisco|title=Mercado: El Levante ficha al bético Xisco|trans-title=Market: Levante sign ''bético'' Xisco|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|language=es|date=1 September 2009|access-date=2 January 2016}}</ref> In [[2009–10 Segunda División|his first]], he played the most he had in years, also scoring eight goals<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eldesmarque.com/valencia/levante-ud/noticias-2/14631-xisco-munoz-se-retira-y-sera-entrenador-ayudante-en-el-nastic-de-tarragona|title=Xisco Muñoz: se retira el cañón del último ascenso|trans-title=Xisco Muñoz: top gun in last promotion retires|publisher=El Desmarque|language=es|date=17 June 2016|access-date=21 January 2021}}</ref> as the [[Valencian Community]] side returned to the top flight after a two-year absence.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.marca.com/2010/06/13/futbol/2adivision/1276451701.html|title=El Levante celebra el centenario con un ascenso|trans-title=Levante celebrate century with promotion|newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|date=13 June 2010|access-date=2 January 2016}}</ref>

Xisco contributed 26 matches – 18 starts – in [[2010–11 La Liga|2010–11]], as Levante finally managed to stay afloat. On 22 January 2011, he scored his only goal of the campaign in a 4–1 away loss against [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=302183&cc=5739|title=Fabiano treble sinks Levante|publisher=ESPN Soccernet|date=22 January 2011|access-date=6 March 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121026013958/http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=302183&cc=5739|archive-date=26 October 2012}}</ref> and was released in May.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.superdeporte.es/levante/2011/05/26/xisco-munoz-marcha-triste-agradecido/128881.html|title=Xisco Muñoz se marcha "muy triste y agradecido"|trans-title=Xisco Muñoz leaves "with great sadness and appreciation"|newspaper=[[Super Deporte]]|first=Ismael|last=Algarra|language=es|date=26 May 2011|access-date=21 January 2021}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
===Dinamo Tbilisi===
Xisco returned to Dinamo Tbilisi in January 2019, as part of [[Zaur Svanadze]]'s staff.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://es.besoccer.com/noticia/xisco-munoz-regresa-al-dinamo-tiflis-566283|title=Xisco Muñoz regresa al Dinamo Tiflis|trans-title=Xisco Muñoz returns to Dinamo Tbilisi|publisher=Be Soccer|language=es|date=13 January 2019|access-date=1 May 2020}}</ref> He left at the end of the year, after the team had [[2019 Erovnuli Liga|recovered their league crown]] following a three-year absence.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://as.com/futbol/2019/12/20/internacional/1576854995_717246.html|title=Vicente y Xisco dejan el Dinamo Tiflis tras ganar la liga georgiana|trans-title=Vicente and Xisco leave Dinamo Tbilisi after winning the Georgian league|newspaper=Diario AS|language=es|date=20 December 2019|access-date=1 May 2020}}</ref>

In August 2020, Xisco again returned to Dinamo, now as head coach of the club.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://as.com/futbol/2020/08/31/migrantes_del_balon/1598895890_570195.html|title=Xisco Muñoz regresa al Dinamo Tbilisi, su segunda casa|trans-title=Xisco returns to Dinamo Tbilisi, his second home|newspaper=Diario AS|first=Martín|last=Manchón|language=es|date=31 August 2020|access-date=13 September 2020}}</ref> With several compatriots on the pitch and the bench, they [[2020 Erovnuli Liga|won the league]] in December.<ref name=COACH>{{cite news|url=https://www.ultimahora.es/deportes/futbol/2020/12/10/1221711/xisco-munoz-gana-liga-georgiana.html|title=Xisco Muñoz hace campeón al Dinamo de Tiflis|trans-title=Xisco Muñoz makes Dinamo Tbilisi into champions|newspaper=[[Última Hora (Spain)|Última Hora]]|language=es|date=10 December 2020|access-date=4 January 2021}}</ref>

===Watford===
Xisco was appointed as manager of [[Watford F.C.|Watford]] on 21 December 2020, after the dismissal of [[Vladimir Ivić]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.watfordfc.com/news/club/official-xisco-munoz-appointed-head-coach|title=Official: Xisco Muñoz appointed head coach|publisher=Watford F.C.|date=21 December 2020|access-date=21 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11686/12168816/xisco-munoz-watford-appoint-spaniard-as-new-head-coach-following-sacking-of-vladimir-ivic|title=Xisco Munoz: Watford appoint Spaniard as new head coach following sacking of Vladimir Ivic|publisher=[[Sky Sports]]|date=21 December 2020|access-date=11 February 2021}}</ref> His debut in the [[EFL Championship]] was five days later, a 1–0 home victory over leaders [[Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.watfordobserver.co.uk/sport/18971214.xisco-munoz-delighted-watford-win-norwich-city/|title=Xisco Munoz delighted by Watford win over Norwich City|newspaper=[[Watford Observer]]|first=Ryan|last=Gray|date=26 December 2020|access-date=4 January 2021}}</ref>

After a run of five straight wins, Xisco was awarded the [[EFL Championship Manager of the Month|Championship Manager of the Month]] award for March 2021.<ref name=MOTM>{{cite web|url=https://www.watfordfc.com/news/munoz-wins-manager-of-the-month-its-for-everyone|title=Muñoz wins Manager Of The Month: "It's for everyone"|publisher=Watford F.C.|first=Kevin|last=Affleck|date=9 April 2021|access-date=9 April 2021}}</ref> On 24 April, his team [[2020–21 EFL Championship|achieved promotion]] to the [[Premier League]] with two games remaining, after a 1–0 win against [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]] at [[Vicarage Road]].<ref name=EFL>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/56781828|title=Watford 1–0 Millwall|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Rob|last=Stevens|date=24 April 2021|access-date=25 April 2021}}</ref>

Xisco was sacked on 3 October 2021, with the side being 15th after seven matches; it was the 13th change in the club's hotseat since the arrival of owner [[Gino Pozzo]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/58778752|title=Xisco Munoz: Watford sack manager after poor spell|publisher=BBC Sport|date=3 October 2021|access-date=3 October 2021}}</ref> In an official statement, Watford wrote that the coach had been presiding over a "negative trend at a time when team cohesion should be visibly improving".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.watfordfc.com/news/club-statement-xisco-munoz|title=Club statement: Xisco Muñoz|publisher=Watford F.C.|date=3 October 2021|access-date=3 October 2021}}</ref>

===Huesca===
On 26 October 2021, Xisco signed as coach of [[SD Huesca|Huesca]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.superdeporte.es/futbol/2021/10/26/xisco-munoz-nuevo-entrenador-huesca-58838880.html|title=Xisco Muñoz, nuevo entrenador del Huesca|trans-title=Xisco Muñoz, new Huesca manager|newspaper=Super Deporte|first=Javier|last=Bengoa|language=es|date=26 October 2021|access-date=17 November 2021}}</ref> Having finished in 13th in his [[2021–22 Segunda División|only season]] with nine wins out of 30,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.heraldo.es/noticias/deportes/futbol/sd-huesca/2022/05/29/xisco-sd-huesca-no-objetivo-disculpas-reflexionar-1577966.html|title=Xisco Muñoz: "No se ha logrado el objetivo, hay que pedir disculpas y reflexionar"|trans-title=Xisco Muñoz: "The objective hasn't been achieved, we have to apologise and reflect"|newspaper=[[Heraldo de Aragón]]|first=Miguel|last=Barluenga|language=es|date=29 May 2022|access-date=16 June 2022}}</ref> he was replaced in June by [[José Ángel Ziganda]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.heraldo.es/noticias/deportes/futbol/sd-huesca/2022/06/13/cuco-ziganda-sd-huesca-si-quiero-1581272.html|title=Cuco Ziganda da el "sí, quiero" a la SD Huesca|trans-title=Cuco Ziganda gives the "yes, I do" to SD Huesca"|newspaper=Heraldo de Aragón|first=Miguel|last=Barluenga|language=es|date=13 June 2022|access-date=16 June 2022}}</ref>

===Anorthosis===
On 4 October 2022, Xisco was appointed manager of [[Cypriot First Division]] club [[Anorthosis Famagusta FC|Anorthosis]] until the end of [[2022–23 Cypriot First Division|the season]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.watfordobserver.co.uk/sport/23017661.ex-watford-boss-munoz-takes-anorthosis-famagusta-cyprus/|title=Ex-Watford boss Munoz takes over at Anorthosis Famagusta in Cyprus|newspaper=Watford Observer|first=Anthony|last=Matthews|date=4 October 2022|access-date=4 October 2022}}</ref> Three months later, after a series of five winless games, he was replaced by [[Vesko Mihajlović]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://protathlima.cyprustimes.com/podosfairo-kyprou/a-katigoria/anorthosi/me-vesko-kontra-se-aek/|title=Με Βέσκο κόντρα σε ΑΕΚ|trans-title=With Vesko against AEK|publisher=Protathlima|language=el|date=5 January 2023|access-date=6 March 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://huelva24.com/art/168308/gorka-santamaria-ya-tiene-nuevo-equipo-y-xisco-munoz-es-cesado-en-chipre|title=Gorka Santamaría ya tiene nuevo equipo y Xisco Muñoz es cesado en Chipre|trans-title=Gorka Santamaría already has a new team and Xisco Muñoz is sacked in Cyprus|publisher=Huelva 24|language=es|date=13 January 2023|access-date=6 March 2023}}</ref>

===Sheffield Wednesday===
On 4 July 2023, Xisco joined [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]] as head coach;<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.swfc.co.uk/news/2023/july/owls-appoint-xisco-munoz-as-first-team-manager/|title=Owls appoint Xisco Muñoz as first team manager|publisher=Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|date=4 July 2023|access-date=4 July 2023}}</ref> he brought in his brother Miguel as assistant, Roberto Cuesta as first-team coach and Antonello Brambilla as goalkeeper coach, all of whom he had worked with at Watford.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.swfc.co.uk/news/2023/july/xisco-adds-to-coaching-team/|title=Xisco adds to coaching team|publisher=Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|date=14 July 2023|access-date=14 July 2023}}</ref> His first competitive game in charge was at home to newly relegated [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]], a 2–1 defeat,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.examinerlive.co.uk/sport/football/news/highlights-sheffield-wednesday-southampton-updates-27459346|title=Sheffield Wednesday 1 Southampton 2 highlights as ex-Blades striker Che Adams bags later winner|newspaper=Yorkshire Examiner|first=Ricky|last=Charlesworth|date=4 August 2023|access-date=6 August 2023}}</ref> and culminated in a 1–0 away loss to [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] on 3 October. He had led the Owls to their worst ever start to a season, with just two points from the first ten matches.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.yorkshirepost.co.uk/sport/football/sheffield-wednesday/xisco-munoz-has-now-led-sheffield-wednesday-to-worst-start-in-their-history-after-loss-to-west-brom-4358615|title=Xisco Munoz has now led Sheffield Wednesday to worst start in their history after loss to West Brom|newspaper=[[The Yorkshire Post]]|date=3 October 2023|access-date=4 October 2023}}</ref> A day later, he was dismissed.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.swfc.co.uk/news/2023/october/club-statement/|title=Club statement|publisher=Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|date=4 October 2023|access-date=4 October 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/66852888|title=Xisco Munoz: Sheffield Wednesday sack boss after winless start in Championship|publisher=BBC Sport|date=4 October 2023|access-date=5 October 2023}}</ref>

===DAC Dunajská Streda===
On 19 November 2023, Xisco signed as manager of [[FC DAC 1904 Dunajská Streda|DAC Dunajská Streda]] until the end of [[2023–24 Slovak First Football League|the campaign]], with the option of another year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://fcdac.sk/sk/clanek.asp?id=Novym-trenerom-DAC-u-Xisco-Munoz-9622|title=Novým trénerom DAC-u Xisco Muňoz|trans-title=Xisco Muñoz is the new manager of DAC|publisher=DAC Dunajská Streda|first=Nagy|last=Krisztián|language=sk|date=19 November 2023|access-date=1 December 2023|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250123210350/https://fcdac.sk/sk/clanek.asp?id=Novym-trenerom-DAC-u-Xisco-Munoz-9622|archive-date=23 January 2025}}</ref> He was relieved of his duties on 27 November 2024.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://fcdac.sk//sk/clanek.asp?id=Xisco-Munoz-konci-na-lavicke-DAC-u-10723|title=Xisco Muňoz končí na lavičke DAC-u|trans-title=Xisco Muňoz ends up on the DAC bench|publisher=DAC Dunajská Streda|first=Nagy|last=Krisztián|language=sk|date=27 November 2024|access-date=29 November 2024|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20241207133349/https://fcdac.sk/sk/clanek.asp?id=Xisco-Munoz-konci-na-lavicke-DAC-u-10723|archive-date=7 December 2024}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Xisco is the older brother of another footballer, [[Toni Muñoz (footballer, born 1982)|Toni Muñoz]]. He appeared once for Mallorca's first team, but spent the vast majority of his career in the lower leagues of his country.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://archivo.marca.com/edicion/marca/futbol/1a_division/murcia/es/desarrollo/1027157.html|title=El Murcia derrota al Larissa en la presentación ante su público|trans-title=Murcia defeat Larissa in presentation to home crowd|newspaper=Marca|language=es|date=18 August 2007|access-date=6 March 2013}}</ref>
