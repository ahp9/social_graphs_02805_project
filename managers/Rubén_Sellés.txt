'''Rubén Sellés Salvador''' (born 15 June 1983) is a Spanish professional [[Manager (association football)|football manager]] who is the manager of [[Segunda División]] club [[Real Zaragoza]].

Sellés has also managed [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] in the [[Premier League]] and [[Reading F.C.|Reading]]. With coaching experience in [[Greece national football team|Greece]], [[Russia national football team|Russia]], [[Azerbaijan national football team|Azerbaijan]], [[Denmark national football team|Denmark]], [[Spain national football team|Spain]] and [[England national football team|England]], Sellés has a [[Master's degree]] in [[Sport|Sports]] & [[Physiology]] from the [[University of Valencia]], and graduated from UEFA's [[UEFA Pro Licence|Pro Licence]] programme aged 25.

==Coaching career==
Born in Marxalenes, a neighborhood in [[Valencia]], Sellés played for local side Parreta CF before finishing his playing career with [[Burjassot CF|Burjassot]].<ref name=GM>{{cite web|url=https://golsmedia.com/comunidad-valenciana/valencianos-por-el-mundo/2019/10/28/valencianos-por-mundo-ruben-selles-dinamarca-marca-doble-goles-balon-parado/|title=VALENCIANOS POR EL MUNDO {{!}} Rubén Sellés: "El fútbol danés es muy atractivo en muchos aspectos"|trans-title=VALENCIANS THROUGH THE WORLD {{!}} Rubén Sellés: "The Danish football is very attractive in a lot of aspects"|publisher=Golsmedia|language=es|date=28 October 2019|accessdate=25 August 2023}}</ref> At the age of 16, he was already coaching the youth sides of Parreta.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/en/news/article/feature-interview-ruben-selles1|title=Feature Interview: Rubén Sellés|publisher=Southampton FC|date=23 March 2023|accessdate=25 August 2023}}</ref> In 2002, Sellés entered the [[University of Valencia]] and became a coach of the university's football team; he led the club to a promotion from the [[Segunda Regional de la Comunidad Valenciana|Segunda Regional]] in their first senior season, and kept the club in the [[Primera Regional de la Comunidad Valenciana|Primera Regional]] in the following campaigns.<ref>{{cite web |date=6 December 2024 |title=The Story So Far: Rubén Sellés |url=https://www.wearehullcity.co.uk/news/2024/december/06/the-story-so-far--ruben-selles/ |access-date=31 August 2025 |publisher=Hull City AFC}}</ref><ref name=GM/>

At the start of the 2008–09 season, Sellés joined a host of compatriots at Greek side [[Aris Thessaloniki F.C.|Aris Thessaloniki]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11700/12824497/ruben-selles-interview-southampton-manager-on-how-scandinavian-culture-sculpted-his-house-on-the-south-coast|title=Ruben Selles interview: Southampton manager on how Scandinavian culture sculpted his house on the south coast|publisher=[[Sky Sports]]|date=4 March 2023|accessdate=25 August 2023}}</ref> being an assistant fitness coach.<ref name=GM/> He departed in 2009 to perform a similar role for the [[Villarreal CF (youth)|''Juvenil'']] squad of [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Dawes |first=Olly |date=8 November 2022 |title=Report: Some agents expected Spaniard to quickly become Southampton manager |url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2022/11/08/southampton-ruben-selles-ralph-hasenhuttl/ |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=HITC }}</ref>

In 2010, Sellés travelled to Russia, acting as assistant manager to [[Igor Lediakhov]] at [[FC Shinnik Yaroslavl|Shinnik Yaroslavl]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Tanswell |first=Jacob |date=4 August 2022 |title=Ruben Selles: Hasenhuttl's new No 2 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/3449192/2022/08/04/ruben-selles-southampton-ralph-hasenhuttl/ |url-access=subscription |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=The Athletic }}</ref> He later worked as [[Óscar Fernández (football manager, born 1974)|Óscar Fernández]]'s assistant at [[Asteras Tripolis F.C.|Asteras Tripolis]] and [[CF Gandía|Gandía]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eldesmarque.com/opinion/el-todocampista/20150401/tecnicos-valencianos-tu-a-drammen-y-yo-a-doha_170003233.html|title=Técnicos valencianos: Tú a Drammen y yo a Doha|trans-title=Valencian managers: You to Drammen and I to Doha|publisher=El Desmarque|language=es|date=1 April 2015|accessdate=25 August 2023}}</ref> before joining [[Neftçi PFK|Neftçi]]; he remained an assistant manager there for two seasons, under managers [[Boyukagha Hajiyev]], [[Arif Asadov]], [[Nazim Suleymanov]] and [[Tarlan Ahmadov]].

Sellés moved to Norwegian club [[Strømsgodset Toppfotball|Strømsgodset]] in 2015, working as Chief Data Analyst,<ref name=":1" /> before returning to Azerbaijan as an assistant of [[Gurban Gurbanov]] at [[Qarabağ FK|Qarabağ]].<ref>{{cite web |last=House |first=Alfie |date=13 June 2022 |title=New assistant Selles opens up on what to expect in Hasenhuttl pairing |url=https://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/20206106.southampton-assistant-selles-expect-hasenhuttl-pairing/ |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=Southern Daily Echo }}</ref> In July 2018, he joined Danish club [[Aarhus Gymnastikforening|Aarhus]], assisting manager [[David Nielsen]] for two seasons.<ref name=":1"/><ref>{{cite web |last=House |first=Alfie |date=14 June 2022 |title=Saints academy starlets can take confidence in 'love' of new coach |url=https://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/20206599.southampton-academy-can-take-confidence-love-new-coach/ |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=Southern Daily Echo }}</ref>

In July 2020, Sellés returned to his hometown after being appointed manager of [[Valencia CF|Valencia]]'s [[Valencia CF (youth)|''Juvenil B'']] squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.superdeporte.es/valencia-cf/2020/07/02/entrenador-fichado-academia-valencia-53805221.html|title=El entrenador que ha fichado la Academia del Valencia|trans-title=The manager who was signed by the Valencia Academy|publisher=[[Super Deporte]]|language=es|date=2 July 2020|accessdate=25 August 2023}}</ref> On 1 January 2021, he returned to Denmark and joined [[F.C. Copenhagen|Copenhagen]] as assistant manager to [[Jess Thorup]].<ref name=":1">{{cite web |title=Rubén Sellés becomes new assistant coach |url=https://www.fck.dk/en/news/ruben-selles-becomes-new-assistant-coach |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=F.C. København }}</ref>

==Managerial career==

===Southampton===
On 10 June 2022, Sellés departed Copenhagen to join [[Premier League]] club [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]], as assistant to [[Ralph Hasenhüttl]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web |date=10 June 2022 |title=Sellés joins as First Team Lead Coach |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/news/2022-06-10/announcement-ruben-selles-first-team-lead-coach-southampton-football-club-10-june-2022 |access-date=10 November 2022 |publisher=Southampton FC }}</ref> On 7 November, Hasenhüttl was sacked by Southampton, and Sellés was placed in caretaker charge of the club.<ref>{{cite web |date=7 November 2022 |title=Club statement: Ralph Hasenhüttl |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/news/2022-11-07/southampton-football-club-part-company-with-ralph-hasenhuttl-statement |access-date=10 November 2022 |publisher=Southampton FC}}</ref> Sellés took charge of one match for ''The Saints'' before the appointment of new manager [[Nathan Jones (Welsh footballer)|Nathan Jones]], a 1–1 draw against [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]] in the [[EFL Cup]] on 9 November – in which Southampton advanced to the next round after a penalty shootout.<ref>{{cite web |last=Wyatt |first=Mark |date=10 November 2022 |title=Every word Ruben Selles said on EFL Cup win, Ralph Hasenhuttl and Nathan Jones |url=https://www.hampshirelive.news/sport/football/football-news/ruben-selles-hasenhuttl-nathan-jones-7804242 |access-date=10 November 2022 |website=Hampshire Live}}</ref> 

He again took temporary charge of the team after Jones was sacked in February 2023.<ref>{{cite web |date=12 February 2023 |title=Club Statement: Nathan Jones |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/news/2023-02-12/southampton-football-club-nathan-jones-part-company-statement |access-date=12 February 2023 |publisher=Southampton FC}}</ref> His first game in charge was a 1–0 away victory over [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Howarth |first=Matthew |title=Chelsea 0–1 Southampton |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/64607244 |date=18 February 2023 }}</ref> On 24 February, Sellés was appointed manager until the end of the [[2022–23 Southampton F.C. season|2022–23 season]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Southampton appoint Selles until end of season |work=BBC Sport |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/64755067 |access-date=24 February 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Sellés appointed to end of season |url=https://www.southamptonfc.com/news/2023-02-24/ruben-selles-mens-first-team-manager-announcement |publisher=Southampton FC |access-date=24 February 2023 |date=24 February 2023}}</ref> On 24 May, Southampton confirmed that they would not renew his contract when it expired at the end of the season.<ref>{{cite web |last=House |first=Alfie |date=24 May 2023 |title=Southampton confirm they will not renew Ruben Selles manager contract |url=https://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/23544114.southampton-confirm-will-not-renew-ruben-selles-manager-contract/ |access-date=24 May 2023 |website=Southern Daily Echo}}</ref> On 28 May, Sellés managed his final game at Southampton; a 4–4 draw against [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] on the final day of the league season.<ref>{{cite web |title=Liverpool FC — Jürgen Klopp assesses Southampton 4–4 Liverpool and season overall |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/jurgen-klopp-southampton-4-4-liverpool-we-have-basis-what-we-have-do |access-date=29 May 2023 |website=www.liverpoolfc.com }}</ref> In his three months in charge, he oversaw the relegation of Southampton to the [[EFL Championship]], having won just two games in 17 matches.<ref>{{cite web |title=Rubén Sellés Southampton Manager Profile, Record & Stats {{!}} Premier League |url=https://www.premierleague.com/managers/131382/Rub%C3%A9n-Sell%C3%A9s/overview |access-date=29 May 2023 |website=www.premierleague.com }}</ref>

===Reading===
On 26 June 2023, [[Reading F.C.|Reading]] announced an agreement with Sellés to become their new first team manager, subject to a successful work visa application;<ref>{{cite web |title=RUBÉN SELLÉS SET TO SIGN SUBJECT TO WORK VISA CLEARANCE |url=https://www.readingfc.co.uk/news/2023/june/26/rub-n-sell-s-set-to-sign-subject-to-work-visa-clearance/ |website=readingfc.co.uk |publisher=Reading F.C. |access-date=26 June 2023 |date=26 June 2023}}</ref> his appointment was confirmed on 14 July.<ref>{{cite web |title=RUBÉN IS A ROYAL – SELLÉS SIGNS TO COMPLETE MOVE TO RG2 |url=https://www.readingfc.co.uk/news/2023/july/14/ruben-selles-completes-move-to-reading-fc/ |website=readingfc.co.uk/ |publisher=Reading F.C. |access-date=14 July 2023 |date=14 July 2023}}</ref> His first professional game in charge was a 0–1 defeat to [[Peterborough United F.C.|Peterborough]]; his first victory came in the following match when Reading beat [[Millwall F.C.|Millwall]] 0–4 away in the [[EFL Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |date=5 August 2023 |title=Reading 0–1 Peterborough |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/66345516 |access-date=10 October 2023 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=8 August 2023 |title=Millwall 0–4 Reading |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/66368512 |access-date=10 October 2023 |website=BBC Sport}}</ref>

=== Hull City ===
On 6 December 2024, Sellés was announced as the new head coach of [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] on a two-and-a-half year contract, with the option for a third, in the place of the dismissed [[Tim Walter]]. In his first match in charge, he drew 1–1 to [[Watford F.C.|Watford]], before going on to seal a 2–1 victory over [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] on home turf, gaining the Tigers their first win in 14 matches. In their first [[FA Cup]] match under his leadership, they lost 5–4 on penalties, after a 1–1 draw to [[Doncaster Rovers F.C.|Doncaster Rovers]], exiting in the competition’s third round. On 15 May 2025, despite getting Hull City to safety, he was dismissed from his role.<ref>{{cite news |date=15 May 2025 |title=Hull City sack third manager in 12 months as Ruben Selles departs |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/ruben-selles-sacked-hull-new-manager-b2751517.html |access-date=15 May 2025 |website=The Independent }}</ref>

===Sheffield United===
On 18 June 2025, Sellés was announced as the new manager of [[Sheffield United F.C.|Sheffield United]] on a three-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |date=18 June 2025 |title=Rubén is our man |url=https://www.sufc.co.uk/news/2025/june/18/ruben-is-our-man/ |access-date=19 June 2025 |publisher=Sheffield United FC}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=18 June 2025 |title=Sheffield United: Ruben Selles replaces Chris Wilder as manager |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11683/13385636/sheffield-united-ruben-selles-replaces-chris-wilder-as-manager |access-date=19 June 2025 |website=Sky Sports}}</ref> The team made a poor start to the season, losing all of their opening five league matches of the season, a run which included a 5–0 away defeat to winless [[Ipswich Town F.C.|Ipswich Town]].<ref>https://www.skysports.com/football/ipswich-vs-sheff-utd/report/534369</ref> On 14 September, the club announced they had sacked Sellés.<ref>{{cite web |title=Sheffield United sack Ruben Selles as head coach after just three months in charge |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11683/13429712/sheffield-united-sack-ruben-selles-as-head-coach-after-just-three-months-in-charge |access-date=14 September 2025 |website=Sky Sports }}</ref><ref>https://www.sufc.co.uk/news/2025/september/14/club-statement/</ref>

=== Zaragoza ===
On 20 October 2025, Sellés was appointed manager of [[Real Zaragoza]], who were at the bottom of the [[Segunda División]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Prince |first=Toby |date=2025-10-20 |title=Ruben Selles appointed Zaragoza coach after turbulent English stint |url=https://tribuna.com/en/news/2025-10-20-ruben-selles-appointed-zaragoza-coach-after-turbulent-english-stint/ |access-date=2025-10-21 |website=Tribuna.com}}</ref>
