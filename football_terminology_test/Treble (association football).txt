A '''treble''' in [[association football]] is achieved when a club team wins three trophies in a single season.<ref name="GOAL">{{cite news |author=GOAL |title=Which Football Teams Have Won the Treble? |work=[[Goal (website)|Goal]] |publication-place=[[London]] |date=10 June 2023 |url=https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/which-football-teams-have-won-treble/1sk4fna3n446j1c7d5za94o5sq |access-date=8 June 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240608124521if_/https://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/which-football-teams-have-won-treble/1sk4fna3n446j1c7d5za94o5sq |archive-date=Jun 8, 2024 |url-status=live}}</ref> A '''continental treble''' involves winning the club's top-level domestic league competition, main domestic cup competition, and main continental trophy.<ref name="GOAL"/><ref>{{cite news |title=Raja Aiming High Once More |work=FIFA.com |publisher=[[FIFA]] |date=4 June 2008 |url=https://www.fifa.com/en/classicfootball/news/newsid=792626.html |access-date=June 8, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080925083527if_/http://www.fifa.com/classicfootball/news/newsid=792626.html |archive-date=Sep 25, 2008 |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="TSN">{{cite news |last=Bonn |first=Kyle |date=10 June 2023 |title=What is the treble? Explaining the trophy haul that makes it up as Man City crowned European champions |url=https://www.sportingnews.com/in/football/news/what-treble-explaining-trophy-haul-league-continent/ax6lbojjqpd6xzlhixnvcurh |work=[[The Sporting News]] |access-date=25 June 2023}}</ref> Although winning a second-tier continental trophy (e.g. [[UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]) has also been described as a continental treble, it is not as widely accepted.<ref name="GOAL"/> A '''domestic treble''' involves winning three national competitions—including the league title, the primary cup competition, and one secondary competition, such as a [[League cup#List of league cups and secondary cups in association football|league cup]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Spiers |first=Graham |title=Walter Smith Demands Final Push as Rangers Target Domestic Treble |work=The Times |date=30 January 2008 |url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/sport/football/scotland/article3274019.ece |access-date=28 March 2009 }}{{dead link|date=September 2024|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Ralston |first=Gary |title=Domestic Treble for Rangers Will Make up for Failure in Europe, Says Pedro Mendes |work=[[Daily Record (Scotland)|Daily Record]] |department=Sport |publisher=Scottish Daily Record and Sunday Mail |date=1 Jul 2012 |orig-date=First released 26 Sep 2008 |url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/other-sports/domestic-treble-for-rangers-will-make-up-991772 |access-date=June 8, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210623092216if_/http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/sport/other-sports/domestic-treble-for-rangers-will-make-up-991772 |archive-date=Jun 23, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Can Rewa win a domestic treble this year in Fiji? |date=3 October 2020 |url=https://oceaniafootballcenter.org/2020/10/03/can-rewa-win-a-domestic-treble-this-year-in-fiji/ |publisher=Oceania Football Center |access-date=5 June 2024}}</ref>

Competitions which consist of a single match or a two-leg match are not normally counted as part of a continental treble (e.g., the [[FA Community Shield]], [[Supercopa de España]], [[Supercoppa Italiana]], [[Trophée des Champions]], [[DFL-Supercup]], [[UEFA Super Cup]], [[Recopa Sudamericana]], [[FIFA Club World Cup]] (2005–2023 editions), [[Intercontinental Cup (1960–2004)|Intercontinental Cup]] and others).<ref name="GOAL" /><ref name="doublerec">{{cite news |last1=Stokkermans |first1=Karel |url=https://www.rsssf.org/miscellaneous/doublerec.html |title=Doing the Double! |publisher=[[RSSSF]] |access-date=19 August 2020}}</ref>

==Continental trebles==
This list includes clubs who have won their country's top-tier league and the primary cup competition ([[Double (association football)|the double]]), in addition to the major continental tournament, all within a single season. [[Tokyo Verdy]] of Japan and [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] of Spain are the only clubs to win continental trebles for both their men's and women's sections, completing the feat in 2019 and 2021, respectively.<ref name="Nippon TV" /><ref name=":0" /> Barcelona is the only club to win multiple trebles with both their men's and women's team, completing the feat in 2024.

===Men===
Twenty-three men's clubs have achieved the feat with 31 occurring in total, the first being [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] of Scotland in May 1967, and then [[TP Mazembe]] (then known as FC Englebert) of the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo]] in November 1967.<ref name="UEFA">{{cite news |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/0251-0e99b1ba85da-ec8053dc0a29-1000--who-s-won-the-treble/ |title=Who has won a treble, including domestic league and cup titles, plus the European Cup or UEFA Champions League? |orig-date=First published in 23 August 2020 |access-date=23 August 2020 |date=10 June 2023 |publisher=[[UEFA]]|location=[[Nyon]]}}</ref><ref name=":1" /> European teams have won the most continental trebles with eleven, by nine clubs.<ref name="TSN"/> The only countries to have more than one team win a continental treble are Mexico, New Zealand, England and the Netherlands, with two teams winning trebles each.<ref name="TSN"/>

No South American team has achieved the feat with the above stipulations, making it the only continent to never have a continental treble winner.<ref name="TSN"/> Multiple Brazilian teams have won some combination of the [[Campeonato Brasileiro Série A]], their [[State football leagues in Brazil|state championship]], the [[Copa do Brasil]], and the now-defunct [[Taça Brasil]], plus the [[Copa Libertadores]], but never by winning the Série A, Copa do Brasil, and Copa Libertadores.{{Efn|name=SAmerica}}

Five men's clubs have won the continental treble two or more times: [[Al Ahly SC|Al Ahly]] of Egypt, [[Auckland City FC|Auckland City]] of New Zealand, [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] of Spain, [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] of Germany and [[Cruz Azul]] of Mexico.<ref name=":2" /><ref name="Auckland" /><ref>{{Cite press release |url=http://www.fcbarcelona.com/football/first-team/detail/article/barca-make-history-with-second-treble |title=Barça make history with second treble! |publisher=[[FC Barcelona]]|location=[[Barcelona]]|access-date=2 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150607120500/http://www.fcbarcelona.com/football/first-team/detail/article/barca-make-history-with-second-treble|archive-date=7 June 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="UEFA" /><ref name="Cruz" /> with Cruz Azul being the first team to achieve it in 1997.<ref name="Cruz" /> Al Ahly and Auckland City are the only teams with more than two continental trebles, with both having won four.<ref name=":2" /><ref name="Auckland" /> Auckland City completed this achievement first, winning their third treble in 2015 and fourth in 2022.<ref name="Auckland" /> Al Ahly and Auckland City are also the only men's clubs to win trebles in back-to-back seasons, though Al Ahly were the first to do this after winning in both 2006 and 2007.<ref name=":2" />

===Women===
Ten women's clubs have won a continental treble with these stipulations, five of which are European clubs, one Asian, one African, one South American and two Oceanian club, with a total of 16 trebles across all the clubs. The first to do so was [[Eintracht Frankfurt (women)|1. FFC Frankfurt]] in 2002, who won the treble again in 2008.<ref name=":3" /> The only other women's club to achieve it more than once are [[Olympique Lyonnais Féminin|Lyon]], who has done so five times (a record for both men's and women's clubs) and [[FC Barcelona Femení|Barcelona]] and [[Sport Club Corinthians Paulista (women)|Corinthians]], who have achieved it twice each. Lyon are also the only women's club to win trebles in back to back years, and have completed this feat on two occasions.<ref name="Palmarès" />

The lack of women's continental trebles compared to men's can largely be attributed to the absence of continental competitions for women until recently. ==Domestic trebles==
A domestic treble is usually made up of the league, main domestic cup and the most prestigious secondary domestic cup (usually being a [[league cup]], like the [[EFL Cup]], [[Scottish League Cup]], or the [[Thai League Cup]]).<ref name="doublerec" /> Usually [[Super cup|super cups]] or regional titles, like the [[State football leagues in Brazil|Brazilian states championships]], [[Biscay Championship]] and [[Glasgow Cup]], are not included in the treble,<ref>{{cite web |date=May 18, 2023 |title=What is a treble in football: Meaning, types, and more as Manchester CIty inch closer to history |url=https://www.foottheball.com/explainer/treble-football-meaning-types-history/ |publisher=foottheball.com}}</ref> therefore domestic trebles are only possible in some countries, as most federations hold only one league and one cup competition,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Boillat |first1=Camille |last2=Poli |first2=Raffaele |date=2014 |title=Governance models across football associations and leagues |url=https://www.cies.ch/fileadmin/documents/CIES/Governance_I_-_Models_across_football_associations_and_leagues.pdf |access-date=30 January 2022 |website=International Centre for Sports Studies |page=62 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201118161714/https://www.cies.ch/fileadmin/documents/CIES/Governance_I_-_Models_across_football_associations_and_leagues.pdf |archive-date=18 November 2020 |url-status=live }}</ref> thus clubs from countries like Spain, Germany and France, who used to hold a secondary national cup but were discontinued, currently cannot achieve the domestic treble in the most accepted form. Domestic trebles that might include super cups, or regional titles, are considered to have an "asterisk" attached to it for not being perceived as a "genuine" achievements.<ref name=Goaltreble>{{cite web |url=https://www.goal.com/en/news/which-football-teams-have-won-treble/1sk4fna3n446j1c7d5za94o5sq |title=Which football teams have won the treble? |date=10 June 2023 |access-date=16 June 2024 |website=[[Goal (website)|Goal.com]]}}</ref>

===Men===
76 men's clubs have won a domestic treble, 27 of whom have done it more than once, with 131 trebles occurring overall. The first team to win a domestic treble was Ireland's Shamrock Rovers in 1924–25. Scottish side Celtic has won the most domestic trebles, with eight.<ref name="doublerec" /> Celtic, Manchester City, Paris Saint-Germain and Bayern Munich are the only men's sides to have won both a domestic treble and a continental treble, but Celtic are the only one to win both in the same season. Celtic also hold the record for the most consecutive men's domestic trebles, with four.

===Women===
Eight women's clubs have won domestic trebles, and three of those teams have won their domestic treble more than once, for a total of 16 trebles overall. Scotland's Glasgow City and Arsenal of England have won the most domestic trebles, with five each. Glasgow City also have the most consecutive women's domestic trebles with four, while Arsenal were the first women's team to win a domestic treble when they won it in 1992–93. They, along with Tokyo Verdy Beleza of Japan and Corinthians of Brazil. are the only women's clubs to win both a continental treble and a domestic treble, and each team won the domestic treble in the same season they won the continental treble (2007 for Arsenal, 2019 for Tokyo Verdy and 2023 for Corinthians).
