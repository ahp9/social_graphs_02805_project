'''''Os Santásticos''''' ({{IPA|pt-BR|us sɐ̃ˈtastʃikus|lang}}, ''The Santastics'') is the nickname for the group of [[Santos FC|Santos Futebol Clube]] players coached by [[Luís Alonso Pérez|Lula]] and [[Antoninho (footballer, born 1921)|Antoninho]] that won a total of 25 titles between 1959 and 1974,<ref name="1959 Taça Brasil">{{cite web |url=http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/tablesae/cbr1959.htm |title=Brazil Cup 1959 |publisher=[[RSSSF]] |access-date=16 March 2010 |archive-date=12 January 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160112151815/http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/tablesae/cbr1959.htm |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Paulista">{{cite web|url=http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/tablesrz/spcamp.htm |title=São Paulo State – List of Champions |publisher=[[RSSSF]] |access-date=16 March 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100323023407/http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/tablesrz/spcamp.htm |archive-date=23 March 2010 }}</ref> including two [[Copa Libertadores]].<ref name="Libertadores">{{cite web |url=https://www.rsssf.org/sacups/copalib.html |title=Copa Libertadores de América |website=[[RSSSF]] |access-date=16 March 2010 |archive-date=24 May 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150524152408/http://www.rsssf.com/sacups/copalib.html |url-status=live }}</ref> The group is considered one of the strongest teams ever assembled in any sport,<ref name="CO 2003">{{cite book |last=Cunha |first=Odir |title=Time dos Sonhos |trans-title=Dream Teams |year=2003 |publisher=Códex |isbn=85-7594-020-1 |language=pt }}</ref> scoring over 3000 goals during this period, with an average of over 2.5 goals per match.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://pele.m-qp-m.us/english/pele_statistics.shtml|title=Pelé, Olé! - Statistics of Pelé's Career|website=pele.m-qp-m.us|access-date=2018-06-03|archive-date=30 April 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110430003313/http://pele.m-qp-m.us/english/pele_statistics.shtml|url-status=live}}</ref> 

Also known as '''''O Balé Branco''''' ({{IPA|pt|u baˈlɛ ˈbɾɐ̃ku|lang}}, ''The White Ballet'') or '''''Time dos Sonhos''''' ({{IPA|pt|ˈtʃĩmi dus ˈsõɲus|lang}}, ''Dream Team''), they dominated Brazilian football and became a symbol of ''[[Jogo Bonito|O Jogo Bonito]]'' thanks to figures such as [[Zito (footballer, born 1932)|Zito]], [[Gylmar dos Santos Neves|Gilmar]], [[Mauro Ramos|Mauro]], [[Mengálvio Pedro Figueiró|Mengálvio]], [[Antônio Wilson Vieira Honório|Coutinho]], [[José Macia|Pepe]] and the iconic [[Pelé]]. Pelé made Santos FC famous around the world in this revolutionary era, therefore his lesser-known teammates are best known as "Pelé's friends."

==The beginning==
In 1956, [[Waldemar de Brito]] took Pelé to [[Santos (São Paulo)|Santos]], an industrial and port city in the state of [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]], to try out for professional club [[Santos FC|Santos Futebol Clube]] telling the directors at Santos that the 15-year-old would be "the greatest football player in the world."<ref name="PTA">

</ref> Santos were at the time the top team in São Paulo, having just won two consecutive State champions when Pelé joined.

Aged 15, Pelé made his debut for Santos on 7 September 1956, scoring one goal in a 7–1 friendly victory over [[Corinthians Futebol Clube|Corinthians de Santo André]].<ref>[http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=6201421919861473613# The PELE Treasury – IOC Athlete of the 20th Century – The King of Football] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110607030357/http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=6201421919861473613 |date=7 June 2011 }} Retrieved 5 May 2011</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Diário Lance – www.lancenet.com.br |url=http://www.lancenet.com.br/noticias/07-10-29/183561.stm |title=// O Campeão da Rede |publisher=Lancenet |access-date=12 June 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080514032427/http://www.lancenet.com.br/noticias/07-10-29/183561.stm |archive-date=14 May 2008 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> When the 1957 season started, Pelé was given a starting place in the first team and, at the age of just 16, became the top scorer in the league. Just ten months after signing professionally, the teenager was called up to the [[Brazil national football team]]. After the [[1962 FIFA World Cup|World Cup in 1962]], wealthy European clubs such as Real Madrid, Juventus and Manchester United tried to sign the young player, but the government of Brazil declared Pelé an "official national treasure" to prevent him from being transferred out of the country.<ref name="treasure">{{cite web|url=http://www.frontfoot.co.za/pelefull.php?qr=pelebio|title=Biography&nbsp;– Edson Arantes "Pelé" Nascimento|work=Article on frontfoot.co.za|access-date=1 October 2006|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061020121835/http://www.frontfoot.co.za/pelefull.php?qr=pelebio|archive-date=20 October 2006|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Pelé won his first major title with Santos in 1958 as the team won the [[Campeonato Paulista]]; Pelé would finish the tournament as top scorer with an incredible 58 goals,<ref>[http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/folha/especial/2008/campeonatopaulista/artilheiros_da_historia-2.shtml Artilheiros da história] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110501110603/http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/folha/especial/2008/campeonatopaulista/artilheiros_da_historia-2.shtml |date=1 May 2011 }} Folha Online. Retrieved 6 May 2011</ref> a record that stands today. A year later, ''O Rei'' would help the team earn their first victory in the [[Torneio Rio-São Paulo]] with a 3–0 over Vasco da Gama.<ref>[http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/miscellaneous/matdecrjsp.htm Matches which decided Rio-São Paulo Tournament] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110423195647/http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/miscellaneous/matdecrjsp.htm |date=23 April 2011 }} RSSSF. Retrieved 6 May 2011</ref> However, Santos was unable to retain the Paulista title.

==1961: Brazilian champions==
{{main|1961 Santos FC season}}
In 1960, Pelé scored 33 goals to help his team regain the Campeonato Paulista trophy but lost out on the Rio-São Paulo tournament after finishing in a disappointing 8th place.<ref>[http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/tablesrz/rjsp1960.htm Torneio Rio-São Paulo 1960] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091216095534/http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/tablesrz/rjsp1960.htm |date=16 December 2009 }} RSSSF. Retrieved 6 May 2011</ref> Another 47 goals from Pelé saw Santos retain the Campeonato Paulista. The club went on to win the [[Taça Brasil]] that same year, crushing [[Esporte Clube Bahia|Bahia]] in the finals; Pelé finished as top scorer of the tournament with 9 goals. The victory allowed Santos to participate in the [[Copa Libertadores]], the most prestigious continental club tournament in the South America.<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/2769331.stm Santos revive spirit of Pelé] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160112150822/http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/2769331.stm |date=12 January 2016 }} ''BBC Sport'' Retrieved 5 May 2011</ref> In March 1961, Pelé scored the ''gol de placa'' (goal worthy of a plaque), against [[Fluminense Football Club|Fluminense]] at the Maracanã.<ref name="placa"/> Pelé received the ball on the edge of his own penalty area, and ran the length of the field, eluding opposition players, and fired the ball beyond the goalkeeper.<ref name="placa">[https://web.archive.org/web/20110308233101/http://www.fifa.com/classicfootball/news/newsid=1392726.html Remembering Pele's gol de placa] ''FIFA'' Retrieved 10 May 2011</ref> The goal was regarded as being so spectacular that a plaque was commissioned with a dedication to ''the most beautiful goal in the history of the Maracanã''.<ref>{{cite book |last= Bellos |first= Alex |title= Futebol: The Brazilian Way of Life |url= https://archive.org/details/futebolbrazilian0000bell |url-access= registration | page=[https://archive.org/details/futebolbrazilian0000bell/page/244 244]|publisher=[[Bloomsbury Publishing]] |year= 2002 |isbn= 0-7475-6179-6 }}</ref>

==1962: The first treble in the world==
Santos' most successful club season started in [[1962 Copa Libertadores|1962]];<ref name="PeleEterno">Anibal Massaini Neto (Director/Producer), (2004). ''[[Pelé Eterno]]'' [Documentary film]. Brazil: Anima Produções Audiovisuais Ltda. International: Universal Studios Home Video.</ref> the team was seeded in Group 1 alongside [[Cerro Porteño]] and [[Deportivo Municipal de La Paz|Deportivo Municipal]], winning every match of their group but one (a 1–1 away draw vs Cerro), with Pelé scoring his first goal in a brace against Cerro. Santos defeated [[Club Deportivo Universidad Católica|Universidad Católica]] in the semifinals and met [[1961 Copa Libertadores|defending champions]] [[C.A. Peñarol|Peñarol]] in the finals in which Pelé scored another brace in the playoff match to secure the first title for a Brazilian club. Pelé finished as the second best scorer of the competition with 4 goals. That same year, Santos would defend, with success, the Campeonato Paulista (with 37 goals from Pelé), the Taça Brasil (Pelé scoring four goals in the final series against Botafogo), and win the [[1962 Intercontinental Cup]] against [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]].<ref name="intercontinental">[https://www.fifa.com/classicfootball/clubs/matchreport/newsid=512321.html Intercontinental Cups 1962 and 1963] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120506041330/http://www.fifa.com/classicfootball/clubs/matchreport/newsid%3D512321.html |date=6 May 2012 }} ''FIFA'' Retrieved 5 May 2011</ref> Wearing his iconic number 10 shirt, Pelé produced one of his best ever performances and scored a [[hat-trick]] in Lisbon, as Santos beat the European champions 5–2.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/timvickery/2009/11/pele.html Will South Africa 2010 produce a new Pele?] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221108113259/https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/timvickery/2009/11/pele.html |date=8 November 2022 }} BBC Sport. Retrieved 5 May 2011</ref>

==1963–1965: ''Pentacampeão''==
Santos tried to defend their title again in [[1964 Copa Libertadores|1964]] but they were thoroughly beaten in both legs of the semifinals by [[Club Atlético Independiente|Independiente]]. Santos won again the Campeonato Paulista, with Pelé netting 34 goals. The club also shared the Rio-São Paulo title with Botafogo and win the Taça Brasil for the fourth consecutive year. The ''Santistas'' would try to resurge in 1965 by winning, for the 9th time, the Campeonato Paulista and the Taça Brasil. In the [[1965 Copa Libertadores]], Santos started convincingly by winning every match of their group in the first round. In the semifinals, Santos met Peñarol in a rematch of the 1962 final. After two legendary matches,<ref name=PeleEterno /> a playoff was needed to break the tie. Unlike 1962, Peñarol came out on top and eliminated Santos 2–1.<ref name=PeleEterno /> Pelé would, however, finish as the topscorer of the tournament with eight goals.<ref>[https://www.rsssf.org/sacups/copalibtops.html Copa Libertadores – Topscorers] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151031101245/http://www.rsssf.com/sacups/copalibtops.html |date=31 October 2015 }} Rsssf.com. Retrieved 10 May 2011</ref>

==1966: A forgettable season==
In 1966, Pelé and Santos also failed to retain the Taça Brasil as ''O Rei'''s goals weren't enough to prevent a 9–4 routing by [[Cruzeiro Esporte Clube|Cruzeiro]] (led by [[Tostão]]) in the final series. Although Santos won the Campeonato Paulista in 1967, 1968 and 1969, Pelé became less and less a contributing factor to the ''Santistas'' now-limited success.

==1967–1969: Rejuvenation of the ''Balé Branco''==
On 19 November 1969, Pelé scored his 1000th goal in all competitions. This was a highly anticipated moment in Brazil.<ref name=PeleEterno /> The goal, called popularly ''O Milésimo'' (The Thousandth), occurred in a match against [[Club de Regatas Vasco da Gama|Vasco da Gama]], when Pelé scored from a [[Penalty kick (association football)|penalty kick]], at the [[Estádio do Maracanã|Maracanã]] Stadium.<ref name="PeleEterno"/>
[[File:Huellas de Pelé.jpg|thumb|230px|The footprints of Pelé inside the [[Estádio do Maracanã|Maracanã]].]]
Pelé states that his most beautiful goal was scored at [[Estádio Rua Javari|Rua Javari stadium]] on a [[Campeonato Paulista]] match against São Paulo rival [[Clube Atlético Juventus|Juventus]] on 2 August 1959. As there is no video footage of this match, Pelé asked that a computer animation be made of this specific goal.<ref name=PeleEterno /> In 1967, the two factions involved in the [[Nigerian Civil War]] agreed to a 48-hour [[ceasefire]] so they could watch Pelé play an [[exhibition game]] in [[Lagos]].<ref name="Lagos">{{cite news|url=http://www.findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_m1608/is_2_22/ai_n16071178/pg_1|title=Ultimate Feats of Fitness|work=Article by Men's Fitness|access-date=1 October 2006|year=2006|archive-date=1 May 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080501192742/http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_m1608/is_2_22/ai_n16071178/pg_1|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==1970–1973: The last years==
After poor investments made by the club's board, Santos had to sell their best players to end their debts. The club also spent the following campaigns with long tours to Africa and Asia to raise funds, while a "reserve" squad played most of the national tournaments.

In 1973, the club won their last trophy with Pelé, the [[Campeonato Paulista]]; after a 0–0 draw against [[Associação Portuguesa de Desportos|Portuguesa]], Santos was ahead on the [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|shoot-out]] when the referee Armando Marques ended the match after miscalculating the score. Shortly after, the title was split between both clubs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/noticia/2013/08/um-campeonato-dois-campeoes-conheca-historia-do-paulista-de-73.html|title=Um campeonato, dois campeões: Conheça a história do Paulista de 73|trans-title=One tournament, two champions: Know the story of the 1973 Paulista|publisher=GloboEsporte.com|language=pt-BR|date=26 August 2013|access-date=30 December 2020|archive-date=8 November 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221108082508/https://ge.globo.com/sp/santos-e-regiao/noticia/2013/08/um-campeonato-dois-campeoes-conheca-historia-do-paulista-de-73.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Pelé subsequently announced his retirement in the end of the 1974 season.
