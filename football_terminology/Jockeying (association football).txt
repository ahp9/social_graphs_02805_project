{{Short description|Defender's skill in association football}}[[File:Austria vs. Russia 20141115 (064).jpg|thumb|Jockeying position (right)]]In [[association football]], '''jockeying''' (also called "shepherding" or "guiding") is the [[Defender (association football)|defender]]'s skill of keeping between the attacker and their intended target (usually the goal). It requires the defender to slow down or delay the attacker by backing off slowly while simultaneously trying to force an error or make a successful [[Tackle_(football_move)#Association_football|tackle]].<ref>{{Cite book|title=Skills and Strategies for Coaching Soccer|last=Hargreaves|first=Alan|publisher=Leisure Press|year=1990|isbn=0-88011-328-6|location=Champaign, Illinois|pages=[https://archive.org/details/skillsstrategies00harg/page/120 120]|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/skillsstrategies00harg/page/120}}</ref>

The defender should be in a low position with both knees bent, turned slightly at an angle from the attacker.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tonys-soccer-school.net/how-to-defend-jockey-in-football.html|title=HOW TO DEFEND - JOCKEY IN FOOTBALL|website=www.tonys-soccer-school.net|language=en-gb|access-date=2018-08-29}}</ref>
