'''The Front Five''' is the collective nickname used for the five [[Association football|footballers]] who were the primary [[Forward (association football)|forward line]] of [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] during the mid-1960s. [[Ian Butler (footballer)|Ian Butler]], [[Chris Chilton]], [[Ray Henderson (footballer)|Ray Henderson]], [[Ken Houghton]],  and [[Ken Wagstaff]] were all simultaneously active for the club between 1965 and 1968.<ref name="Butler">{{cite web|url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player/ianbutler.html|title=Ian Butler|work=Neil Brown|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref><ref name="Chilton">{{cite web|url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player/chrischilton.html|title=Chris Chilton|work=Neil Brown|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref><ref name="Henderson">{{cite web|url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player/rayhenderson.html|title=Ray Henderson|work=Neil Brown|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref><ref name="Houghton">{{cite web|url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player1/kenhoughton.html|title=Ken Houghton|work=Neil Brown|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref><ref name="Wagstaff">{{cite web|url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player/kenwagstaff.html|title=Ken Wagstaff|work=Neil Brown|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref> The quintet's zenith was the [[1965–66 Hull City A.F.C. season|1965–66]] season, in which Hull were promoted as champions of the [[Football League Third Division|Third Division]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hullcity-mad.co.uk/feat/edy1/19611980_rising_under_britton_then_decline_30228/index.shtml|title=1961–1980: Rising under Britton then Decline|work=Hull City Mad|date=2 January 2002|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref> During that campaign, the Tigers scored 109 goals in the league, 100 of which were scored by the Front Five.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wearehullcity.co.uk/news/2025/may/07/the-front-five/|title=The Front Five|work=Hull City A.F.C.|date=7 May 2025|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref>

==Ian Butler==
[[Ian Butler (footballer)|Ian Butler]] joined Hull City for £40,000 in January 1965, arriving from [[Rotherham United F.C.|Rotherham United]] alongside Houghton. Playing as a [[Midfielder#Winger|left winger]], he amassed 339 appearances across all competitions during his eight years with the club, scoring 70 goals before leaving in July 1973.<ref name="Butler"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oncloudseven.com/508-ian-butler/|title=508 Ian Butler|work=oncloudseven.com|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref>

==Chris Chilton==
[[Chris Chilton]] was first contracted to Hull City, his hometown club, at age 16. The [[Forward (association football)#Centre forward|centre forward]] would become one of the best players in the Tigers' history. Scoring 222 goals in 477 appearances across all competitions, "Chillo", as he was affectionally known, is still Hull's record goalscorer to this day. He was signed by [[Coventry City F.C.|Coventry City]] for £92,000 in August 1971.<ref name="Chilton"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oncloudseven.com/484-chris-chilton/|title=484 Chris Chilton|work=oncloudseven.com|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref> He was one of five inaugural inductees to Hull's [[Hull City A.F.C.#Hall of Fame|Hall of Fame]] in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wearehullcity.co.uk/club/history/hall-of-fame/chris-chilton/|title=Chris Chilton|work=Hull City A.F.C.|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref> Furthermore, the East Stand of the [[MKM Stadium]] was posthumously renamed in his honour on 2 March 2022.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wearehullcity.co.uk/news/2022/march/introducing-the-chris-chilton-stand/|title=Introducing the Chris Chilton Stand|work=Hull City A.F.C.|date=2 March 2022|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref> Chilton had died the year before, on 20 May 2021, aged 77.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wearehullcity.co.uk/news/2021/may/chris-chilton/|title=Chris Chilton|work=Hull City A.F.C.|date=20 May 2021|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref>

==Ray Henderson==
[[Ray Henderson (footballer)|Ray Henderson]] signed for Hull City from [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]] in June 1961. The Front Five's [[Midfielder#Winger|right winger]] would go on to rack up 61 goals from 264 appearances across all competitions during his seven-year spell in the [[East Riding of Yorkshire]]. He left for [[Reading F.C.|Reading]] in October 1968.<ref name="Henderson"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oncloudseven.com/489-ray-henderson/|title=489 Ray Henderson|work=oncloudseven.com|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref> The [[Wallsend]] native died on 18 January 2024, aged 86.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wearehullcity.co.uk/news/2024/january/18/in-memoriam--ray-henderson/|title=In Memoriam: Ray Henderson|work=Hull City A.F.C.|date=18 January 2024|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref>

==Ken Houghton==
[[Ken Houghton]] arrived alongside Butler from [[Rotherham United F.C.|Rotherham United]] in January 1965, also for £40,000. The [[Forward (association football)#Inside forward|inside forward]] played 304 times for the Tigers, scoring 91 goals in the process. He left for [[Humber derby]] rivals [[Scunthorpe United F.C.|Scunthorpe United]] in May 1973.<ref name="Houghton"/> Houghton returned to Hull, initially as interim manager but later permanent manager, in February 1978. He was dismissed in December 1979.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oncloudseven.com/509-ken-houghton/|title=509 Ken Houghton|work=oncloudseven.com|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref>

==Ken Wagstaff==
[[Ken Wagstaff]] signed for Hull City in November 1964, joining from [[Mansfield Town F.C.|Mansfield Town]]. Thanks to his partnership with the other four, particularly Chilton, the [[Forward (association football)#Inside forward|inside forward]] scored 197 goals in 434 appearances across all competitions whilst with the Tigers.<ref name="Wagstaff"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oncloudseven.com/507-ken-wagstaff/|title=507 Ken Wagstaff|work=oncloudseven.com|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref> Nicknamed "Waggy", he only left Hull upon retiring in 1976, and was later inducted to the club's [[Hull City A.F.C.#Hall of Fame|Hall of Fame]] in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wearehullcity.co.uk/club/history/hall-of-fame/ken-wagstaff/|title=Ken Wagstaff|work=Hull City A.F.C.|accessdate=11 May 2025}}</ref>
