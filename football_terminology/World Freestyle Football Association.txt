The '''World Freestyle Football Association''' ('''WFFA''') is the international [[Sport governing body|governing body]] for the sport of [[Freestyle Football]].<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=http://www.worldfreestylefootball.org/about/association|title=World Freestyle Football Association news|last=noCtrl|website=www.worldfreestylefootball.org|access-date=2019-05-03|archive-date=2019-09-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190913120203/http://www.worldfreestylefootball.org/about/association|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Freestyle Football is defined as an art and sport, which involves participants creatively juggling a ball using all parts of the body to entertain audiences and outperform opponents; it combines street culture of [[association football|football]] tricks, [[dance]], [[acrobatics]] and [[music]].

==Organisation==
The WFFA develops and manages the ''Official World Rankings System'', the ''World Freestyle Day'' (held annually on 1 September), all rules and regulations of official WFFA competitions, and the key WFFA events across the calendar, including the World Freestyle Football Championship, its continental qualifiers the Pulse Series, the Super Ball World Open, and the NextGen World Championship.

The WFFA is a non-profit organisation, headquartered in [[Barcelona]], [[Catalonia]], [[Spain]].<ref name="auto"/>

==Membership==
The organisation operates throughout 119 countries  (as of May 2025) with the mission of growing awareness and participation in the sport of [[Freestyle Football]]  for both [[male]] and [[females]] of all ages. The ambition for the sport to be used as a tool for positive social change in communities worldwide.  The Key Leaders in each country have a role to play in the overall governance of the sport, by casting votes on any rule changing matters and contributing new ideas to grow the sport in their regions. The structure and worldwide network for the sport has been in development since 2007.

==Events==
WFFA football events can be ''Open'' or ''Closed'' events. All Open-events (National, Continental and World) allow participants to score World Ranking points, whilst all Closed-events are invitation-only for pre-qualified or elite athletes.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|url=http://www.worldfreestylefootball.org/events|title=World Freestyle Football Association news|last=noCtrl|website=www.worldfreestylefootball.org}}</ref> Events can take place live or online. Events annually receive over 200 million engagements online and connect to audience across the world through social media platforms like [[Facebook]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/thewffa/|title=The World of Freestyle Football|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref> [[Instagram]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/thewffa/|title=WFFA (@thewffa)|website=www.instagram.com/thewffa}}</ref> [[YouTube]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/@theWFFA|title=WFFA (@thewffa)|website=www.youtube.com/@theWFFA}}</ref> Threads and [[TikTok]].

The key events in the freestyle football calendar include:

*[[Super Ball World Open]], [[Prague]], [[Czech Republic]].
*[[World Frestyle Football Championship]] (WFFC).
*Pulse Series, including five continental championships in North America, South America, Europe, Asia-Pacific and Africa that act as qualifiers for the WFFC.
*WFFA NextGen World Championshp, for athletes under the age of 16.
