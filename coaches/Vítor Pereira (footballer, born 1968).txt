'''Vítor Manuel de Oliveira Lopes Pereira''' (born 26 July 1968) is a Portuguese [[Association football|football]] [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and former player who played as a [[midfielder]].

Following an amateur playing career, he became manager of [[FC Porto|Porto]], where he won the [[Primeira Liga]] in both of his seasons. After leaving in 2013 he worked in several countries, winning a Greek league and cup [[Double (association football)|double]] with [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]] in 2015 and the [[Chinese Super League]] with [[Shanghai SIPG F.C.|Shanghai SIPG]] in [[2018 Chinese Super League|2018]].

==Football career==
===Early career===
Born in [[Espinho, Portugal|Espinho]], Pereira only played amateur football, and retired at the age of 28.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/vitor-pereira-o-treinador-tactico-perfil|title=Vítor Pereira, o treinador táctico (perfil)|trans-title=Vítor Pereira, the tactical manager (profile)|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Sérgio|last=Pereira|language=pt|date=21 June 2011|access-date=17 December 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.gazetaesportiva.com/apostas/guias/como-vitor-pereira-se-saira-em-sua-trajetoria-no-corinthians|title=Como Vitor Pereira se sairá em sua trajetória no Corinthians?|trans-title=How will Vitor Pereira fare in his Corinthians spell?|newspaper=Gazeta Esportiva|first=Rafael|last=Dornas|language=pt|date=7 March 2022|access-date=27 December 2022}}</ref> He started managing at junior level, and his first head coach experience arrived midway through the [[2004–05 Segunda Divisão B|2004–05 season]], when he was appointed at [[A.D. Sanjoanense|Sanjoanense]] in the [[Segunda Divisão|third division]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/fc-porto/detalhe/vitor-pereira-tem-lugar-em-equipa-com-mistica|title=Vítor Pereira tem lugar em equipa com mística|trans-title=Vítor Pereira has a place in a team with mystique|newspaper=[[Record (Portuguese newspaper)|Record]]|language=pt|date=31 May 2010|access-date=16 December 2019}}</ref>

Subsequently, Pereira worked with [[S.C. Espinho|Espinho]] of the same league,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/campeonato-de-portugal/detalhe/sp-espinho-infesta-0-0-tigres-marcam-passo-na-luta-pela-lideranca|title=Sp. Espinho-Infesta, 0–0: Tigres marcam passo na luta pela liderança|trans-title=Sp. Santa Clara|Santa Clara]] in the [[Segunda Liga]], leading the [[Azores]] side to the third position in his [[2008–09 Liga de Honra|first year]] and the fourth in [[2009–10 Liga de Honra|the second]], on both occasions narrowly missing out on [[Primeira Liga]] promotion.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/santa-clara/detalhe/vitor-pereira-olha-para-a-subida|title=Vítor Pereira olha para a subida|trans-title=Vítor Pereira eyeing promotion|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=28 April 2009|access-date=16 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/santa-clara/detalhe/chegou-a-hora-da-verdade|title=Chegou a hora da verdade|trans-title=The moment of truth has arrived|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=29 January 2010|access-date=16 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/primeira-liga/artigos/vitor-pereira-quer-ver-santa-clara-na-i-liga|title=Vítor Pereira quer ver Santa Clara na I Liga|trans-title=Vítor Pereira wants to see Santa Clara in the I League|publisher=[[SAPO (company)|SAPO]]|language=pt|date=25 July 2012|access-date=7 March 2023}}</ref>

===Porto===
In the summer of 2010, Pereira left Santa Clara to become assistant manager to [[André Villas-Boas]] at Porto.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/fc-porto/detalhe/muralha-de-aco-673653|title=Muralha de aço|trans-title=Steel wall|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=29 December 2010|access-date=16 December 2019}}</ref> On 21 June 2011, following the head coach's departure to [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], he was promoted to first-team manager,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gb/news/2885/europe/2011/06/21/2542084/new-porto-boss-vitor-pereira-says-club-will-continue-winning|title=New Porto boss Vitor Pereira says club will 'continue winning'|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|first=Luís|last=Mira|date=21 June 2011|access-date=21 June 2011}}</ref> winning his first official match – and title – [[2011 Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira|2–1]] against [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória de Guimarães]] in the [[Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.publico.pt/2011/08/07/desporto/noticia/epoca-nova-o-mesmo-fc-porto-1506629|title=Época nova, o mesmo FC Porto|trans-title=New season, same FC Porto|newspaper=[[Público (Portugal)|Público]]|first=David|last=Andrade|language=pt|date=7 August 2011|access-date=6 August 2019}}</ref>

Despite a less than stellar performance both in [[UEFA|Europe]] – being knocked out of the [[UEFA Champions League]] in the [[2011–12 UEFA Champions League|group stage]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rtp.pt/noticias/fc-porto/porto-eliminado-da-champions_d506939|title=Porto eliminado da "Champions"|trans-title=Porto ousted from the Champions League|publisher=[[Rádio e Televisão de Portugal]]|first=Pedro|last=Araújo Pina|language=pt|date=6 December 2011|access-date=7 March 2023}}</ref> and in the [[UEFA Europa League]]'s [[2011–12 UEFA Europa League|round of 32]]<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.jn.pt/desporto/derrota-por-4-0-foi-um-resultado-mentiroso-diz-vitor-pereira-2320405.html|title=Derrota por 4–0 foi um "resultado mentiroso", diz Vítor Pereira|trans-title=4–0 loss was a "false score", says Vítor Pereira|newspaper=[[Jornal de Notícias]]|language=pt|date=22 February 2012|access-date=7 March 2023}}</ref>– and in the [[Taça de Portugal]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://desporto.sapo.pt/futebol/primeira-liga/artigos/uma-nova-licao-da-academica-ao-dragao|title=Uma nova lição da Académica ao Dragão|trans-title=Académica teach another lesson to Dragon|publisher=SAPO|language=pt|date=10 March 2012|access-date=7 March 2023}}</ref> Pereira led the club to the [[2011–12 Primeira Liga|league title]] in his first season in charge.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.publico.pt/2012/04/29/desporto/noticia/vitor-pereira-heide-dar-a-este-clube-muitos-mais-titulos-1544075|title=Vítor Pereira: "Hei-de dar a este clube muitos mais títulos"|trans-title=Vítor Pereira: "I will give many more titles to this club"|newspaper=Público|language=pt|date=29 April 2012|access-date=7 March 2023}}</ref> In March 2013, following the team's [[2012–13 UEFA Champions League|elimination from Champions League contention]] (2–1 on aggregate against [[Málaga CF|Málaga]]), he came under heavy criticism, notably due to his decision of benching [[James Rodríguez]] during the first half of both legs. The Colombian stated his disappointment in the coach's decision, but said that he respected him and trusted his reasons, adding that the situation was possibly created because of his questionable fitness, even though he claimed to be 100% fit.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2013/03/14/manchester-united-target-james-rodriguez-unhappy-being-benched-b/|title=Manchester United target James Rodriguez unhappy being benched by Porto|publisher=[[HITC]]|first=Dan|last=Coombs|date=14 March 2013|access-date=7 March 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2013/03/15/porto-coach-hits-back-at-unhappy-manchester-united-target-james/|title=Porto coach hits back at unhappy Manchester United target James Rodriguez|publisher=HITC|first=Dan|last=Coombs|date=15 March 2013|access-date=7 March 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://maisfutebol.iol.pt/vitor-pereira-e-james-afinal-quem-tem-razao|title=Vítor Pereira e James: afinal, quem tem razão?|trans-title=Vítor Pereira and James: who's right, after all?|publisher=Mais Futebol|first=Pedro Jorge|last=Da Cunha|language=pt|date=15 March 2013|access-date=8 February 2023}}</ref>

In early May 2013, as Porto ranked second in the league, Pereira deemed the Portuguese league as a "dirty competition".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/fc-porto/detalhe/vitor-pereira-e-um-campeonato-sujinho-sujinho-819236|title=Vítor Pereira: "É um campeonato sujinho, sujinho"|trans-title=Vítor Pereira: "It's a dirty, dirty championship"|newspaper=Record|language=pt|date=2 May 2013|access-date=6 August 2019}}</ref> Only a few days later, after his team defeated [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] at home to surpass its opponents – eventually winning [[2012–13 Primeira Liga|the league title]], conceding six draws in 30 games<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=por/news/newsid=1954050.html|title=Unbeaten Porto wrap up 27th league title|publisher=UEFA|first=Nuno|last=Tavares|date=19 May 2013|access-date=20 May 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130520064442/http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=por/news/newsid=1954050.html|archive-date=20 May 2013}}</ref>– he considered it to be a "highly competitive and prestigious league".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.zerozero.pt/noticia.php?id=110997|title=Vítor Pereira: "Foi uma Liga competitiva e de alto nível"|trans-title=Vítor Pereira: "It was a competitive and prestigious League"|publisher=Zerozero|first=José|last=Bragança|language=pt|date=18 May 2013|access-date=23 May 2013}}</ref>

===Five clubs in eight years===
[[File:Vítor Pereira (Fenerbahçe, 2015).jpg|thumb|left|150px|Pereira as manager of [[Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)|Fenerbahçe]] in August 2015]]
In late May 2013, Pereira was interviewed for the vacant job at [[Premier League]] club [[Everton F.C.|Everton]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/vitor-pereira-shouldnt-ruled-out-4029454|title=Vitor Pereira shouldn't be ruled out of Everton FC manager hunt|newspaper=[[Liverpool Echo]]|first=Greg|last=O'Keeffe|date=30 May 2013|access-date=20 June 2013}}</ref> but eventually signed a two-year deal with [[Al Ahli Saudi FC|Al Ahli]] of the [[Saudi Professional League]]. On 7 January 2015 he moved countries again, replacing the fired [[Míchel (footballer, born 1963)|Míchel]] at the helm of [[Olympiacos F.C.|Olympiacos]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.olympiacos.org/en/article/44212/vitor-pereira-new-coach-olympiacos|title=Vitor Pereira, new coach of Olympiacos|publisher=Olympiacos F.C|date=7 January 2015|access-date=8 January 2015}}</ref> and eventually winning [[Double (association football)|the double]].<ref name=IND>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/international/olympiakos-win-greek-league-for-the-fifth-straight-year-10188335.html|title=Olympiakos win Greek league for the fifth straight year|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|first=Konstantinos|last=Lianos|date=19 April 2015|access-date=22 April 2015}}</ref>

On 10 June 2015, Olympiacos announced a mutual contract termination with Pereira.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.olympiacos.org/article/48558/lysi-synergasias-me-ton-bitor-pereira|title=Λύση συνεργασίας με τον Βίτορ Περέιρα|trans-title=Collaboration with Vítor Pereira ended|publisher=Olympiacos F.C|language=el|date=10 June 2015|access-date=10 June 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304111934/https://www.olympiacos.org/article/48558/lysi-synergasias-me-ton-bitor-pereira/|archive-date=4 March 2016}}</ref> The following day, he was appointed at [[Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)|Fenerbahçe]] for two years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fenerbahce.org/eng/detay.asp?ContentID=3544|title=Our new football coach Vitor Pereira|publisher=Fenerbahçe S.K|date=11 June 2015|access-date=11 June 2015}}</ref>

The Turkish side cut ties unilaterally with Pereira on 15 August 2016, with the case being subsequently taken to the [[Court of Arbitration for Sport]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maisfutebol.iol.pt/internacional/15-08-2016/oficial-fenerbahce-termina-contrato-de-vitor-pereira|title=OFICIAL: Fenerbahçe termina contrato de Vítor Pereira|trans-title=OFFICIAL: Fenerbahçe end Vítor Pereira's contract|publisher=Mais Futebol|language=pt|date=15 August 2016|access-date=15 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://aa.com.tr/en/sports/turkey-s-fenerbahce-sacks-manager-pereira-mid-contract/628930|title=Turkey's Fenerbahce sacks manager Pereira mid-contract|publisher=[[Anadolu Agency]]|date=15 August 2016|access-date=15 August 2016}}</ref> On 18 December, he was named head coach of [[TSV 1860 Munich|1860 Munich]] in the German [[2. Bundesliga]], with the one-and-a-half-year deal being made effective the following 1 January.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dfb.de/news/detail/portugiese-pereira-neuer-trainer-von-1860-muenchen-159742/|title=Vítor Pereira neuer Trainer von 1860 München|trans-title=Vítor Pereira new 1860 Munich manager|publisher=[[German Football Association]]|language=de|date=18 December 2016|access-date=18 December 2016}}</ref> The team finished [[2016–17 2. Bundesliga|the season]] third from bottom, and eventually got relegated on 30 May 2017 after a 3–1 loss aggregate loss to [[SSV Jahn Regensburg]] in the play-offs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/40100802|title=Former Bundesliga champions 1860 Munich relegated to Germany's third tier|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=30 May 2017|access-date=30 May 2017}}</ref>

Pereira became the manager of [[Shanghai SIPG F.C.|Shanghai SIPG]] on 12 December 2017, replacing Villas-Boas at the [[Chinese Super League]] club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sports.sina.com.cn/china/j/2017-12-12/doc-ifypnsip9940008.shtml|title=官宣!佩雷拉成为上港新任主帅 昔日曾辅佐博阿斯|trans-title=Official announcement! Former Villas-Boas assistant Pereira became the new coach|publisher=[[Sina Sports]]|language=zh|date=12 December 2017|access-date=12 December 2017}}</ref> In his [[2018 Chinese Super League|debut campaign]], he guided them to their first-ever title in the competition.<ref name=China>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/desportos/interior/vitor-pereira-conquista-titulo-chines-e-faz-historia-a-triplicar-10141898.html|title=Vitor Pereira conquista título chinês e faz história a triplicar|trans-title=Vitor Pereira wins Chinese title and makes history three times|newspaper=[[Diário de Notícias]]|first=David|last=Pereira|language=pt|date=7 November 2018|access-date=6 August 2019}}</ref> In December 2019 he turned down a new approach from Everton<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/50735683|title=Vitor Pereira out of running for Everton manager's job|publisher=BBC Sport|date=10 December 2019|access-date=11 December 2019}}</ref> and, one year later, left Shanghai.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.the-afc.com/competitions/afc-champions-league/latest/news/vitor-pereira-leaves-shanghai-sipg|title=Vitor Pereira, Shanghai SIPG part ways|publisher=[[Asian Football Confederation]]|date=31 December 2020|access-date=31 December 2020}}</ref>

Pereira returned to Fenerbahçe on 2 July 2021, on a two-year contract.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://portugoal.net/portuguese-abroad/2167-an-unfinished-love-vitor-pereira-returns-as-fenerbahce-manager|title="An unfinished love" Vítor Pereira returns as Fenerbahçe manager|publisher=PortuGOAL|first=Sean|last=Gillen|date=2 July 2021|access-date=5 July 2021}}</ref> He was sacked on 20 December after a 2–2 home draw with [[Istanbul]] rivals [[Beşiktaş J.K.|Beşiktaş]]; his side were in fifth position and 14 points behind leaders [[Trabzonspor]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://portugoal.net/portuguese-abroad/2389-official-fenerbahce-sack-portuguese-coach-vitor-pereira|title=Official: Fenerbahce sack Portuguese coach Vítor Pereira|publisher=PortuGOAL|first=Sean|last=Gillen|date=21 December 2021|access-date=27 December 2021}}</ref> A month later, he was again linked with Everton as owner [[Farhad Moshiri]]'s lead candidate, prompting anger and vandalism from fans who saw him as inadequate and wanted eventual appointee [[Frank Lampard]] instead; he defended his reputation in an interview on [[Sky Sports News]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/jan/26/vitor-pereira-defends-record-in-attempt-to-placate-everton-fans|title=Vítor Pereira defends record in attempt to placate Everton fans|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|first=Andy|last=Hunter|date=26 January 2022|access-date=1 February 2022}}</ref>

===Flamengo===
Shortly after leaving Corinthians, Pereira was appointed at Flamengo also of the Brazilian top flight.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ge.globo.com/futebol/times/flamengo/noticia/2022/12/13/flamengo-anuncia-acordo-com-vitor-pereira.ghtml|title=Flamengo anuncia acordo com treinador Vitor Pereira|trans-title=Flamengo announce agreement with manager Vitor Pereira|publisher=Globo Esporte|language=pt|date=13 December 2022|access-date=13 December 2022}}</ref> On 28 January 2023, his team [[2023 Supercopa do Brasil|lost]] the [[Supercopa do Brasil]] 4–3 to [[SE Palmeiras|Palmeiras]].<ref name=SB>{{cite news|url=https://www.lance.com.br/futebol-nacional/palmeiras-vence-flamengo-por-a-em-jogo-eletrizante-e-conquista-a-supercopa-do-brasil.html|title=Palmeiras vence Flamengo em jogo eletrizante e conquista a Supercopa do Brasil|trans-title=Palmeiras beat Flamengo in electrifying match and conquer Brazilian Supercup|newspaper=[[Lance!]]|first=Matheus|last=Dantas|language=pt|date=28 January 2023|access-date=8 February 2023}}</ref> His second defeat took place on 7 February, 3–2 against [[Al Hilal SFC|Al Hilal]] in the [[2022 FIFA Club World Cup|semi-finals]] of the [[FIFA Club World Cup]] after playing the entire second half with one player less.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ge.globo.com/futebol/mundial-de-clubes/jogo/07-02-2023/flamengo-alhilal.ghtml|title=Flamengo perde do Al-Hilal e dá adeus precoce ao sonho do bi mundial|trans-title=Flamengo lose to Al-Hilal and say goodbye to dream of second World Cup early on|publisher=Globo Esporte|language=pt|date=7 February 2023|access-date=8 February 2023}}</ref> Later that month, they were [[2023 Recopa Sudamericana|beaten]] on penalties by Ecuador's [[C.S.D. Independiente del Valle|Independiente del Valle]] in the [[Recopa Sudamericana]] (1–1 over two legs).<ref name=RS>{{cite news|url=https://www.lance.com.br/flamengo/flamengo-frustra-torcida-perde-nos-penaltis-para-o-del-valle-e-e-vice-campeao-da-recopa-sul-americana.html|title=Flamengo frustra torcida, perde nos pênaltis para o Del Valle e é vice-campeão da Recopa Sul-Americana|trans-title=Flamengo frustrate fans, lose to Del Valle on penalties and are Recopa Sudamericana runners-up|newspaper=Lance!|first=Matheus|last=Dantas|language=pt|date=1 March 2023|access-date=7 March 2023}}</ref>

Pereira was dismissed on 11 April 2023, two days after [[2023 Campeonato Carioca|losing 4–3 on aggregate]] to [[Fla–Flu|rivals]] [[Fluminense FC|Fluminense]] in the [[Campeonato Carioca]].<ref name=CC>{{cite web|url=https://www.uol.com.br/esporte/futebol/ultimas-noticias/2023/04/11/vitor-pereira-flamengo-demitido.htm|title=Flamengo demite Vítor Pereira após sequência de tropeços em decisões|trans-title=Flamengo dismiss Vítor Pereira following shortcomings galore in deciders|publisher=[[Universo Online]]|first=Bruno|last=Madrid|language=pt|date=11 April 2023|access-date=11 April 2023}}</ref>

===Al Shabab===
Pereira returned to the [[Saudi Pro League]] on 4 February 2024, replacing the fired [[Igor Bišćan]] at [[Al Shabab FC (Riyadh)|Al Shabab]] on at a deal until the end of [[2023–24 Saudi Pro League|the season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kingfut.com/2024/02/04/vitor-pereira-al-shabab-manager/|title=OFFICIAL: Vítor Pereira appointed Al Shabab new head coach|publisher=[[KingFut]]|first=Ali Ismail|last=Gaballah|date=4 February 2024|access-date=5 February 2024}}</ref> He took charge of the side when they were just four points above the relegation zone and led them to eighth place, subsequently renewing his contract until June 2025.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://observador.pt/2024/06/23/vitor-pereira-renova-por-uma-epoca-pelos-sauditas-do-al-shabab/|title=Vítor Pereira renova por uma época pelos sauditas do Al Shabab|trans-title=Vítor Pereira renews for one season with Saudi Al Shabab|newspaper=[[Observador]]|language=pt|date=23 June 2024|access-date=17 December 2024}}</ref>

On 5 April 2025, Wolves stretched its points total to 12 points above the relegation zone with a 2–1 victory at [[Ipswich Town F.C.|Ipswich Town]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6257901/2025/04/07/wolves-vitor-pereira-andre-fans/|title=Wolves’ wild celebrations after Ipswich win: Andre’s selfie, a viral video and Pereira in the pub|publisher=[[The Athletic]]|first=Steve|last=Madeley|date=7 April 2025|access-date=26 April 2025}}</ref> The following weekend, after defeating [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur]] 4–2 at home, Pereira celebrated with fans at the Moon Under Water pub.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1drx4zpwkro|title=Wolves head coach joins celebratory fans in the pub|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Caroline|last=Gall|date=14 April 2025|access-date=26 April 2025}}</ref> On the 20th, they [[2024–25 Premier League|confirmed survival]] with the 1–0 away win against [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]], completing a league double over their opponent for the first time since the [[1979–80 Football League|1979–80 season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/manchester-united-vs-wolverhampton-wanderers/report/506134|title=Man Utd 0–1 Wolves: Pablo Sarabia's fine free-kick secures Premier League status for Vitor Pereira's side|publisher=[[Sky Sports]]|first=Peter|last=Smith|date=20 April 2025|access-date=28 April 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.beinsports.com/en-us/soccer/premier-league/articles/wolves-semedo-eyeing-strong-finish-after-sealing-premier-league-survival-2025-04-24|title=Wolves' Semedo eyeing strong finish after sealing Premier League survival|publisher=[[beIN Sports]]|date=24 April 2025|access-date=28 April 2025}}</ref>

Pereira signed a new three-year deal on 18 September 2025, in spite of having lost the first four games of the [[2025–26 Premier League|new campaign]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/c7v1rv1v2nno|title=Vitor Pereira: Wolves manager signs new three-year contract at Molineux|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Steve|last=Sutcliffe|date=18 September 2025|access-date=19 September 2025}}</ref> On 2 November, however, with the side bottom of the table with just two points, he was sacked.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/13458316/vitor-pereira-sacked-wolves-dismiss-portuguese-boss-after-winless-premier-league-start-and-carabao-cup-defeat|title=Vitor Pereira sacked: Wolves dismiss Portuguese boss after winless Premier League start and Carabao Cup defeat|publisher=Sky Sports|date=2 November 2025|access-date=2 November 2025}}</ref>
