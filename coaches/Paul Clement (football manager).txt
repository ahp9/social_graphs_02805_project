'''Paul Clement''' (born 8 January 1972) is an English professional [[association football|football]] [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and former player, who is the assistant coach to [[Carlo Ancelotti]] of the [[Brazil national football team|Brazil national team]].
He has previously been assistant manager to Ancelotti several times – at [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]], [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]], and [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]]. Clement has managed [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]], [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]], [[Reading F.C.|Reading]], and [[Cercle Brugge]] and has previously held coaching roles at [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]], [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]], [[Everton F.C.|Everton]], and the [[England national under-21 football team|England under-21]] and [[Republic of Ireland national under-21 football team|Republic of Ireland under-21]] teams.

In his first season as assistant manager, he won the [[Premier League]] title with Ancelotti at Chelsea.<ref name=Chelsea>{{cite web|url=http://www.chelseafc.com/the-club/history/style/trophy-cabinet/league-titles/premier-league--2009---10.html |title=Premier League champions: 2009/10 |publisher=Chelsea F.C.}}</ref> They also won the [[UEFA Champions League]] title and [[Copa del Rey]] in their first season at Real Madrid.<ref name = "Real">{{cite news| url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/27383593 |title=Real Madrid 4–1 Atletico Madrid |work=BBC Sport |date=24 May 2014 |access-date=25 May 2014}}</ref> Clement obtained his [[UEFA Pro Licence]] in 2009, and made his managerial debut with Derby County in 2015.<ref name="Derby County appoint Paul Clement as head coach">{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32955201 |title=Derby County appoint Paul Clement as head coach |work=BBC Sport |publisher=BBC |date=1 June 2015 |access-date=1 June 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
===Early coaching===
Clement was born in [[Wandsworth]], London.<ref name="Derby Profile">{{cite web |url=https://www.dcfc.co.uk/news/2015/06/in-profile-paul-clement |title=In profile: Paul Clement |publisher=Derby County F.C. |date=1 June 2015 |access-date=28 September 2017}}</ref> He is the elder son of former [[Queens Park Rangers F.C.|Queens Park Rangers]] and [[England national football team|England]] player [[Dave Clement]] and brother of former [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]] player [[Neil Clement]].<ref name = psg>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/18071937 |title=England's French connection at Paris St-Germain |first=Matt |last=Spiro |work=BBC Sport |publisher=BBC |date=16 May 2012 |access-date=4 September 2013}}</ref> Paul Clement, however, did not progress as a player beyond non-league football with [[Banstead Athletic F.C.|Banstead Athletic]] and [[Corinthian Casuals F.C.|Corinthian Casuals]].<ref name = "psg"/><ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.surreycomet.co.uk/news/11227496.Teacher_Paul_keeps_it_will_keep_it_Real_at_the_Bernabeu/ | title=Teacher Paul keeps it will keep it Real at the Bernabeu | work=Surrey Comet | access-date=1 June 2015}}</ref> He concentrated on coaching from the age of 23, as he worked in the [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] Centre of Excellence while holding down a job as a [[physical education|PE]] teacher at [[Glenthorne High School]].<ref name="Derby Profile"/><ref name = "psg"/> Clement obtained his [[UEFA A Licence|UEFA 'A' coaching licence]] in 1999 and became a full-time football coach in 2000, when [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]] appointed him to a role in their academy having been their Head of Education and Welfare.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NT4jlVvtxqs|title=FEATURE &#124; Paul Clement On His Coaching Career|via=www.youtube.com}}</ref><ref name = "psg"/> Clement also helped coach the [[Republic of Ireland national under-21 football team]], working with [[Don Givens]].<ref name = "psg"/>

===Chelsea===
Clement returned to Chelsea in 2007, initially working with their under-16 team.<ref name = "psg"/> He progressed through the coaching ranks there, working under managers [[José Mourinho]], [[Avram Grant]], and [[Luiz Felipe Scolari]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/av/football/24077190 |title=The English assistant at Real Madrid |work=BBC Sport |date=17 September 2013 |access-date=17 October 2017}}</ref> He took charge of the Chelsea [[Chelsea F.C. Under-23s and Academy|reserve team]], replacing [[Brendan Rodgers]], before working with the Chelsea first team when [[Guus Hiddink]] was appointed manager in 2009.<ref name =psg/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chelseafc.com/news/blogs/inside-blue/inside-blue/swansea_s-blue-bosses-.html |title=Swansea's Blue Bosses |publisher=Chelsea F.C. |date=24 February 2017 |access-date=18 October 2017}}</ref> Clement then became assistant manager to [[Carlo Ancelotti]] during his two seasons at Chelsea.<ref name = "psg"/> They went on to win the [[Premier League]] title and [[FA Cup]] in their [[2009–10 Chelsea F.C. season|first season]] in charge.<ref name=Chelsea/><ref name="Clement Chelsea">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39079614 |title= Swansea City: Paul Clement seeking happy return at Stamford Bridge |work=BBC Sport |date=24 February 2017 |access-date=1 March 2017}}</ref> Ancelotti was sacked in his second season and Clement left the club soon after.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/feb/24/paul-clement-swansea-chelsea-stamford-bridge |title=Paul Clement relishes being back at Chelsea six years after the boot |first=Dominic |last=Fifield |newspaper=The Guardian |date=24 February 2017 |access-date=17 October 2017}}</ref>

After Chelsea, Clement briefly worked as a coach at [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]] for four months, assisting [[Steve Kean]] in the 2011–12 season.<ref name = "psg"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/football-league/blackburn-vs-derby-match-report-paul-clement-escapes-former-home-with-a-point-a6703571.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220525/https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/football-league/blackburn-vs-derby-match-report-paul-clement-escapes-former-home-with-a-point-a6703571.html |archive-date=25 May 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |title=Blackburn vs Derby match report: Paul Clement escapes former home with a point|website=[[Independent.co.uk]]|date=21 October 2015}}</ref>

===Paris Saint-Germain===
Clement was then hired by [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] after Ancelotti had been appointed their head coach mid-season in December 2011.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2013/mar/08/paul-clement-englishman-psg |title=Paul Clement more than happy to be the 'other' Englishman at PSG |work=The Guardian |publisher=Guardian News and Media Limited |date=8 March 2013 |access-date=4 September 2013 |first=Dominic |last=Fifield }}</ref> The pair won the [[Ligue 1]] title in their [[2012–13 Ligue 1|only full season]] at the club; it was PSG's first league title since 1994.<ref name="PSG title">{{cite news|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-soccer-france-idUKBRE94B0DW20130512 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170302112136/http://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-soccer-france-idUKBRE94B0DW20130512 |url-status=dead |archive-date=2 March 2017 |title=PSG clinch first French league title since 1994 |work=Reuters |date=13 May 2013 |access-date=1 March 2017}}</ref> Whilst at the club, Clement coached such players as [[David Beckham]] and [[Zlatan Ibrahimović]].<ref name=Players>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/dec/16/derby-county-paul-clement-carlo-ancelotti |title= Derby's Paul Clement: 'Seeing how Carlo Ancelotti operated was invaluable'  |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |date=16 December 2015 |access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref>

===Real Madrid===
Clement was appointed assistant manager at [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]] after Ancelotti became their head coach in June 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/sport/story/216295.html |title=English coach joins Zidane on Ancelotti's Real staff |work=espn.co.uk |publisher=ESPN Sports Media |date=26 June 2013 |access-date=4 September 2013 |archive-date=21 August 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140821190943/http://www.espn.co.uk/football/sport/story/216295.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> During his time at the club, Clement worked alongside [[Zinedine Zidane]] and coached players including [[Sergio Ramos]], [[Xabi Alonso]] and [[Cristiano Ronaldo]].<ref name ="Players"/> He helped them win the [[Copa del Rey]] and tenth [[UEFA Champions League]] title, along with the [[UEFA Super Cup]], at the end of his first season at the [[Santiago Bernabéu Stadium|Bernabéu]].<ref name = "Real"/><ref name="Super Cup">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28741099 |title=Super Cup: Cristiano Ronaldo scores twice in Real Madrid win |work=BBC Sport |date=12 August 2014 |access-date=10 July 2017}}</ref> They also went on to win the club's first [[FIFA Club World Cup]] in 2014.<ref name="FIFA Club World Cup">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/30565151 |title=Club World Cup final: Real Madrid 2–0 San Lorenzo |work=BBC Sport |date=20 December 2014 |access-date=10 July 2017}}</ref>

After the team failed to win [[La Liga]] in their second season, Ancelotti was sacked on 25 May 2015 and Clement walked out four days later.<ref>{{cite news|title=Real Madrid: Paul Clement exit follows sacking of Carlo Ancelotti|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32937777|access-date=29 May 2015|work=BBC Sport|date=29 May 2015}}</ref>

===Derby County===
Soon after, on 1 June, he was appointed [[manager (association football)|manager]] of [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Derby County F.C.|Derby County]], who had sacked [[Steve McClaren]] after finishing eighth [[2014–15 Football League Championship|the previous season]].<ref name="Derby County appoint Paul Clement as head coach"/> Despite only losing once in 19 games from September to December, Clement was sacked on 8 February 2016 after one win in seven.<ref name="Paul Clement: Derby County sack head coach">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35528402|title=Paul Clement: Derby County sack head coach|work=BBC Sport |publisher=BBC|date=8 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/football/news/22705/10717033/is-paul-clement-the-man-to-keep-swansea-in-the-premier-league |title=Is Paul Clement the man to keep Swansea in the Premier League? |publisher=Sky Sports |date=3 January 2017 |access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref> His final match was a 1–1 draw against [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]].<ref name="Derby County » Fixtures & Results 2015/2016">{{cite web|title=Derby County » Fixtures & Results 2015/2016|url=https://www.worldfootball.net/teams/te476/derby-county/all-matches/|publisher=World Football|access-date=21 June 2016}}</ref> They were in fifth place at the time he was sacked.<ref name="Paul Clement: Derby County sack head coach"/> He finished with a record of 14 wins, 12 draws, and seven losses.<ref name=Soccerbase/>

In a statement, club chairman [[Mel Morris (businessman)|Mel Morris]] said that a lack of progress had been made under Clement, despite the club being only five points behind leaders [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35533154 |title=Mel Morris: Paul Clement's short-term view cost him Derby job |work=BBC Sport |date=9 February 2016 |access-date=6 November 2017}}</ref><ref name="Derby Sack">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2016/feb/09/derby-county-paul-clement-gamble-sacked |title=Why did Derby abort the Paul Clement gamble after only seven months? |newspaper=The Guardian |date=9 February 2016 |access-date=6 November 2017}}</ref> Clement's style of football was also cited as a contributing factor.<ref name="Derby Sack"/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/35524122 |title=Paul Clement leaves Derby: Is his sacking the strangest this season? |work=BBC Sport |date=9 February 2016 |access-date=6 November 2017}}</ref>

After Derby, Clement briefly helped coach the [[England national under-21 football team|England under-21 team]], working under manager [[Gareth Southgate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefa.com/news/2016/mar/24/gareth-southgate-paul-clement-england-under-21s |title=Gareth Southgate grateful to have Paul Clement in U21s camp |publisher=The FA |date=24 March 2016 |access-date=9 July 2017}}</ref>

===Bayern Munich===
Clement was hired by [[FC Bayern Munich|Bayern Munich]] in June 2016 as their assistant manager, again working alongside Ancelotti.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ancelotti macht sein Trainerteam komplett|url=http://www.sueddeutsche.de/sport/fc-bayern-muenchen-ancelotti-macht-sein-trainerteam-komplett-1.3042833|access-date=21 June 2016|newspaper=Süddeutsche Zeitung|date=21 June 2016|language=de}}</ref> He helped Bayern to win the [[2016 DFL-Supercup]], beating [[Borussia Dortmund]] 2–0.<ref name=Bayern>{{cite web |url=http://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/noblsp-2016-supercup-dortmund-bayern-post-match-interview.jsp |title=2016 Supercup: Exclusive post-match interviews |publisher=Bundesliga.com |access-date=15 August 2017 |archive-date=2 January 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170102082730/http://www.bundesliga.com/en/news/Bundesliga/noblsp-2016-supercup-dortmund-bayern-post-match-interview.jsp |url-status=dead }}</ref> Whilst at the club, Clement and Ancelotti changed their usual coaching method of [[Marking (association football)#Man-to-man marking|man marking]] to resemble the [[Marking (association football)#Zonal marking|zonal marking]] employed by former Bayern manager [[Pep Guardiola]].<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/39231357 |title=Swansea City: Paul Clement says Pep Guardiola sold him on zonal marking |work=BBC Sport |date=10 March 2017 |access-date=9 July 2017}}</ref> As of December 2016, Clement and Ancelotti only lost one match during their time in the [[Bundesliga]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.walesonline.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/who-swansea-city-manager-favourite-12384329 |title=Who is Swansea City manager favourite Paul Clement and why was he sacked in his only job as a boss? |publisher=WalesOnline}}</ref>

===Swansea City===
Clement was appointed as the new head coach of Premier League club [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]] on 3 January 2017, with the club in the [[Promotion and relegation|relegation zone]] having sacked their second manager of the season, [[Bob Bradley]].<ref name=SwanseaIn>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38492260 |title=Swansea City: Paul Clement confirmed as third boss of the season |work=BBC Sport |date=3 January 2017 |access-date=23 March 2018}}</ref> [[Claude Makélélé]] joined him as his assistant; Clement and Makélélé had previously coached at Paris Saint-Germain.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/38588997 |title=Swansea City appoint Claude Makelele as their assistant manager |work=BBC Sport |date=11 January 2017 |access-date=19 January 2017}}</ref>

===Reading===
[[Jaap Stam]] left [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Reading F.C.|Reading]] by mutual consent on 21 March 2018; Clement was announced as his successor two days later on a three-year contract.<ref name=ReadingIn>{{cite news |title=Reading: Paul Clement named new manager at Championship club |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/43499111 |work=BBC Sport |date=23 March 2018 |access-date=23 March 2018}}</ref> He was sacked on 6 December 2018 after poor results left the club outside of the relegation zone only on [[goal difference]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Paul Clement: Reading sack manager after less than nine months |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/46467268 |access-date=6 December 2018 |work=BBC Sport |date=6 December 2018}}</ref>

===Cercle Brugge===
Clement was appointed as the new head coach of Cercle Brugge on 3 July 2020 on a three-year contract.{{citation needed|date=July 2020}} He was sacked on 1 February 2021.

=== Everton ===
On 31 January 2022, Clement was appointed as a first team coach at [[Everton F.C.|Everton]] as part of [[Frank Lampard]]'s new backroom staff.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Correspondent|first=Paul Joyce, Northern Football|title=Frank Lampard signs two-and-a-half year deal to lead Everton rebuild|newspaper=[[The Times]] |language=en|url=https://www.thetimes.com/sport/football/article/frank-lampard-signs-two-and-a-half-year-deal-to-lead-everton-rebuild-5mj035xqz|access-date=2022-01-31|issn=0140-0460}}</ref> He left the club almost one year later on 23 January 2023, following Lampard's sacking as manager.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/63610629 | title=Lampard sacked as Everton manager | work=BBC Sport }}</ref>
