{{Short description|English football coach (born 1981)}}
'''Ben Dawson''' (born 11 February 1981)<ref>{{cite web |title=Ben Dawson |url=https://www.tntsports.co.uk/football/ben-dawson_prs712990/person.shtml |publisher=[[TNT Sports]] |access-date=1 December 2024}}</ref> is an English football coach. He held various positions at [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] from 2009 to 2024, before moving to [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] as a first-team coach from 2024 to 2025.

==Career==
Dawson joined [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]]'s academy in 2009. He left three years later for a job with [[the Football Association]], and returned to Newcastle in 2014 as assistant to [[Peter Beardsley]] in the [[Newcastle United F.C. Reserves and Academy|under-21 team]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ryder |first1=Lee |title=Long serving Newcastle United coach quits after approach from Premier League rival |url=https://www.chroniclelive.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/long-serving-newcastle-united-coach-29609686 |access-date=1 December 2024 |work=[[Evening Chronicle]]|date=25 July 2024}}</ref>

In January 2018, Beardsley was suspended due to an inquiry into accusations about his conduct towards players, and Dawson became manager of the team.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Taylor |first1=Louise |title=Sunderland meet Newcastle – but this is not the derby everyone wants |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/jan/08/sunderland-newcastle-not-derby-everyone-wants-checkatrade |access-date=1 December 2024 |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=8 January 2019}}</ref> His team topped their group in the [[EFL Trophy]] in [[2018–19 EFL Trophy|2018–19]], and met the senior side of [[Tyne–Wear derby|northeast rivals]] [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] in the third round on 8 January, losing 4–0 at the [[Stadium of Light]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Taylor |first1=Louise |title=Sunderland enjoy illusion of rivalry in 4-0 win over Newcastle Under-21s |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/blog/2019/jan/08/sunderland-newcastle-jack-ross-checkatrade-trophy |access-date=1 December 2024 |work=The Guardian |date=8 January 2019}}</ref> In the league, the under-21s reached the play-off final for promotion from the Second Division of [[Premier League 2]], but lost 2–1 away to [[Southampton F.C. Under-21s and Academy|Southampton]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Stanforth |first1=Miles |title=Newcastle United's Under-23s suffer another agonising final defeat |url=https://www.shieldsgazette.com/sport/newcastle-uniteds-under-23s-suffer-another-agonising-final-defeat-117540 |access-date=1 December 2024 |work=The Shields Gazette |date=13 May 2019}}</ref>

Dawson then returned to Newcastle's academy. In July 2019, he led the first team on a tour of China between the exit of [[Rafael Benítez]] and appointment of [[Steve Bruce]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Ben Dawson preparing Newcastle players for arrival of new boss in China |url=https://www.jerseyeveningpost.com/uksport/2019/07/16/ben-dawson-preparing-newcastle-players-for-arrival-of-new-boss-in-china/ |access-date=1 December 2024 |work=[[Jersey Evening Post]] |date=16 July 2019}}</ref> He formed part of the coaching staff of Bruce, interim manager [[Graeme Jones]],<ref>{{cite news |last1=Waugh |first1=Chris |title=Steve Bruce departs as Newcastle head coach |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/4195310/2021/10/10/steve-bruce-departs-as-newcastle-head-coach/ |access-date=1 December 2024 |work=[[The Athletic]] |date=10 October 2021}}</ref> and [[Eddie Howe]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Waugh |first1=Chris |title=Newcastle confirm Eddie Howe’s backroom staff |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/3688121/2021/11/16/newcastle-confirm-eddie-howes-backroom-staff/ |access-date=1 December 2024 |work=The Athletic |date=16 November 2021}}</ref>

In July 2024, Dawson moved to [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] as part of the staff of [[Steve Cooper (football manager)|Steve Cooper]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mashiter |first1=Nick |title=Cooper adds Newcastle's Dawson to coaching staff |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/cv2gzzz5y71o |access-date=1 December 2024 |publisher=[[BBC Sport]] |date=25 July 2024}}</ref> Cooper was sacked on 24 November and Dawson was appointed caretaker manager.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sanghera |first1=Mandeep |title=Leicester City sack manager Cooper after 12 league games |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/c8dmndv2n6zo |access-date=1 December 2024 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=24 November 2024}}</ref> He criticised the players for their conduct at their Christmas party in [[Copenhagen]] after Cooper's last match, and said they had been "dealt with".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mashiter |first1=Nick |title=Foxes' players told behaviour unacceptable - Dawson |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/cy9j7wwpzrdo |access-date=1 December 2024 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=29 November 2024}}</ref> On 30 November, in his one game before [[Ruud van Nistelrooy]] took over, Leicester lost 4–1 away to [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] in the [[Premier League]].<ref>{{cite news |title='A bit of a challenging afternoon' |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/c5yry4lxmz4o |access-date=1 December 2024 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=30 November 2024}}</ref> Dawson and Danny Alcock – who had joined Cooper's team at the same time – left on 24 February 2025.<ref>{{cite news |title=Dawson and Alcock leave Leicester City |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/articles/c5y4wkq7573o |access-date=24 February 2025 |publisher=[[BBC Sport]] |date=24 February 2025}}</ref>
