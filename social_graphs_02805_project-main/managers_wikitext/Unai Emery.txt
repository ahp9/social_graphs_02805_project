'''Unai Emery Etxegoien''' (born 3 November 1971) is a Spanish [[Association football|football]] [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and former player who is the head coach of [[Premier League]] club [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]]. He is widely regarded as one of the best managers in the world.<ref>{{Cite web |last=White |first=Mark |date=2024-05-06 |title=Ranked! After a career spent playing mostly in Spain's [[Segunda División]], Emery transitioned into coaching after retiring in 2004. He began at [[Lorca Deportiva CF|Lorca Deportiva]], where he achieved promotion to the Segunda División in his first season. He then joined [[UD Almería|Almería]], whom he led to promotion to [[La Liga]] for the first time in the club's history. He subsequently moved to [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], leading the team to top-three finishes. After his contract was not renewed at Valencia, he moved to [[FC Spartak Moscow|Spartak Moscow]] for six months but was sacked due to poor performance, before moving to [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] in 2013, where he won an unprecedented three consecutive [[UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]s.

Emery moved to French club [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]] in 2016. There, he won a [[2017–18 Ligue 1|Ligue 1]] title, two [[Coupe de France]] titles, two [[Coupe de la Ligue]]s, and two [[Trophée des Champions]], including a domestic [[List of association football teams to have won four or more trophies in one season|quadruple]] in his second season.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lemonde.fr/football/article/2017/05/28/football-vers-une-deuxieme-saison-au-psg-pour-l-entraineur-unai-emery_5135018_1616938.html |title=Football: vers une deuxième saison au PSG pour l'entraîneur Unai Emery?|trans-title=Football: second season in sight at PSG for coach Unai Emery? |newspaper=[[Le Monde]] |first=Rémi |last=Dupré |language=fr |date=28 May 2017 |access-date=29 May 2017}}</ref> After the expiry of his contract, Emery was appointed as head coach of English club [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] in 2018, succeeding [[Arsène Wenger]]. He finished [[2019 UEFA Europa League Final|Europa League]] runner-up in his first season, before being dismissed in November 2019. He was hired by [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]] in July 2020, where he won the [[2020–21 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] in his first season and guided the club to a [[2021–22 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] semi-final run in the following season. In October 2022, he returned to the Premier League to manage Aston Villa and guided them to a [[UEFA Conference League]] semi-final in 2024 and the Champions League quarter-finals a year later after successfully finishing in the top four of the Premier League.

==Playing career==
Emery, a [[Midfielder#Wide midfielder|left-sided midfielder]], was a youth graduate of [[Real Sociedad]], but never really broke into the first team (aged 24 he appeared in five [[La Liga]] games, scoring against [[Albacete Balompié|Albacete]] in an 8–1 home win).<ref>{{cite news |url=http://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1996/05/13/pagina-21/1328870/pdf.html |title=La Real se desmelena|trans-title=Real go crazy |newspaper=[[Mundo Deportivo]] |language=es |date=13 May 1996 |access-date=25 April 2013}}</ref> After that, he resumed his career mostly in the [[Segunda División]], amassing totals of 215 matches and nine goals over seven seasons. He retired with [[Lorca Deportiva CF|Lorca Deportiva]] at the age of 32, after [[2003–04 Segunda División B|one season]] in [[Segunda División B]]. In 2002, he signed for [[Burgos CF|Burgos]] but was one of several players released before making a single competitive appearance due to lack of funds.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Izquierdo |first1=David |title=El descenso a 2ªB del Burgos salpica a la Real|trans-title=Burgos' descent to 2ªB has a knock-on effect on Real Sociedad|url=http://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/2002/07/27/pagina-7/500525/pdf.html#&mode=fullScreen |access-date=22 June 2022 |work=[[Mundo Deportivo]] |date=27 July 2002 |language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Managerial career==
===Lorca and Almería===
[[File:Unai Emery.JPG|thumb|right|Emery with [[UD Almería|Almería]]]]

Emery suffered a serious knee injury while at Lorca in the [[2004–05 Segunda División B|2004–05 season]], and he was offered the vacant coach's position by the club president. He immediately helped the club achieve promotion to the second division for the first time in its history,<ref name="SAGA">{{cite news |url=http://elpais.com/diario/2005/06/27/deportes/1119823209_850215.html |title=Emery asciende al Lorca contra la saga familiar|trans-title=Emery promotes Lorca against family saga |newspaper=El País |language=es |date=27 June 2005 |access-date=10 May 2015|last1=Rodrigálvarez|first1=Eduardo}}</ref> as well as beating top-level side [[Málaga CF|Málaga]] in the [[2004–05 Copa del Rey|Copa del Rey]]. He was subsequently awarded the [[Miguel Muñoz Trophy]] as coach of the season. In [[2005–06 Segunda División|his second season]], the [[Region of Murcia|Murcians]]' first ever in the second division, the team finished fifth with 69 points, only five points off promotion to the top flight;<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.diariovasco.com/pg060618/prensa/noticias/Deportes/200606/18/DVA-SUB-346.html |title=Un éxito del Lorca de Emery|trans-title=Success of Emery's Lorca |newspaper=[[El Diario Vasco]] |language=es |date=18 June 2006 |access-date=10 May 2015}}</ref> they suffered relegation [[2006–07 Segunda División|in 2007]], after Emery's departure.

Emery then moved to [[UD Almería|Almería]] in division two,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://hemeroteca-paginas.mundodeportivo.com/EMD02/PUB/2006/06/22/EMD20060622039MDP.pdf |title=Unai Emery, ya en Almería|trans-title=Unai Emery, in Almería as of now |newspaper=Mundo Deportivo |language=es |date=22 June 2006 |access-date=11 May 2015}}</ref> and again helped his squad overachieve: after guiding them to a [[2006–07 Segunda División|first ever promotion in 2007]],<ref>{{cite news |url=http://elpais.com/diario/2007/05/20/deportes/1179612009_850215.html |title=Almería, 27 años después|trans-title=Almería, 27 years later |newspaper=El País |language=es |date=20 May 2007 |access-date=11 May 2015|last1=Díaz|first1=María José López}}</ref> the [[Andalusia]]n side finished eighth in La Liga in [[2007–08 La Liga|2007–08]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2008/05/19/mas_futbol/1211178452_850215.html |title=La juerga del Almería agrava la 'depre' perica|trans-title=Almería's party worsens parakeet 'depre' |newspaper=[[Diario AS]] |language=es |date=19 May 2008 |access-date=11 May 2015}}</ref> This prompted a move to [[Valencia CF|Valencia]], where he succeeded [[Ronald Koeman]] as coach.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20111005154355/http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=700064.html Emery to take on Valencia challenge]; UEFA.com, 22 May 2008</ref>

===Valencia===
In [[2008–09 La Liga|2008–09]], his first season with ''Los Che'', Emery led them to a sixth-place finish, with subsequent qualification to the [[UEFA Europa League]], in spite of the club's serious financial problems. The team reached the Round of 32 in the [[2008–09 UEFA Cup|UEFA Cup]], losing on [[away goals rule|away goals]] after a 3–3 aggregate draw against [[FC Dynamo Kyiv|Dynamo Kyiv]], and the quarter-finals of the [[2008–09 Copa del Rey|Copa del Rey]], exiting against [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]].

In [[2009–10 La Liga|2009–10]], Emery led Valencia to third place, so the club returned to the [[UEFA Champions League]] after two years. After dropping down to [[2009-10 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]] (previously known as UEFA Cup), they lost in the quarter-finals to eventual winners [[Atlético Madrid]] on [[away goals rule|away goals]], and exited in the round of 16 in the [[2009–10 Copa del Rey|Copa del Rey]] against [[Deportivo de La Coruña|Deportivo La Coruña]], losing 4–3 on aggregate. In May 2010, Emery renewed his contract for another year.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rtve.es/deportes/20100513/unai-emery-renueva-su-contrato-valencia/331186.shtml |title=Unai Emery renueva su contrato con el Valencia|trans-title=Unai Emery renews his contract with Valencia |publisher=[[RTVE]] |language=es |date=13 May 2010 |access-date=11 May 2015}}</ref>

[[2010–11 La Liga|2010–11]] started without [[David Villa]] and [[David Silva]], sold to [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] and [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] respectively. Despite this, Valencia won five out of the first six league games (with one draw), starting the [[2010–11 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] campaign with a 4–0 win in Turkey against [[Bursaspor]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.as.com/futbol/articulo/valencia-sale-lider-infierno-turco/20100914dasdasftb_41/Tes |title=El Valencia sale líder del infierno turco|trans-title=Valencia emerge leader from Turkish hell |newspaper=Diario AS |language=es |date=14 September 2010 |access-date=8 March 2017}}</ref> before being eliminated in the round of 16 by [[FC Schalke 04|Schalke 04]], and being knocked out in the same stage in the [[2010–11 Copa del Rey|Copa del Rey]] by [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]]. In the domestic league, the side finished third, thus again qualifying for the Champions League.

In [[2011–12 Valencia CF season|2011–12]], Emery's side entered the [[2011-12 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] and were paired with [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]], [[Bayer Leverkusen]] and [[KRC Genk]]. They finished third in their group and subsequently entered the [[2011-12 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]. Valencia lost in the semi-finals to eventual winners [[Atlético Madrid]] again. He left the club in June 2012, after again finishing third in [[2011–12 La Liga|2011–12]], ensuring Valencia's qualification for [[2012–13 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://hemeroteca-paginas.mundodeportivo.com/EMD03/PUB/2012/05/06/EMD20120506025MDP.pdf |title=El Valencia se asegura la tercera posición|trans-title=Valencia confirm third place |newspaper=Mundo Deportivo |language=es |date=6 May 2012 |access-date=11 May 2015}}</ref>

===Spartak Moscow===
On 13 May 2012, [[Leonid Fedun]], owner of [[FC Spartak Moscow|Spartak Moscow]], announced Emery as the Russian club's coach for the following two seasons.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/clubfootball/news/newsid=1630656.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120516234605/http://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/clubfootball/news/newsid=1630656.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=16 May 2012 |title=Spartak name Emery new coach |publisher=FIFA.com |date=14 May 2012 |access-date=14 August 2012}}</ref> On 25 November, he was sacked after a run of poor results, after only managing the side for six months. His last game in charge was a 5–1 home loss in the [[Oldest Russian derby|derby]] against [[FC Dynamo Moscow|Dynamo Moscow]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://int.soccerway.com/news/2012/November/25/spartak-dismiss-emery-after-derby-defeat/ |title=Spartak dismiss Emery after derby defeat |publisher=Soccerway |date=25 November 2012 |access-date=25 November 2012}}</ref>

===Sevilla===
[[File:Zenit-Sevilla (6).jpg|thumb|upright=0.8|Emery managing [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]] in 2015]]

Emery returned to Spanish football on 14 January 2013, replacing the sacked [[Míchel (footballer, born 1963)|Míchel]] at the helm of Sevilla.<ref>[http://www.sevillafc.es/nuevaweb/actualidad/noticias/27616/michel-es-destituido-y-deja-paso-a-emery-que-firma-hasta-junio-de-2014 Míchel es destituido y deja paso a Emery, que firma hasta Junio de 2014 (Míchel is sacked and makes way for Emery, who signs until June 2014)] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130410033837/http://www.sevillafc.es/nuevaweb/actualidad/noticias/27616/michel-es-destituido-y-deja-paso-a-emery-que-firma-hasta-junio-de-2014 |date=10 April 2013 }}; Sevilla FC, 14 January 2013 (in Spanish)</ref> He led the club to fifth position in [[2013–14 La Liga|2013–14]], his first full season. On 14 May 2014, he won the [[2014 UEFA Europa League Final|Europa League final]], defeating [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] on [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalties]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/27375727 |title=Sevilla 0–0 Benfica |website=BBC Sport |date=14 May 2014 |access-date=15 May 2014}}</ref>

After finishing the [[2014–15 La Liga|2014–15 season]] in fifth place, one point behind former club Valencia, Emery again [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League|won the Europa League]] after defeating [[FC Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk|Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk]],<ref name="uel2015">{{cite news |last1=Rose |first1=Gary |title=Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk 2–3 Sevilla |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32908634 |website=BBC Sport |date=27 May 2015 |access-date=25 August 2020 }}</ref> thus qualifying for the [[2015–16 UEFA Champions League|following Champions League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/0221-0e90e71238fc-6fd33fb9f42a-1000--spain-to-have-five-clubs-in-champions-league/ |title=Spain to have five clubs in Champions League |publisher=[[UEFA]] |date=27 May 2015 |access-date=27 May 2015}}</ref> The victory meant that Sevilla became the most successful club in the history of the UEFA Cup/Europa League, with four wins,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/0221-0e90e477a0d6-7757fcd8b605-1000--sevilla-out-on-their-own-in-all-time-standings/ |title=Sevilla out on their own in all-time standings |publisher=[[UEFA]] |date=27 May 2015 |access-date=5 June 2015}}</ref> and Emery signed a one-year contract extension on 5 June 2015 which would have kept him at the club until 2017, after reported interest from [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] and [[S.S.C. Napoli|Napoli]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allsportsnews.co.uk/news/premier-league/28539/unai-emery-rejects-west-ham-to-stay-at-sevilla |title=Unai Emery rejects West Ham to stay at Sevilla |publisher=All Sports News |date=June 2015 |access-date=5 June 2015}}</ref>

In [[2015–16 La Liga|2015–16]], Emery led Sevilla to seventh position, having played the final games of the domestic league with several reserves and youth team players after the team confirmed their place in the [[2016 UEFA Europa League Final|Europa League final]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.europapress.es/deportes/futbol-00162/noticia-emery-estamos-ilusion-intacta-tres-competiciones-no-hemos-conseguido-nada-20160430164044.html |title=Emery: "Estamos con ilusión intacta en tres competiciones pero no hemos conseguido nada"|trans-title=Emery: "We are as hungry as can be in three competitions but we have not achieved anything" |publisher=[[Europa Press (news agency)|Europa Press]] |language=es |date=30 April 2016 |access-date=18 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2016/05/13/primera/1463142736_415282.html |title=Emery cita a 19 jugadores para Bilbao con cinco del filial|trans-title=Emery summons 19 players to Bilbao with five from the reserves |newspaper=Diario AS |language=es |date=13 May 2016 |access-date=18 May 2016}}</ref> This season, Sevilla entered the [[UEFA Champions League]] group stage as title holders of [[UEFA Europa League]] the previous season, they were third in the group and transferred to [[UEFA Europa League]] again. ===Villarreal===
[[2020-21 Villarreal season|On 23 July 2020]], Emery was announced as the new head coach of La Liga club [[Villarreal CF|Villarreal]], succeeding [[Javier Calleja]] on a three-year deal.<ref>{{cite news |title=Unai Emery: Villarreal appoint former Arsenal manager |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53513821 |access-date=23 July 2020 |website=BBC Sport |date=23 July 2020}}</ref> On 23 December, he broke the club record of 18 matches unbeaten, after a 1–1 draw at home to [[Athletic Bilbao]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.football-espana.net/2020/12/23/unai-emery-breaks-unbeaten-club-record-at-villarreal|title=Unai Emery Breaks Unbeaten Club Record at Villarreal|publisher=Football España|date=23 December 2020}}</ref>

Villa to their highest Premier League points total after 19 games, the season's midpoint.<ref>{{cite web |title=Match Pack: Brighton & Hove Albion |url=
