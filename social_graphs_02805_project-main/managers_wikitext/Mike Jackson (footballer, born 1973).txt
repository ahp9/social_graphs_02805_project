'''Michael James Jackson''' (born 4 December 1973) is an English [[Association football|football]] manager and former professional footballer. He is a first team coach for [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]] and has served as [[caretaker manager]] of the club in the past.

As a player, Jackson was a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] who played in the [[Football League]] for [[Crewe Alexandra F.C.|Crewe Alexandra]], [[Bury F.C.|Bury]], [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]], [[Tranmere Rovers F.C.|Tranmere Rovers]], [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]] and [[Shrewsbury Town F.C.|Shrewsbury Town]]. He moved into management in 2014 and has been in charge of both Tranmere Rovers and Shrewsbury Town.

==Playing career==
Jackson started his career as a trainee at [[Crewe Alexandra F.C.|Crewe Alexandra]] in 1992. He signed for [[Bury F.C.|Bury]] in August 1993 on a free transfer where he stayed until 1997 making 125 league appearances and scoring nine goals. In March 1997 he signed for [[Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End]] for a fee of £125,000. In seven years at the club, he made 251 league appearances and scored 17 goals. While at Preston, he had a one-month loan spell at [[Tranmere Rovers F.C.|Tranmere Rovers]] from December 2002 to January 2003, having been frozen out of the team at [[Deepdale]] by manager [[Craig Brown (footballer, born 1940)|Craig Brown]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Jackson determined to make Prenton hit |url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/jackson-determined-make-prenton-hit-3556178 |access-date=18 April 2022 |work=[[Liverpool Echo]] |date=19 December 2002}}</ref> In May 2004, he signed for Tranmere on a free transfer,<ref>{{cite news |title=Little thrilled as Rovers snap up Jackson |url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/little-thrilled-rovers-snap-up-3544545 |access-date=18 April 2022 |work=Liverpool Echo |date=26 May 2004}}</ref> making 84 appearances in two years at the club.

In June 2006, Jackson joined [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]] on a free transfer.<ref name="sb3855">{{Cite web | title = Profile – Michael Jackson | work = [[Racing Post|Soccerbase]] | url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid-3855 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081026212705/http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid-3855 | url-status=dead | archive-date=26 October 2008 | access-date = 23 June 2008}}</ref> He scored his first goal for Blackpool on 19 August 2006 in a 4–2 league win at [[Bristol City F.C.|Bristol City]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_2/4786321.stm|title=Bristol City 2–4 Blackpool |publisher=[[BBC]]|date=19 August 2006 |access-date=8 May 2013}}</ref> He became an instrumental part of the Blackpool team and captained the side in their promotion to the English Championship in May 2007. Jackson picked up an injury in February 2008 which kept him out for two months before he made a late-season comeback.<ref name="BBC7459857">{{Cite news | title = Shrews close in on Jackson deal | publisher = [[BBC Sport]] | date = 23 June 2008 | url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/b/blackpool/7469630.stm | access-date = 23 June 2008 }}</ref> On 7 May 2008, however, he was released by Blackpool.<ref name="BFC1145801">{{Cite news|title=Retained List Latest |publisher=[[Blackpool F.C.]] |date=7 May 2008 |url=http://www.blackpoolfc.premiumtv.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10432~1307664,00.html |access-date=7 May 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080725090113/http://www.blackpoolfc.premiumtv.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0%2C%2C10432~1307664%2C00.html |archive-date=25 July 2008 }}</ref> After his goal against Bristol City, he scored further goals against [[Norwich City]] in the [[FA Cup]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/fa_cup/6349599.stm|title=Norwich 3–2 Blackpool (aet) |publisher=[[BBC]] |date=13 February 2007 |access-date=8 May 2013}}</ref> and [[Southend United]] in the [[Football League Cup|League Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/league_cup/7008008.stm|title=Blackpool 2–1 Southend (aet) |publisher=[[BBC]] |date=25 September 2007 |access-date=8 May 2013}}</ref>

On 23 June 2008, it was reported that Jackson was about to sign a two-year contract with [[Shrewsbury Town F.C.|Shrewsbury Town]].<ref name="BBC7459857"/><ref name="Gazette4213026">{{Cite news | title =  Shrewsbury Town sign Pool skipper Jackson | work = [[Blackpool Gazette]] | date = 23 June 2008 | url = http://www.blackpoolgazette.co.uk/sports-news/Shrewsbury-Town-sign-Pool-skipper.3469485.jp | access-date = 23 June 2008 }}</ref> The transfer was completed the following day, along with the club record transfer for [[Grant Holt]].<ref>{{Cite news |  url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/s/shrewsbury/7469127.stm | title=Shrews break club record for Holt | publisher=BBC Sport | date=24 June 2008}}</ref> Jackson was a regular in the Shrewsbury side, notably scoring in a 7–0 win over [[Gillingham F.C.|Gillingham]],<ref>{{Cite news | title = Shrewsbury 7–0 Gillingham | publisher = [[BBC Sport]] | date = 13 September 2008 | url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_3/7602072.stm | access-date = 19 June 2014 }}</ref> but did not play again after a 3–1 defeat away at [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] on 21 February 2009.<ref>{{Soccerbase season|3855|2008|access-date=19 June 2014}}</ref>

It was announced on 13 January 2010 that Jackson had retired from playing due to a persistent knee injury, as a specialist advised him not to risk his long-term health by continuing, but he was to remain at the club until the end of the 2009–10 season in a coaching capacity.<ref>{{Cite news | title = Injuries force Jackson to retire | publisher = [[BBC Sport]] | date = 13 January 2010 | url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/teams/s/shrewsbury/8456457.stm | access-date = 16 January 2010 }}</ref>

==Coaching and managerial career==
Jackson continued in his coaching role at Shrewsbury, and was named as [[caretaker manager]] for the final match of the 2009–10 season following the dismissal of [[Paul Simpson (footballer)|Paul Simpson]].<ref name="news.bbc.co.uk">{{cite web|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/eng_div_3/8632890.stm|title=Football – Port Vale 1–1 Shrewsbury|publisher=BBC Sport |date=8 May 2010 |access-date=22 January 2014}}</ref> He resumed his coaching role under new manager [[Graham Turner]], and was appointed as caretaker manager again following Turner's resignation on 22 January 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/25843211|title=Shrewsbury Town: Michael Jackson to take caretaker charge|publisher=BBC Sport |date=22 January 2014 |access-date=22 January 2014}}</ref> A month later, he was given the managerial position on a contract running until the end of the [[2013–14 Shrewsbury Town F.C. season|2013–14]] season,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/26288800|title=Shrewsbury Town: Mike Jackson named manager for rest of season|publisher=BBC Sport |date=21 February 2014 |access-date=23 February 2014}}</ref> but was unable to save the team from relegation to [[EFL League Two|League Two]]. Despite this, the club opted to retain his services as assistant to new manager [[Micky Mellon]], alongside goalkeeping coach [[Danny Coyne]].<ref name="BBC Sport">{{cite web 
|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/27375687|title=Shrewsbury Town: Micky Mellon appointed as new manager|publisher=BBC Sport |date=12 May 2014 |access-date=12 May 2014}}</ref>

After a sequence of only two wins in the opening eleven matches of the [[2016–17 Shrewsbury Town F.C. season|2016–17]] season, Jackson left the club by mutual consent in October 2016 along with Mellon,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shrewsburytown.com/news/article/2016-17/shrewsbury-town-micky-mellon-3350459.aspx|title=Micky Mellon leaves|publisher=Shrewsweb|date=6 October 2016|access-date=6 October 2016}}</ref> before once again being appointed as his assistant again at Tranmere shortly afterwards.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37667236|title=Michael Jackson: Tranmere Rovers appoint new assistant manager|publisher=BBC Sport|date=15 October 2016|access-date=16 October 2016}}</ref>

Following Mellon's departure to [[Dundee United F.C.|Dundee United]], Jackson was named manager of Tranmere Rovers on 18 July 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mike Jackson appointed first team manager|url=https://www.tranmererovers.co.uk/news/2020/july/mike-jackson-appointed-first-team-manager/|access-date=18 July 2020|website=Tranmere Rovers official website}}</ref> He was sacked on 31 October 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Club Statement|url=https://www.tranmererovers.co.uk/news/2020/october/club-statement2/|access-date=31 October 2020|website=Tranmere Rovers official website}}</ref>

In July 2021, Jackson was appointed interim manager of the [[Burnley F.C. Academy|Burnley Under-23s team]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-09|title=Burnley hand role to former Tranmere Rovers boss Mike Jackson|url=https://the72.co.uk/241744/burnley-hand-role-to-former-tranmere-rovers-boss-mike-jackson/|access-date=2021-08-23|website=The 72}}</ref> On 15 April 2022, after [[Sean Dyche]] was dismissed, [[Burnley F.C.|Burnley]] appointed Jackson as caretaker manager for their [[Road (sports)|away]] match against [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Burnley sack two days later.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sims |first1=Andy |title=Burnley interim boss Mike Jackson on Ashley Westwood injury and West Ham United draw
